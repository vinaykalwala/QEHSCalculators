📘 About This Calculator

This calculator helps determine the Discharge Rate (W) of CO₂ required for fire suppression in a protected area. It takes the Final Design Quantity (mFD) — which represents the total amount of CO₂ needed for effective fire suppression — and the Discharge Time (t) — the duration over which the CO₂ will be released — to calculate how fast the gas should be discharged.

Understanding the discharge rate is essential for designing and operating CO₂ fire suppression systems. If the gas is released too slowly, it may not suppress the fire effectively. If it is released too quickly, it could pose safety risks to personnel and damage equipment.

This tool is intended for fire safety engineers, facility managers, and designers to ensure that CO₂ systems are sized correctly, work efficiently, and comply with fire safety standards. By entering the required inputs, users can quickly assess whether the system will deliver CO₂ at an appropriate rate for the protected space.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Final Design Quantity (mFD): The total amount of CO₂ required (kg).
  • Discharge Time (t): The time over which the CO₂ is discharged (seconds).

📌 Input Validation Rules

  • ✅ All fields are mandatory: Both Final Design Quantity (mFD) and Discharge Time (t) must be filled.
  • ✅ Numeric values only: Letters, special characters, or empty input are invalid.
  • ✅ Positive values required: Zero or negative values are not allowed.
  • ✅ Final Design Quantity (mFD): Must be between 50 kg and 50,000 kg. Values outside this range trigger warnings.
  • ✅ Discharge Time (t): Must be between 5 and 300 seconds, as per NFPA/ISO total flooding CO₂ guidelines.
  • ✅ Decimal precision: mFD max 4 decimals; t max 2 decimals to ensure realistic reporting.
  • ⚠️ Discharge Rate : Outputs below 1 kg/s or above 500 kg/s trigger warnings for review.
  • ⚠️ Edge cases: Division by zero, extremely low/high inputs, or unrealistic combinations will display error messages and hide the placeholder.

📊 Output Validations

  • If Discharge Rate < 1 kg/s → ⚠️ Very low discharge rate. Check system sizing and inputs.
  • If Discharge Rate > 500 kg/s → ⚠️ Very high discharge rate. Verify practicality and safety.
  • If Discharge Rate 1–500 kg/s → ✅ Discharge rate calculated successfully and within typical range. System is functioning as expected.

📐 Governing Principle

Discharge Rate for CO₂ (flooding) → NFPA 12 (Standard): This standard specifies the minimum discharge rates and times for CO₂ flooding systems. It ensures that CO₂ is released at a rate sufficient to suppress fires effectively in protected spaces while maintaining safety for personnel and equipment.

By following these guidelines, designers and engineers can size CO₂ systems appropriately, ensuring that the protected area receives the necessary concentration of CO₂ within the required discharge time.

💡 Conclusion

This CO₂ Discharge Rate Calculator provides a quick and reliable method to determine the discharge rate (W = mFD / t) for fire suppression systems. By entering the Final Design Quantity (mFD) and Discharge Time (t), users can ensure that the CO₂ is released at a rate that is both effective for fire suppression and safe for personnel and equipment. The calculator also incorporates input validation rules and feedback to maintain compliance with NFPA, ISO, and OSHA fire safety standards.
