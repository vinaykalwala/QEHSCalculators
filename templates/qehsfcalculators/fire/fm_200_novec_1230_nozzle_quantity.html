📘 About This Calculator

This calculator determines the required number of FM-200 or NOVEC 1230 nozzles based on the total hazard volume and the coverage capacity of each nozzle. Proper nozzle placement is essential to ensure even distribution of the suppression agent.

⚙️ Understanding the Parameters

  • Net Hazard Volume (V): The total volume of the protected space where fire suppression is required (measured in cubic meters).
  • Nozzle Coverage Area (N): The volume that a single nozzle can effectively cover, provided by the manufacturer (measured in cubic meters per nozzle).

🔹 What is a Nozzle?

A nozzle is a discharge device that releases FM-200 or NOVEC 1230 into the protected area. Nozzles ensure even distribution of the agent, helping to quickly suppress a fire.

❗ Why are Nozzles Important?

  • Controlled Discharge: Regulates the flow and velocity of the agent.
  • Even Coverage: Ensures the entire area receives proper protection.
  • Compliance: Follows NFPA 2001 and ISO 14520 fire safety standards.

📍 Where is this used?

This calculation is used in designing fire suppression systems for server rooms, data centers, control rooms, industrial spaces, and other enclosed areas where FM-200 or NOVEC 1230 systems are installed. Nozzles must be properly spaced to ensure the agent reaches all areas efficiently.

✅ Input Validation Rules

To ensure accuracy, compliance, and safety, all inputs in this calculator must follow NFPA 2001 and ISO 14520 guidelines, as well as manufacturer data.

  • Net Hazard Volume (V): Must be between 1 m³ and 100,000 m³. Values outside this range are not compliant with NFPA system design limits.
  • Nozzle Coverage (N): Must be between 1 m³ and 5,000 m³ per nozzle. These values are based on manufacturer specifications and system testing standards.
  • Numeric & Positive Only: Inputs must be positive numbers, with up to 1 decimal place for precision. Letters or negative values are invalid.
  • Logical Consistency: Nozzle coverage should be proportionate to room volume. For example, a 100 m³ room cannot realistically use a 5,000 m³ nozzle.
  • Cross-Check: If the result exceeds 100 nozzles, the design must be sectionalized for compliance and practicality.

These rules prevent unsafe designs, ensure regulatory compliance, and maintain cost-effective fire suppression solutions.

❓ Why do we need this calculation?

  • Ensures Proper Agent Distribution: Determines the optimal number of nozzles required.
  • Regulatory Compliance: Meets NFPA 2001 and ISO 14520 fire safety standards.
  • Prevents Dead Zones: Ensures no areas are left unprotected.
  • Optimizes Costs: Avoids unnecessary overuse of nozzles, reducing installation costs.

📊 What does the result mean?

The result indicates the total number of nozzles required to ensure proper distribution of the suppression agent. Larger volumes or lower-coverage nozzles require more nozzles, while higher-coverage nozzles reduce the count.
