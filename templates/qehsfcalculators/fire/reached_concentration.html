The Reached Concentration Calculator is designed to estimate the concentration of an extinguishing agent within an enclosed space after deployment. Fire suppression systems, such as CO‚ÇÇ, FM-200, or inert gas systems, rely on achieving a precise concentration of the agent to effectively suppress fires without harming personnel or equipment.

By calculating the reached concentration, engineers and safety managers can:

  • Assess whether the amount of extinguishing agent deployed is sufficient to suppress a fire in the protected space.
  • Ensure compliance with fire safety standards, regulations, and design codes (NFPA, ISO 14520, EN 15004).
  • Optimize the quantity of agent used, reducing unnecessary chemical usage and cost while maintaining safety.
  • Evaluate the potential risk to personnel from excessive agent concentrations, ensuring safe re-entry after discharge.

The calculator considers the Agent Mass (W), the Specific Volume (SV) of the agent, and the Volume of Space (VW) to determine the effective concentration reached after deployment. The result is expressed as a percentage, indicating how much of the protected space is occupied by the extinguishing agent.

Proper use of this calculator supports fire safety engineers in designing, validating, and optimizing fire suppression systems. It also helps facilities comply with safety regulations, maintain operational efficiency, and reduce fire-related risks to life and property.
