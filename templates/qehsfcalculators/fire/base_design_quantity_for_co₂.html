📘 About This Calculator

This calculator determines the Base Design Quantity (mBD) of CO₂ required for fire suppression in a given space. The base design quantity is a critical parameter in designing fire suppression systems, ensuring that the correct amount of CO₂ is used to effectively suppress fires.

Fire suppression systems rely on achieving the correct quantity of CO₂ to effectively suppress fires. Too little CO₂ may be ineffective, while excessive CO₂ could pose risks to personnel and equipment.

⚙️ Parameters

  • Net Hazard Volume (Vnet): The total volume of the hazard area (m³) that must be protected.
  • Flooding Factor (FF): A coefficient provided in standards (e.g., NFPA 12), dependent on hazard type and space.

💡 Why This Matters

Properly calculating the base design quantity ensures:

  • ✅ Compliance with fire safety standards and regulations.
  • 💰 Optimal use of CO₂, reducing waste and costs.
  • 🔥 Effective fire suppression without unnecessary risks to life or equipment.

🛡️ Input Validations

  • ✅ All fields are mandatory and must be completed before calculation.
  • ✅ Numeric values only: Non-numeric entries are invalid.
  • ✅ Positive values required: Zero or negative numbers are not allowed.
  • ✅ Net Hazard Volume (Vnet): Must be between 1 m³ and 100,000 m³. Values outside this range will trigger warnings or errors.
  • ✅ Flooding Factor (FF): Must be between 0.3 and 1.2, in line with NFPA/ISO design standards. Typical values are 0.5–1.0 for gaseous agents.
  • ✅ Result validation: Very low CO₂ (<100 kg) or very high (>20,000 kg) outputs will trigger warnings for review.
  • ⚠️ Any error or warning will be displayed in the result box (default image will disappear).
