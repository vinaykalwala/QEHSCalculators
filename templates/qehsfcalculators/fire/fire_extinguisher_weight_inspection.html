📘 About This Calculator

Fire extinguishers lose weight over time due to leakage or discharge. Regular inspections help ensure readiness. This calculator estimates the percentage weight loss compared to the initial weight.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Initial Weight (kg): The fire extinguisher's weight before loss.
  • Current Weight (kg): The extinguisher's weight after loss.

📌 Input Validation Rules

  • Mandatory Fields: Both Initial and Current Weight must be entered.
  • Positive Numbers Only: Zero or negative values are not allowed.
  • Range Check:
    • Initial Weight should typically be between 1–50 kg (portable & trolley units).
    • Current Weight must be ≥ 0 and ≤ Initial Weight.
  • Decimal Precision: Maximum 2 decimal places (kg readings).
  • Logical Consistency: Current weight cannot exceed Initial weight.
  • Extreme Loss Check: If Weight Loss >30%, system will warn for data entry error.
  • Compliance Thresholds:
    • ✅ <5% Loss → Acceptable (Serviceable)
    • ⚠️ 5–10% Loss → Borderline (Service soon)
    • 🚨 >10% Loss → Non-compliant (Refill/Replace)

📊 Output Validations

Based on the calculated Weight Loss %, the following thresholds indicate extinguisher serviceability and compliance:

  • ✅ If weight loss < 5% → Acceptable: Extinguisher is serviceable.
  • ⚠️ If weight loss is 5–10% → Borderline: Schedule servicing soon.
  • 🚨 If weight loss > 10% → Non-compliant: Refill or replace immediately.

📐 Governing Principle

Fire extinguisher weight inspection standards depend on the location and applicable regulations, with key standards including the NFPA 10 (National Fire Protection Association) in the United States and IS 15683 (Bureau of Indian Standards) in India. These standards mandate routine visual inspections to ensure the extinguisher is in its proper place, undamaged, and has not been tampered with, often including a weight check to confirm it contains the correct extinguishing agent and is operational.

🌐 International Standards
  • NFPA 10 (National Fire Protection Association): This is a widely recognized standard in the United States that outlines requirements for portable fire extinguishers, including inspection, maintenance, and recharging procedures. NFPA 10 specifies that visual inspections, which include a weight check, should occur at least monthly to ensure the extinguisher is ready for use.
🇮🇳 Indian Standards
  • IS 15683 (Bureau of Indian Standards): This standard governs the performance and construction of portable fire extinguishers in India. It requires a product manual that details the performance and construction criteria, and BIS certification is necessary for portable fire extinguishers.
⚖️ Weight Inspection Purpose
  • Ensuring Full Charge: A weight check confirms the extinguisher is fully charged and contains the appropriate amount of extinguishing agent.
  • Identifying Tampering: Weight variations can indicate that an extinguisher has been partially or fully discharged and needs recharging or has been tampered with.
  • Maintaining Operational Readiness: Weight inspection is a quick and effective way to ensure the extinguisher is ready for immediate use in case of a fire.
📘 Where to Find Specifics
  • NFPA 10: For the United States and other regions that adopt this standard, check the latest edition of NFPA 10.
  • IS 15683: In India, refer to the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) and its associated product manuals for details on fire extinguisher requirements.
