üìê Governing Principle

The Fire Safety Occupancy Calculator for assembly and recreation limits is based on the National Building Code of India (NBC), specifically Part IV, Fire Protection, which outlines occupant load factors for different occupancy groups, including assembly. These factors, found in tables within the NBC, dictate the required floor area per person to calculate the maximum occupant load for a space.

Standard:

Here's how it works:
  • Identify the Occupancy Group: Determine if the building or area is classified as an "Assembly" occupancy.
  • Find the NBC Part IV: Refer to the specific tables in the National Building Code of India, Part IV (Fire Protection).
  • Locate the Occupant Load Factor: Look for the table that lists the occupant load (square meters per person) for assembly buildings.
  • Calculate the Occupant Load: Divide the total area of the room or floor by the prescribed occupant load factor to find the maximum number of people allowed.
  • Example: If a room is an assembly area and the NBC specifies an occupant load of 1 person per 0.5 square meters, then a 100-square-meter room can hold a maximum of 200 people.

Therefore, for a fire safety occupancy calculator related to Assembly and Recreation in India, you would use the occupant load factors provided in the National Building Code of India, Part IV.
