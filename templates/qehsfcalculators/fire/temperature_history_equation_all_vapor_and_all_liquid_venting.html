⚙️ Parameters Explained

  • Tr (K) – Initial Temperature: The starting temperature before any heat is applied.
  • q (J/kg) – Heat Release Rate: Amount of energy released or absorbed per kilogram of substance.
  • Cv (J/kg·K) – Specific Heat: Heat required to increase 1 kg of substance by 1 K at constant volume.
  • t (s) – Time Elapsed: Duration of heat application; must be less than emptying time (tr).
  • hfa (J/kg) – Latent Heat of Vaporization: Energy required to convert liquid to vapor without temperature change.
  • vfa (m³/kg) – Specific Volume of Liquid: Volume occupied by 1 kg of liquid.
  • tr (s) – Emptying Time: Time required for the system to be emptied or evacuated.

🔥 Real-life Applications

  • Fire safety engineering – predicting temperature rise in pressure vessels or enclosed spaces exposed to heat.
  • Chemical and process industries – assessing thermal hazards during reactions or heat transfer processes.
  • Explosion risk analysis – estimating conditions under which phase change or rapid heating may lead to overpressure.
  • Safety equipment design – ensuring materials can withstand predicted temperature rises without failure.
  • Environmental safety – predicting heat stress in confined areas or storage tanks during thermal events.

💡 Interpretation

  • ✅ Low Temperature Rise: Temperature increase is minimal; the system remains within safe operational limits. Normal operations can continue with routine monitoring.
  • ⚠️ Moderate Temperature Rise: Temperature is elevated; monitor the system closely. Consider implementing ventilation, cooling, or temporary operational adjustments to prevent overheating.
  • 🚨 High Temperature Rise: Temperature increase is significant and potentially hazardous. Immediate actions required: consider system shutdown, personnel evacuation, and activation of heat mitigation measures. Ensure compliance with fire safety and industrial standards (NFPA, OSHA, ISO 45001).

🛡️ Input Validations

  • All fields are mandatory — leaving any field empty will trigger an error.
  • Only positive numeric values are allowed; zero or negative values are invalid.
  • Elapsed time (t) must be greater than 0 and less than emptying time (tr) — ensures logical consistency for the ln(1 - t/tr) calculation.
  • Values outside typical industrial ranges will trigger a warning (orange) but will not block the calculation. Typical thresholds for reference:
    • Initial Temperature (Tr): ≤ 1500 K
    • Heat Release Rate (q): ≤ 10,000,000 J/kg
    • Specific Heat (Cv): ≤ 5000 J/kg·K
    • Latent Heat of Vaporization (hfa): ≤ 3,000,000 J/kg
    • Specific Volume (vfa): ≤ 0.5 m³/kg
    • Emptying Time (tr): ≤ 100,000 s
  • Calculation errors (e.g., ln(1 - t/tr) undefined, division by zero, non-finite results) will display a red error and prevent result display.
  • All warnings and errors appear in the result box after the placeholder image disappears, maintaining consistent user feedback.
