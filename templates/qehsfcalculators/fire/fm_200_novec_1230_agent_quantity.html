📘 About This Calculator

This calculator determines the required amount of FM-200 or NOVEC 1230 fire suppression agent for a given protected space. Accurate calculation ensures effective fire suppression, avoids excessive use of agent, and supports compliance with international fire safety standards.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Net Hazard Volume (V): Total volume of the protected enclosure (m³).
  • Design Concentration (C): Required agent concentration (%) based on hazard type.
  • Specific Vapor Volume (S): Volume occupied by 1 kg of suppression agent (m³/kg), as per manufacturer data.

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • All fields mandatory: V, C, and S must be provided.
  • Numeric values only: Non-numeric or blank entries are invalid.
  • Net Hazard Volume (V): Must be between 1 – 100,000 m³ (practical NFPA 2001 range).
  • Design Concentration (C): Must be between 3% – 15%. Below 3% is ineffective; above 15% may exceed safety exposure limits.
  • Specific Vapor Volume (S): Must be between 0.5 – 2.0 m³/kg, per manufacturer data.
  • Division by Zero Check: (100 − C) must be > 0.
  • Cross-field consistency: Very large volumes (>10,000 m³) with high concentrations (>12%) trigger warnings.

📊 Output Validations

  • < 50 kg: ⚠️ Very low agent requirement. Confirm if adequate for suppression.
  • 50 – 10,000 kg: ✅ Agent quantity within typical suppression range. Calculation successful.
  • > 10,000 kg: ⚠️ Very high requirement. Review practicality and confirm inputs.

These feedback messages are displayed along with the result, guiding designers to assess whether the calculated agent mass is realistic, compliant, and safe.

📐 Governing Principle

The agent quantity calculators for FM-200 and Novec 1230 are based on the NFPA 2001 standard, "Standard for Clean Agent Fire Extinguishing Systems". This standard provides the necessary guidelines, formulas, and parameters, such as net hazard volume and design concentration, to accurately calculate the required amount of clean agent for a fire suppression system.

📘 Why NFPA 2001 is Used
  • Safety: Ensures that the correct amount of agent is used to effectively suppress a fire while maintaining a safe margin for occupied spaces.
  • Consistency: Provides a uniform method for engineers to design and calculate agent quantities across different clean agent systems.
  • Comprehensive Guidance: Covers various aspects of clean agent systems, including the calculation of agent concentration and the impact of factors like temperature and volume on the system's effectiveness.

In essence, when you use an FM-200 or Novec 1230 agent quantity calculator, you are applying the principles and requirements set forth by the NFPA 2001 standard to ensure a properly designed fire suppression system.

By calculating the correct agent mass, engineers and safety managers can ensure proper fire protection, avoid costly overdesign, and meet NFPA/ISO compliance requirements.
