üìê Governing Principle

An Equivalent Water Flow Rate through a Check Valve is not based on a single standard, but rather on the standard definition of the flow coefficient (Cv or Kv), which is defined by bodies like the Fluid Controls Institute (FCI) and the International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC). The most common standard for Cv is that a valve's Cv value will pass a certain volume of water (US gallons per minute) with a one psi pressure drop.

Understanding Flow Coefficients:

    • Cv (Control Valve): In the US, the Flow Coefficient (Cv) quantifies a valve's flow capability.
    • Kv (SI unit): In the SI system, Kv is the volume of water in cubic meters per hour that flows through a valve with a one-bar pressure drop.
  • How Cv is Determined: A standardized test is used by manufacturers to determine valve flow characteristics, and the Cv value is published for easy comparison.
  • How to Calculate or Find Cv:
    • Use an online calculator.
    • Contact the manufacturer directly.
