📘 About This Calculator

This calculator estimates the breathing duration of an SCBA (Self-Contained Breathing Apparatus) cylinder. SCBA cylinders provide a portable supply of breathable air, crucial for personnel working in hazardous atmospheres such as smoke, toxic gases, low oxygen areas, or chemical zones.

By entering the cylinder's pressure, volume, and breathing rate, this tool calculates how long the air supply will last. Understanding this duration is essential for planning safe operations, avoiding dangerous situations, and ensuring personnel can complete tasks or evacuate safely.

  • Firefighters assessing operational time in smoke-filled environments.
  • Industrial safety teams working in confined spaces or hazardous gas areas.
  • Rescue personnel planning missions with limited air supply.
  • Training purposes to understand air consumption under different workloads.

Monitoring breathing rates is critical, as increased exertion or stress can reduce available air. This calculator helps teams make informed decisions, plan backup supplies, and maintain safety.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • Cylinder Pressure (Bar): Air pressure in the SCBA cylinder (e.g., 300 Bar).
  • Cylinder Volume (Liters): Cylinder capacity (e.g., 6 liters).
  • Breathing Rate (LPM): Rate of air consumption (liters per minute, typical 40–60 LPM).
  • SCBA Duration (Minutes): Calculated duration of air supply. Feedback is displayed alongside the result.

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • All fields are mandatory; empty inputs trigger warnings.
  • Numeric values only; non-numeric inputs are invalid.
  • Positive numbers required; zero or negative values are invalid.
  • Cylinder Pressure: 50–450 Bar; outside this range triggers caution.
  • Cylinder Volume: 2–15 Liters; outside this range triggers caution.
  • Breathing Rate: 10–100 LPM; outside this range triggers caution.
  • Cross-field checks: unrealistic combinations (high pressure with low volume or low pressure with high breathing rate) trigger warnings.
  • Edge cases: breathing rate = 0 or calculated duration <5 or >120 min trigger warnings or critical alerts.
  • Feedback compliance: all results are categorized according to safe, moderate, short, or critical durations in line with NFPA/OSHA guidelines.

📐 Governing Principle

  • SCBA breathing duration calculation is based on industry-specific standards such as the NFPA 1981 (USA) and EN 137 / BIS 10245 (Europe/India), which define performance requirements, minimum duration, and reserve time.
  • Key Standards:
    • NFPA 1981 (USA): Specifies SCBA performance, with cylinders available in 30, 45, or 60-minute durations.
    • EN 137 / BIS 10245 (Europe/India): Defines minimum duration & performance criteria for SCBAs under working conditions.
  • How Calculations Are Made:
    • Cylinder Capacity – volume of air stored.
    • Pressure – operating cylinder pressure.
    • Air Consumption Rate – air inhaled per minute based on activity/exertion.
    • Working duration is derived from available air divided by breathing rate.
    • Effective duration includes safety reserve (10–15 minutes typically).
  • Important Considerations:
    • Standards ensure minimum operational safety durations.
    • Higher exertion reduces practical duration significantly.
