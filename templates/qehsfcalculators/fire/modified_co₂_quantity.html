📘 About This Calculator

This calculator determines the Modified Quantity (mD) of CO₂ required for fire suppression, adjusting the base design quantity using the Material Conversion Factor (MCF). It ensures that different hazards or materials receive the correct CO₂ dose for effective fire control.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Base Design Quantity (mBD): CO₂ amount calculated for the protected volume in kg.
  • Material Conversion Factor (MCF): Factor accounting for the hazard type or material; adjusts the base quantity to ensure proper fire suppression.

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Mandatory Fields: Both mBD and MCF must be provided. Empty inputs trigger a warning.
  • Numeric Values Only: All inputs must be numeric; text or special characters are invalid.
  • Positive Values: mBD > 0 kg, MCF > 0. Zero or negative values are invalid.
  • Practical Upper Limits: mBD ≤ 10,000 kg; MCF ≤ 5. Exceeding these values triggers a caution.
  • Output Reasonableness: Modified Quantity (mD) < 50,000 kg. Values above this limit trigger a caution for verification.
  • Feedback Based on Range:
    • mD < 50 kg → ⚠️ Very low – verify hazard type and protected volume.
    • 50 ≤ mD ≤ 5,000 kg → ✅ Normal range – CO₂ dose is appropriate.
    • mD > 5,000 kg → ⚠️ High – verify hazard type, volume, and MCF.
  • Edge Cases: Empty inputs, non-numeric entries, negative or zero values, and extreme combinations trigger warnings or errors.
  • Compliance Rationale: These validations align with NFPA 12, OSHA, ISO 45001, and QEHS best practices, ensuring safe and auditable CO₂ dosing.

📊 Output Validations

  • ⚠️ Modified Quantity < 50 kg: Very low – may indicate underestimated hazard; verify protected volume and MCF. (Displayed as feedback in the result)
  • ✅ 50 ≤ Modified Quantity ≤ 5,000 kg: Normal range – CO₂ dose appropriate. (Displayed as feedback in the result)
  • ⚠️ Modified Quantity > 5,000 kg: High – check hazard type, protected volume, and MCF. (Displayed as feedback in the result)
  • 🚨 Modified Quantity > 50,000 kg: Unrealistic; likely input error. (Blocked calculation with error)
  • All assessments/feedback displayed with calculation results are repeated here for clarity and audit purposes.

📐 Governing Principle

The calculation of the modified CO₂ quantity is guided by fire protection principles and hazard assessment standards. Each protected volume has a base CO₂ requirement, which must be adjusted according to the type of hazard, material, and fire severity. The adjustment ensures that the CO₂ system provides sufficient concentration to suppress or extinguish potential fires effectively, without under-dosing (which could allow fire propagation) or over-dosing (which can cause unnecessary waste or personnel hazards).

This methodology aligns with NFPA 12 – Standard on Carbon Dioxide Extinguishing Systems, which specifies design considerations, hazard classifications, and recommended safety factors. Compliance ensures that fire suppression systems are reliable, auditable, and consistent with internationally recognized fire safety practices.

🏁 Conclusion

This calculator helps engineers and safety officers determine the appropriate CO₂ quantity for fire suppression. By combining validated inputs, output thresholds, and the governing NFPA 12 principle, the tool ensures safe, effective, and auditable CO₂ dosing. Feedback on result ranges directly guides operational decisions, highlighting low, normal, or high CO₂ quantities.
