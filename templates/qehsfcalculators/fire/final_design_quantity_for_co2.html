📘 About This Calculator

This calculator determines the Final Design Quantity (mFD) of CO₂ required for fire suppression, including adjustments for leakage, ventilation, and temperature extremes. It ensures accurate CO₂ dosing for effective fire protection under real-world conditions.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Modified Quantity (mD): Base CO₂ amount in kilograms (kg).
  • Leakage Adjustment (mlo): Extra CO₂ to compensate for leaks (kg).
  • Ventilation Adjustment (mlv): CO₂ compensation for airflow (kg).
  • Temperature Adjustment (mT): CO₂ compensation for temperature extremes (kg).

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • All fields are mandatory; empty fields trigger a warning.
  • Numeric values only: Non-numeric entries are invalid.
  • Modified Quantity (mD) must be greater than zero; negative or zero values are not allowed.
  • Modified Quantity above 10,000 kg triggers a caution for unusually high input.
  • Leakage (mlo), Ventilation (mlv), Temperature (mT) Adjustments must be zero or positive.
  • Adjustments above 5,000 kg trigger a caution for unusually high values.
  • Adjustments exceeding 50% of mD trigger a warning for potential design issue.

⚡ Output Validations

The Final Design Quantity (mFD) is calculated as the sum of modified quantity and all adjustments. Feedback and assessment are displayed alongside the result:

  • mFD < 100 kg → ⚠️ Very low CO₂ quantity – verify protected volume (orange).
  • 100–5,000 kg → ✅ Normal range – safe (green).
  • 5,001–20,000 kg → ⚠️ High CO₂ quantity – verify large space or adjustments (orange).
  • > 20,000 kg → 🚨 Extreme CO₂ quantity – check inputs and design (red).

This ensures the engineer receives real-time feedback and assessment for design decisions.

📐 Governing Principle

The final design quantity in a CO₂ calculator is guided by internationally recognized standards that ensure accuracy, transparency, and comparability of results. Commonly used standards include ISO 14067, the GHG Protocol Product Standard, and PAS 2050. Each framework provides structured methodologies for quantifying and reporting carbon emissions or equivalent CO₂ outputs.

Standard: The choice of standard depends on the calculator’s purpose, audience, and level of detail.

  • ISO 14067: Best suited for external, credible reporting such as product labels or sustainability reports.
  • GHG Protocol Product Standard: Offers a balanced approach for internal and external carbon assessments.
  • PAS 2050: Ideal for smaller organizations seeking a simpler and faster carbon accounting method.

Related concept: Selecting the right standard for a CO₂ calculator is similar to choosing the correct framework for statistical or safety-based measurements — it ensures that the final design quantity accurately reflects real-world performance and regulatory compliance while aligning with the calculator’s intended use.
