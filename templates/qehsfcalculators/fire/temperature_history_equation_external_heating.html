This calculator estimates the temperature history during homogeneous-vessel venting when there is a constant external heat input. It considers the effects of heat addition, vaporization, and the venting process. This is crucial for analyzing scenarios with external heating sources, such as fire exposure. The formula assumes homogeneous conditions within the vessel, meaning the temperature and composition are uniform throughout. Also, it assumes that the liquid is vaporizing; if no liquid remains, the formula is no longer valid.

üìñ Governing Principle

For calculations involving the temperature history equation with external heating, there is no single, specific global standard. Instead, the methodologies and models for thermal analysis are typically guided by standards from industry-specific organizations. The applicable standards vary depending on the system being analyzed.

  • For Printed Circuit Boards (PCBs): IPC-2152 provides detailed guidance on conductor sizing with temperature rise data; IPC-2221 offers conservative design constraints for trace widths under heating.
  • For building performance and energy efficiency: ISO 13789:2017 calculates heat transfer coefficients; ASHRAE Standard 169 provides climate data necessary for thermal calculations.
  • For materials and general thermal analysis: ASTM standards guide thermal analysis methods, including thermogravimetry and differential scanning calorimetry, to evaluate material property changes with temperature.
  • For numerical simulations: Thermal simulations rely on fundamental heat transfer principles (conduction, convection, radiation) and are validated against benchmark problems such as those from NAFEMS.
