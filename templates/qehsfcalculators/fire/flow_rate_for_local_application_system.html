📘 About This Calculator

This calculator determines the Design Rate of CO₂ required for local application systems, ensuring effective fire suppression for the protected space.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Protected Volume (V): The space requiring CO₂ protection (m³).
  • Flow Rate (Q): Required CO₂ release per unit volume per second (kg/m³·s or L/s).
  • Design Rate of CO₂: Total CO₂ discharged per second to achieve effective coverage.

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • All fields are mandatory; empty fields trigger a warning.
  • Numeric values only: Non-numeric entries are invalid.
  • Positive values required: Zero or negative numbers are invalid.
  • Protected Volume (V): > 0 m³; values above 1,000,000 m³ trigger a caution for unrealistic design.
  • Flow Rate (Q): > 0 kg/m³·s (or L/s); values above 1,000 kg/m³·s trigger a caution for possible overpressure.
  • Any errors or warnings will display in the result box; placeholder image disappears upon calculation.

⚡ Output Validations

The Design Rate of CO₂ Feedback is displayed alongside the result to guide safe design:

  • < 0.1 kg/s → ⚠️ Low Design Rate: May not achieve sufficient fire suppression (orange).
  • 0.1–1,000 kg/s → ✅ Normal Design Rate: System likely effective (green).
  • > 1,000 kg/s → ⚠️ High Design Rate: May exceed system capacity; check cylinder size and pipe rating (red).

This ensures real-time assessment of design adequacy with safety feedback.

📐 Governing Principle

There is no single global standard for determining the flow rate of a local application system, as it varies depending on the system’s purpose and operational requirements. In the context of fire protection systems, NFPA 13 is widely recognized internationally for calculating water density and required flow rates for sprinkler systems. Other systems may follow different international, national, or internal standards depending on the application type.

Standard: The applicable standard depends on the nature of the system and regional regulations.

  • NFPA 13: Used globally for determining water density (GPM/ft²) and total flow for fire sprinklers based on hazard levels.
  • IS 15493 (India): Governs the flow rate calculation for engineered gaseous fire extinguishing systems.
  • CIPHE or Internal Guidelines: Applied in plumbing and HVAC systems using loading units (LU) or fixture-based estimation methods.
  • Manufacturer Specifications: Often provide precise design parameters and flow requirements for proprietary systems.

Related concept: Selecting the correct standard for a flow rate calculator follows the same principle as aligning with recognized frameworks in other technical domains — ensuring accuracy, regulatory compliance, and reliability. Identifying the system type, consulting local codes, and referring to manufacturer guidelines are key steps in applying the correct governing standard.
