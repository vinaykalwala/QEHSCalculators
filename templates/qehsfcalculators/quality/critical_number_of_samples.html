🔎 About This Calculator

The Critical Number of Samples Calculator determines the minimum number of samples required to ensure a specified level of accuracy and confidence in measurement results. It is especially useful for laboratory quality control, clinical diagnostics, and statistical sampling.

❓ Explanation of Parameters

  • Z-Score (Z): Represents the confidence level. For example, 1.96 corresponds to a 95% confidence level, and 2.58 corresponds to a 99% confidence level.
  • Analytical Coefficient of Variation (CVA): Reflects the precision of the measurement method in percentage terms.
  • Within-Subject Biological Variation (CVI): Represents the natural variability within an individual over time, expressed as a percentage.
  • Acceptable Deviation (D): Defines the allowable percentage difference from the true value for the measurement.

📊 Interpretation Guidelines

  • ≤ 2 samples: Small sample size indicates minimal variability and high precision
  • 3-5 samples: Moderate sample size is required when variability is higher
  • > 5 samples: Large sample size is necessary for highly variable measurements

📊 Real-Life Applications

  • Clinical Diagnostics: Ensures accurate patient results by accounting for biological and analytical variability.
  • Laboratory Quality Control: Determines the sample size required to maintain consistent testing accuracy.
  • Statistical Sampling: Supports research and industrial processes by ensuring reliable measurements with specified confidence.
