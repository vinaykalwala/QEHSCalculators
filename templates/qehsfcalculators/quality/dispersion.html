Dispersion Interpretation

🔎 About This Calculator

The Dispersion Calculator estimates the dispersion of a result based on the number of replicate assays (nA), patient samples (nS), and other statistical variables. It helps understand the effect of duplicate assays and multiple samples on result variability.

📌 Input Validations

  • Z-Score: Required, must be > 0
  • CVA (%): Required, must be > 0
  • CVI (%): Required, must be > 0
  • nA: Required, must be ≥ 1
  • nS: Required, must be ≥ 1

❓ Explanation of Parameters

  • Z-Score (Z): Represents the confidence level (e.g., 1.96 for 95% confidence).
  • Analytical CV (CVA): Indicates the precision of the measurement method as a percentage.
  • Within-Subject Biological Variation (CVI): Natural biological variability in percentage.
  • nA (Replicate Assays): Number of times the same sample is measured.
  • nS (Patient Samples): Number of different patient samples.

💡 Key Insights

  • Replicating analyses or using multiple samples reduces dispersion.
  • Lower precision (CVA) significantly improves dispersion, especially if CVA is comparable to CVI.
  • When precision is poor (e.g., 10%), duplicate assays or multiple samples have a larger impact on reducing dispersion.
  • Optimize precision to minimize analytical noise and reveal biological signals.
  • Consider biological variation and precision when designing assays for better accuracy.
