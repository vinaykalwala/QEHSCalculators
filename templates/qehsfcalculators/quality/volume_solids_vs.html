ð Governing Principle

The Volume Solids Calculator is derived from internationally recognized standards that define how to measure and calculate the non-volatile matter in paints and coatings. The most relevant is ISO 3233 and its parts, particularly ISO 3233-3, which detail procedures for determining volume solids content.

Additional references include the Indian Standard IS 101-8-6 (1993) for paints and the U.S. EPA Method 24 for volatile matter and water content. These provide the scientific basis for calculating the percentage of solids that remain in a coating after drying.

  • ISO 3233: Primary international method for calculating volume solids in coatings.
  • IS 101-8-6 (India): National standard for determining volume solids.
  • ASTM D2697 / ASTM D5201: Related U.S. test methods for volume and density of coatings.
  • EPA Method 24: Defines volatile matter and water content in coatings.

By following these standards, the calculator ensures compliance, accuracy, and reliability of results in industrial and protective coating applications.
