🔎 About This Calculator

The Sensible Heat Gain from Infiltration Calculator estimates the amount of heat added to a space due to the uncontrolled entry of outdoor air. This is crucial in HVAC systems to determine the cooling or heating load required for maintaining indoor thermal comfort.

❓ Explanation of Parameters

  • Airflow: The volumetric flow rate of air entering the space (CFM for Imperial, m³/s for SI).
  • Temperature Difference (ΔT): The difference between outdoor and indoor air temperatures.
  • Constants:
    • 1.085: Imperial unit constant used for airflow in CFM
    • 1210: SI unit constant used for airflow in m³/s

📌 Input Validations

  • Airflow (CFM or m³/s): Required, must be > 0
  • Temperature Difference (ΔT): Required, must be > 0
  • Realistic Values: Airflow typically ranges from 10-10,000 CFM (0.005-5 m³/s)
  • Temperature Difference: Typically ranges from 1-50°F (0.5-28°C)

📝Output Validations

  • Low Heat Gain (< 1,000 BTU/h): Minimal impact on HVAC load; usually negligible for residential systems.
  • Moderate Heat Gain (1,000 – 10,000 BTU/h): Standard residential or small commercial impact; HVAC sizing should consider this load.
  • Significant Heat Gain (10,000 – 50,000 BTU/h): Substantial impact on HVAC systems; may require larger equipment or supplemental cooling.
  • High Heat Gain (> 50,000 BTU/h): Major impact; specialized HVAC solutions or system redesign may be required.

📐 Governing Principle

The Sensible Heat Gain from Infiltration calculator is based on the principles outlined in the ASHRAE Fundamentals Handbook. It estimates the amount of heat added to a space due to uncontrolled infiltration of outdoor air, which is a key component of the total HVAC cooling load.

  • Core Concept: Sensible heat gain occurs when air at a different temperature enters a space, causing energy transfer to or from the conditioned environment.
  • Methodology: Calculations use airflow (CFM or m³/s) and temperature difference (ΔT) between outdoor and indoor air.
  • ASHRAE Constants: Standardized constants ensure unit consistency:
    • Imperial: 1.085 (for CFM × ΔT → BTU/h)
    • SI: 1210 (for m³/s × ΔT → Watts)
  • Design Relevance: Helps engineers size HVAC systems correctly, ensuring thermal comfort and energy efficiency.
  • Assumptions: Infiltration is uncontrolled, steady-state, and airflow is evenly mixed.

⚠️ Always ensure that airflow and temperature inputs are realistic and within design ranges for accurate HVAC load estimation.

💡 Conclusion

Sensible heat gain from infiltration is a critical factor in maintaining indoor air quality and thermal comfort. By calculating and addressing this parameter, buildings can improve energy efficiency, reduce costs, and ensure a consistent indoor environment.
