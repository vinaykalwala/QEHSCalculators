📘 About This Calculator

The Composite Risk Score combines exposure magnitude with probability of occurrence to quantify overall risk. It provides a single metric that reflects both how often a hazard occurs and how severe its consequences are. This approach enables better prioritization of safety measures and resource allocation.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Composite Exposure Score: Numerical score representing magnitude/frequency of exposure (typically 0-100 scale).
  • Percentage Risk Index: The probability or relative importance of the risk (0-100%).

📊 Interpretation

The Composite Risk Score ranges from 0 to 1000, where:

  • 0-250 → Low risk (routine monitoring)
  • 250-500 → Moderate risk (requires controls)
  • 500-750 → High risk (immediate action needed)
  • 750-1000 → Extreme risk (stop operations)

🔍 Real-Life Applications

Composite risk scores are essential in various safety applications:

  • Fire Safety Planning: Assessing overall fire risks in buildings
  • Industrial Safety: Evaluating machine operation hazards
  • Chemical Handling: Determining storage and handling procedures
  • Emergency Preparedness: Prioritizing disaster response plans

🛡️ Validation Rules Applied

  • Composite Exposure Score: 0–100, max 1 decimal, no negatives.
  • Risk Index (%): 0–100%, max 1 decimal, no negatives.
  • Cross-field Check: If Exposure = 0 → final score = 0, regardless of Risk Index.
  • Output (Composite Risk): Always between 0–1000.
  • Audit Note: Validations align with OSHA, NFPA, and ISO 45001 standards.

🏭 Industry Standards & Guidelines

Used in NFPA risk assessment methodologies and OSHA hazard evaluation frameworks. Aligns with ISO 45001 occupational health and safety standards.

📈 Why This Calculation is Important?

Composite risk scores enable safety professionals to prioritize hazards that combine high exposure with high probability, directing resources to areas needing most urgent attention in fire prevention and workplace safety.
