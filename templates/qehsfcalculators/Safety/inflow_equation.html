📘 About This Calculator

This tool calculates the Production Rate for oil wells using the Inflow Performance Relationship (IPR) equation, which estimates oil well productivity based on reservoir and well conditions.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Formation Permeability (k): Determines ease of fluid flow in the rock formation (md).
  • Formation Thickness (h): The height of the producing formation (ft).
  • Reservoir Pressure (Pe): The pressure at the reservoir boundary (psi).
  • Wellbore Pressure (Pw): The pressure at the wellbore (psi).
  • Formation Volume Factor (Bo): Adjusts for expansion effects (RB/STB).
  • Oil Viscosity (μo): Higher viscosity slows flow (cp).
  • Drainage Radius (re): Defines the drainage area (ft).
  • Wellbore Radius (rw): The radius of the wellbore (ft).
  • Skin Factor (Sf): Indicates well performance (damage or enhancement).

📊 Interpretation

Production Rate Guidelines:

  • Below 100 STB/D → Low production rate
  • 100-500 STB/D → Moderate production rate
  • 500-2000 STB/D → Good production rate
  • 2000-5000 STB/D → High production rate
  • Above 5000 STB/D → Very high production rate

🔑 Key Applications

  • Well Performance Prediction: Estimates production rates for oil wells.
  • Reservoir Management: Helps in planning reservoir development strategies.
  • Economic Evaluation: Supports economic decisions regarding well development.
  • Production Optimization: Guides decisions to enhance oil extraction efficiency.

✅ Validation Rules & Input Constraints

  • Total Resolution Time (hours): Must be ≥ 0 and ≤ 10,000. Decimals allowed (max 2 places).
  • Number of Issues Resolved: Must be an integer ≥ 1 and ≤ 10,000.
  • Logical Consistency: ART = TRT ÷ NI. Division by zero not allowed.
  • Edge Cases: ART = 0 → Warning (check data); ART > 24 h/issue → Flagged as unrealistic.
  • Output: Rounded to 2 decimals, expressed in hours per issue resolved.
