The Hazard Score is a metric used to quantify the risk level of identified hazards. It combines the total of hazard indicators with a severity multiplier to produce a comprehensive risk rating. This simple yet powerful calculation helps prioritize safety interventions in various environments.

‚öôÔ∏è Parameters Used

  • Sum of Hazard Factors: Total of all identified hazard components (frequency, exposure, etc.)
  • Hazard Weightage Factor: Multiplier reflecting severity/consequence (typically 1-5 scale)

üìä Interpretation Guidelines

  • 0-10 ‚Üí Low risk (routine monitoring)
  • 10-25 ‚Üí Moderate risk (requires controls)
  • 25-50 ‚Üí High risk (immediate action needed)
  • 50+ ‚Üí Extreme risk (stop activities)

üîç Real-Life Applications

  • Workplace Safety: Prioritizing hazard controls in industrial settings
  • Fire Risk Assessment: Evaluating building fire hazards
  • Chemical Storage: Ranking hazardous material risks
  • Construction Safety: Assessing site-specific dangers
  • Emergency Planning: Identifying high-risk scenarios

üìà Common Weightage Factors
