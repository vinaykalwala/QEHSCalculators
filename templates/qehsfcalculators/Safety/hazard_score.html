The Hazard Score Calculator is a quantitative tool designed to evaluate and prioritize workplace, chemical, or process-related hazards. By multiplying the sum of hazard factors by a weightage factor, this calculator produces a numerical score that helps organizations identify, classify, and mitigate risks according to their severity and likelihood.

‚öô Parameters Used

  • Sum of Hazard Factors: The total score representing all identified hazards (e.g., frequency, exposure level, number of people affected).
  • Hazard Weightage Factor: A multiplier based on the severity or consequence of each hazard, typically ranging from 1 to 10.

‚úÖ Input Validation Rules

  • Sum of Hazard Factors: Must be between 0 and 100 (maximum one decimal place).
  • Hazard Weightage Factor: Must be between 1 and 10 (maximum one decimal place).
  • No negatives: Negative or zero weightage values are invalid.
  • Logical consistency: If hazards exist, weightage cannot be 0.
  • Range check: Inputs exceeding the defined maximums are flagged as invalid.

Validation thresholds are aligned with ISO 45001, OSHA 29 CFR 1910, and NFPA 551 guidelines.
