📘 About This Calculator

The Collision Rate (CR) is a metric used to determine how often crashes occur at specific intersections or road segments. It is expressed as the number of crashes per million vehicles and helps identify high-risk areas for road safety improvements.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Number of Crashes (A): Total number of recorded traffic collisions during the analysis period.
  • Annual Average Daily Traffic (V): The average number of vehicles passing a specific location per day over a year.
  • Number of Days (D): The total number of days over which the crash data is collected.
  • Time in Years (T): The duration (in years) for which the crash data is analyzed.

📊 Interpretation

The Collision Rate is typically interpreted as:

  • Below 50 → Low collision rate: The area appears to be relatively safe
  • 50 - 150 → Moderate collision rate: The area may need periodic monitoring
  • Above 150 → High collision rate: Consider implementing safety improvements

🔍 Purpose of Collision Rate

The CR value is essential in various traffic safety applications:

  • Identify High-Risk Areas: Pinpoint intersections or road segments with a high frequency of crashes
  • Monitor Trends: Track changes in crash frequency over time to evaluate the effectiveness of safety measures
  • Prioritize Improvements: Allocate resources to areas with the highest collision rates to improve road safety

✅ Input Validation Rules (CR Calculator)

  • Crashes (A): ≥ 0, typically ≤ 1000.
  • AADT (V): > 0, typically 100–100,000 vehicles/day.
  • Days (D): > 0, typically ≤ 3650 (10 years).
  • Years (T): > 0, typically 0.1–10 years.
  • No division by zero: V, D, or T cannot be zero.
  • Extreme CR values: >1000 flagged for review.

Compliance Note: These checks align with FHWA Highway Safety Manual and ISO 39001 (Road Traffic Safety Management).
