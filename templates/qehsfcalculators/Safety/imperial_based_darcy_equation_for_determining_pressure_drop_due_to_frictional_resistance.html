‚ùì What is the D'Arcy-Weisbach Equation?

The D'Arcy-Weisbach equation is a fundamental formula in fluid mechanics used to calculate the pressure drop or head loss due to friction along a given length of pipe with a constant flow rate.

‚öôÔ∏è Parameters Used

  • Friction Factor (f): Dimensionless parameter that depends on pipe roughness and flow type (laminar or turbulent).
  • Length of Pipe (L): The distance over which the fluid travels [m]. Longer pipes cause more frictional loss.
  • Flow Velocity (u): The speed at which the fluid moves through the pipe [m/s]. Faster flows lead to greater energy loss.
  • Pipe Diameter (D): The internal diameter of the pipe [m]. Smaller diameters increase resistance.
  • Gravity (g): Acceleration due to gravity (9.81 m/s¬≤) - affects the fluid's energy loss.
