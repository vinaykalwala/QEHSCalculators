📘 About TRIFR

The **Total Recordable Incident Frequency Rate (TRIFR)** is a safety metric that measures the number of recordable incidents (fatalities, lost time injuries, restricted work, and medical treatment cases) for every **1,000,000** hours worked. It is a lagging indicator widely used internationally to benchmark safety performance, especially in industries with large workforces.

⚙️ Formula Used

The TRIFR is calculated as follows:

$$ \text{TRIFR} = \frac{\text{Total Recordable Incidents}}{\text{Total Hours Worked}} \times 1,000,000 $$

📊 TRIFR vs. TRIR

While similar, TRIFR and TRIR (Total Recordable Incident Rate) use different multipliers, which is important for accurate comparison.

Metric Multiplier Common Usage
TRIFR 1,000,000 hours International standards (e.g., Australia, UK), global reporting, industries with large workforces.
TRIR 200,000 hours U.S. OSHA standard, representing 100 employees working a full year.
