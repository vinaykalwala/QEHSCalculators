📘 About This Calculator

This calculator estimates the **Turnaround Time (τ)** during homogeneous-vessel venting when there is a constant external heat input. It determines the time available to take action before a critical event, considering the effects of vaporization and external heating. This value represents the crucial window of time available for operators to take corrective action before a potentially hazardous condition, such as overpressurization, occurs.

It's a fundamental calculation in thermal hazard analysis, emergency relief system design, and process safety planning.

📐 Parameters

  • τ (Turnaround Time): The time available before a critical event occurs.
  • tr (Emptying Time): Time to empty the vessel without external heating.
  • V (Volume): Vessel volume.
  • hfa (Latent Heat): Energy required to vaporize the liquid.
  • QT (External Heat Input): Constant external heat rate.
  • vfa (Specific Volume): Specific volume of the liquid.

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Emptying Time ($t_r$): Must be a valid number greater than 0.
  • Volume (V): Must be a valid number greater than 0.
  • Latent Heat ($h_{fa}$): Must be a valid number greater than 0.
  • Heat Input ($Q_T$): Must be a valid number greater than 0.
  • Specific Volume ($v_{fa}$): Must be a valid number greater than 0.
  • Cross-Field Check: Denominator in calculation must not be zero (avoids invalid division).

💡 Allowed Decimals

  • All inputs can accept values up to 2 decimal places for practical accuracy.

📊 Output Validations

  • ❌ Critical Condition (t < 0): The heating rate exceeds the emptying rate. System will overpressurize. Immediate process review required.
  • ⚠️ Danger (t ≤ 5 min): Very short response time. Relies on automated safety systems and rapid operator action.
  • 🟡 Warning (5–15 min): Limited time for intervention. Manual response possible but requires high alert levels.
  • ✅ Safe (t > 15 min): Sufficient time available for operators to act safely and take corrective action.
  • ❌ Invalid Number: If calculation results in a non-finite value, inputs must be checked and corrected.

✅ Conclusion

This calculation helps understand time constraints during venting with external heating. Accurate inputs are crucial for reliable results. The turnaround time is a critical safety metric for emergency response and risk mitigation, supporting better safety planning in high-risk industrial environments.
