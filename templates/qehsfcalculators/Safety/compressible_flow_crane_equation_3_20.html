📘 About This Calculator

This tool calculates the Flow Rate for Compressible Fluids using Crane Equation 3-20, which is used for determining flow through valves, fittings, and pipes in various industrial applications.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Expansion Factor (Y): Adjusts for compressibility effects in high-pressure flows.
  • Internal Diameter (d): The inside diameter of the pipe in inches.
  • Pressure Drop (ΔP): The difference between upstream and downstream pressure in psia.
  • Upstream Pressure (P₁): The absolute pressure at the inlet in psia.
  • Loss Coefficient (K): Accounts for resistance from fittings, valves, and pipe roughness.
  • Absolute Temperature (T₁): The absolute temperature at the inlet in Rankine.
  • Specific Gravity (Sg): The ratio of gas density to air density at standard conditions.

📊 Interpretation

Flow Rate Guidelines:

  • Below 100 SCFM → Low flow rate
  • 100-1,000 SCFM → Moderate flow rate
  • 1,000-10,000 SCFM → Normal industrial range
  • 10,000-50,000 SCFM → High flow rate
  • Above 50,000 SCFM → Very high flow rate

🔑 Key Applications

  • Oil & Gas Pipelines: Ensures safe transportation of natural gas.
  • Power Plants: Determines steam flow through turbines and boilers.
  • Fire Safety Systems: Calculates gas flow rates in suppression systems.
  • Process Industries: Sizing of pressure relief valves and vent systems.

✅ Validation Rules & Input Constraints

  • Expansion Factor (Y): Must be between 0.1 and 1.0.
  • Internal Diameter (d): Must be > 0 inches.
  • Pressure Drop (ΔP): Must be > 0 psia.
  • Upstream Pressure (P₁): Must be > 0 psia.
  • Loss Coefficient (K): Must be > 0.
  • Absolute Temperature (T₁): Must be > 0 Rankine.
  • Specific Gravity (Sg): Must be > 0.
  • Pressure Check: ΔP must be less than P₁.

🏭 Industry Standards & Guidelines

Based on ASME & Crane Technical Paper 410 standards for pressure loss calculations in compressible flow systems.

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Expansion Factor (Y): Must be between 0.1 and 1.0.
  • Internal Diameter (d): 0.25 – 48 inches.
  • Pressure Drop (ΔP): > 0 psia and less than P₁.
  • Upstream Pressure (P₁): > 0 psia (typical 14.7–3000 psia).
  • Loss Coefficient (K): > 0 (typical 0.1–100).
  • Temperature (T₁): > 0 R, ≤ 2000 R.
  • Specific Gravity (Sg): > 0 (typical 0.1–5).
  • Cross-check: ΔP must be less than P₁, warn if ΔP/P₁ > 0.5 (possible choked flow).

Note: These validations follow ASME and Crane TP-410 standards for compressible flow calculations.
