üìò About Gravitational Force

The force of gravity, commonly known as **weight**, is the force exerted on an object by a gravitational field. This calculator determines this force for a particle, which is fundamental to understanding its behavior in many contexts, from settling in a fluid (sedimentation) to determining the load on a structure.

‚öôÔ∏è Formula Used

The force of gravity ($F_g$) is calculated using Newton's second law ($F=ma$) where the mass (m) is derived from density ($\rho$) and volume (V), and the acceleration (a) is the gravitational acceleration (g).

$$ F_g = (\rho_p \cdot V_p) \cdot g $$

üí° Applications & Limitations

  • Environmental Engineering: Modeling how pollutants or sediments settle in water or air.
  • Structural & Mechanical Engineering: Calculating the weight of components for load-bearing and stress analysis.
  • Limitations: This calculation provides the raw gravitational force. For a particle settling in a fluid, you must also consider opposing forces like **buoyancy** and **drag** to determine the net settling force and velocity.
