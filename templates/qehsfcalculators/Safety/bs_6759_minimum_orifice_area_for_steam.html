📘 About This Calculator

The BS-6759 Minimum Orifice Area Calculator for Steam helps determine the minimum orifice area required for steam safety valves based on the BS-6759 standard.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Mass Flow Rate (ṁ): The total mass flow rate of steam that needs to be released (kg/h).
  • Absolute Relieving Pressure (Pᵣ): The total system pressure at which the valve will relieve steam (bar a).
  • Derated Coefficient of Discharge (Kdr): A correction factor accounting for discharge efficiency (0–1).
  • Superheat Correction Factor (Ksh): A factor adjusting for superheated steam conditions.

📝 Input Validation Rules

  • Mass Flow Rate (ṁ): Must be > 0 and ≤ 1,000,000 kg/h.
  • Absolute Relieving Pressure (Pᵣ): Must be > 1.01 bar a and ≤ 320 bar a.
  • Derated Coefficient of Discharge (Kdr): Must be between 0.65 and 1.0.
  • Superheat Correction Factor (Ksh): Must be between 0.9 and 1.2.
  • Orifice Area Results: <50 mm² = very small, 50–2000 mm² = optimal, 2000–8000 mm² = large, >8000 mm² = unrealistic.
  • Invalid / extreme values: Blocked with error messages to ensure compliance with BS 6759 & ISO 4126.

📊 Output Validations

  • Orifice Area < 50 mm²: ⚠️ Very small; verify against valve catalog.
  • Orifice Area 50–2000 mm²: ✅ Optimal range; suitable for standard safety valves.
  • Orifice Area 2000–8000 mm²: ⚠️ Large orifice; may require pilot-operated or multiple valves.
  • Orifice Area > 8000 mm²: ❌ Unrealistic; recheck input data.

📏 Governing Principle

BS 6759 is a British Standard that outlines methods for calculating the minimum required orifice area for safety valves, including those used in steam applications. This standard is specifically referenced for steam, air/gases, and liquids.

How it Works

  • Calculation Formula: Uses the BS-6759 formula to determine the minimum orifice area required for a given mass flow rate and system pressure, adjusted by Kdr and Ksh factors.
  • Flow Estimation: Ensures that the calculated area allows adequate steam discharge to prevent overpressure.

Relevant Standards & Guidelines

  • BS 6759 (UK): Specifies design, performance, and testing requirements for safety valves.
  • ISO 4126: Safety valve standard for pressure relief devices for gases and steam.

✅ Conclusion

This tool helps engineers and technicians determine the minimum orifice area required for a safety valve in steam systems. Proper valve sizing enhances safety, efficiency, and compliance with industrial safety standards.
