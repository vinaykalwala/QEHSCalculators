About Valve Authority

Valve Authority (N) is a measure of how effectively a control valve can regulate flow within a hydraulic or pneumatic system. It is the ratio of the pressure drop across the fully open valve to the total pressure drop of the entire circuit. A well-designed system has a valve authority within an optimal range to ensure stable and efficient control.

Interpretation of Results

  • Low Authority (< 0.25): The valve has poor control over the flow. The system pressure drop is dominant, making valve adjustments ineffective.
  • Optimal Range (0.25 - 0.5): This range provides good, stable flow control without excessive energy loss.
  • High Authority (> 0.5): The valve dominates the system pressure drop, which can lead to inefficient energy use and potentially unstable or overly sensitive control.

✅ Validation Rules

  • Pressure Drop Across Valve (ΔP₁): Must be a positive number. A valve must have some resistance to flow.
  • Pressure Drop Across Circuit (ΔP₂): Must be a non-negative number.
