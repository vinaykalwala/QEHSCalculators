About Valve Closing Force

This formula calculates the total force required to close a valve. This force must overcome both the force exerted by the fluid pressure on the valve's seating area and the internal friction of the valve's components. Accurate calculation is crucial for selecting appropriate actuators and ensuring reliable valve operation and safety.

Parameters Explained

  • Valve Seating Area (A): The contact area where the valve seals against fluid pressure, measured in square meters (m¬≤).
  • Differential Pressure (ŒîP): The pressure difference across the valve (upstream vs. downstream), measured in kilopascals (kPa).
  • Friction Allowance: An additional force required to overcome mechanical resistance from seals, packing, and sliding components, measured in kilonewtons (kN).
