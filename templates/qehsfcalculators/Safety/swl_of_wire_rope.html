📘 About This Calculator

The Safe Working Load (SWL) Calculator determines the maximum weight a wire rope can safely handle. Ensures compliance with OSHA, ASME, and ISO standards for lifting safety.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • Diameter (mm): Measured thickness of the wire rope.
  • Conversion: 1 inch = 25.4 mm

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Diameter is required (cannot be blank).
  • Must be numeric, no symbols or text.
  • Valid range: 1 – 200 mm.
  • Decimals allowed: up to 1 place.
  • Diameter ≤ 0 → invalid.
  • Diameter < 1 mm → too small for industrial use.
  • Diameter > 200 mm → flag as non-standard, verify input.

✅ Output Validations

  • SWL < 1 ton → Light duty application. Suitable for small loads.
  • 1 ≤ SWL < 5 tons → Medium duty application. Common for general lifting tasks.
  • 5 ≤ SWL < 20 tons → Heavy duty application. Suitable for industrial lifting.
  • SWL ≥ 20 tons → Very heavy duty application. Requires specialized equipment and procedures.

📋 Standards

  • OSHA: 29 CFR 1910 – Wire rope safety requirements.
  • ASME B30.9: Safety Standard for Slings.
  • ISO 2408: Steel wire ropes – Requirements.
  • API 9A: Specification for Wire Rope.

🔎 Conclusion

This calculator helps prevent lifting accidents by ensuring wire ropes are not overloaded. Always combine SWL calculations with regular inspections, correct rigging practices, and adherence to OSHA/ASME/ISO standards.
