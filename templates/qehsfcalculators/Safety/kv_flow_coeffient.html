📘 About This Calculator

The Kv Flow Coefficient Calculator determines the flow capacity of a valve by calculating how much fluid can pass through at a given pressure drop, considering the fluid's specific gravity.

❓ What is Kv Flow Coefficient?

The Kv value represents the flow rate in cubic meters per hour (m³/h) of water at a temperature between 5°C and 40°C that will pass through a valve with a pressure drop of 1 bar.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Flowrate (Q): The volume of fluid passing through the valve per unit time, measured in cubic meters per hour (m³/h).
  • Specific Gravity (SG): The density of the fluid compared to water. It determines how heavy or light the fluid is.
  • Pressure Drop (ΔP): The difference in pressure before and after the valve, measured in bar.

📊 Interpretation

Higher Kv Values = greater flow capacity. Generally:

  • Small valves: Kv values below 5
  • Medium valves: Kv values between 5 and 50
  • Large valves: Kv values above 50

🌍 Real-Life Applications

  • HVAC Systems: Determining proper valve sizes for heating, ventilation, and air conditioning
  • Water Treatment Plants: Ensuring efficient water flow management in purification systems
  • Oil and Gas Industry: Controlling the flow of fuels and chemicals through pipelines
  • Industrial Machinery: Regulating pressure and fluid movement in hydraulic systems

📋 Industry Standards

IEC 60534-2-1, ISO 5167, and ANSI/FCI 70-2 standards provide guidelines for flow coefficient calculations and valve sizing in various industrial applications.

✅ Validation Rules (Applied in Calculator)

  • Flowrate (Q): Must be > 0 and ≤ 10,000 m³/h. Decimals allowed (3 max).
  • Specific Gravity (SG): Must be > 0 and ≤ 10 (dimensionless). Typical fluids range 0.5 – 1.5.
  • Pressure Drop (ΔP): Must be > 0 and ≤ 100 bar. Decimals allowed (2 max).
  • Formula: Kv = Q × √(SG / ΔP) (IEC 60534-2-1 / ANSI/FCI 70-2 compliant).
  • Output Validation: Kv must be ≥ 0 and ≤ 10,000. Values above this trigger a warning.
  • Edge Cases: Prevent division by zero, negative inputs, or unrealistic SG > 10.
