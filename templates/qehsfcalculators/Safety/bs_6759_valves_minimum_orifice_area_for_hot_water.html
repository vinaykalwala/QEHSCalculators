📘 About This Calculator

This calculator determines the minimum orifice area required for hot water discharge through a safety relief valve, based on heating capacity, pressure, and discharge efficiency according to BS 6759 standards.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Q (Heating Capacity): The energy (in kW) that the system can release as heat.
  • PR (Absolute Relieving Pressure): The total pressure at which the valve releases hot water, measured in bar absolute.
  • Kdr (Derated Coefficient of Discharge): A correction factor representing valve efficiency in real conditions (typically 0.65–0.85).

📝 Input Validations

  • Q (Heating Capacity): Must be 1–100,000 kW. Below 1 kW or above 100,000 kW is flagged.
  • PR (Absolute Relieving Pressure): Must be >1 bar a and ≤200 bar a. ≤1 bar a is invalid.
  • Kdr (Coefficient): Allowed 0.6–0.85 (typical). Must not exceed 1.0.
  • Division by zero is prevented if PR or Kdr = 0.
  • Calculated Orifice Area (Ao) > 1,000,000 mm² is flagged as unrealistic.

📊 Output Validations

  • Ao < 10 mm²: ⚠️ Very small orifice size. Verify availability of certified valves.
  • Ao 10–100 mm²: ✅ Standard orifice size. Most commercial valves can handle this.
  • Ao 100–1000 mm²: ⚠️ Medium/large orifice size. Confirm valve model compatibility.
  • Ao > 1000 mm²: ⚠️ Large orifice size. Consider multiple valves for safety compliance.
  • Ao > 1,000,000 mm²: 🚨 Inputs likely incorrect. Unrealistic orifice area.

📏 Governing Principle

Standard for Minimum Orifice Area Calculation: The calculation of the minimum orifice area for safety valves, including those for hot water applications, is governed by BS 6759 : Part 1 (1984), which is the British Standard for Safety Valves. This standard specifically addresses safety valves for steam and hot water where the set pressure is greater than 1 bar gauge.

How it Works

  • Calculation Formula: Ao = Q / (0.329 × PR × Kdr)
  • Material Estimation: Ensures minimum orifice opening for safe hot water discharge and proper pressure relief.

Relevant Standards & Guidelines

  • BS 6759: British Standard for safety valves covering performance, design, and testing.
  • Manufacturer Guidelines: Provides coefficients of discharge and practical sizing ranges.

✅ Conclusion

This formula is a critical safety calculation for hot water relief valves, ensuring proper pressure control and system safety.
