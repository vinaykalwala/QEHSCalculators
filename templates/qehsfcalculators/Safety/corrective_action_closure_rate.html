📘 About This Calculator

The Corrective Action Closure Rate (CACR) calculator measures the percentage of identified corrective actions that have been successfully closed within a given timeframe. This metric helps organizations assess how efficiently they are resolving safety, quality, or compliance issues and improving their internal processes.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Closed Corrective Actions (C): The number of corrective actions that have been completed and verified.
  • Total Corrective Actions Identified (T): The total number of corrective actions initiated during a specific period.

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Closed Actions (C): Must be ≥ 0, integer, cannot exceed Total Actions.
  • Total Actions (T): Must be > 0, integer (denominator cannot be zero).
  • Cross-field Validation: Closed Actions ≤ Total Actions.
  • Practical Range: Both C and T within 0–10,000 (values beyond flagged for audit verification).
  • No Decimals: Both values must be whole numbers since actions are countable.

Compliance Note: Validation aligns with ISO 9001, ISO 45001, and OSHA audit practices for CAPA performance monitoring.

📈 Output Validation Rules

  • Range Enforcement: The Closure Rate (%) is always between 0% and 100%.
  • Rounding: Results are rounded to two decimal places for reporting accuracy.
  • Interpretation Bands:
    • Below 70% → Low closure rate: Urgent improvement needed. Investigate root causes and allocate resources.
    • 70%–89% → Moderate progress: Continue monitoring and improve closure timelines.
    • 90% and above → Excellent closure rate: Strong compliance and timely corrective action management.

Assessment Feedback: The same feedback above is displayed dynamically along with the result in the calculator’s output section under “Performance Assessment.”

📗 Governing Principle

A Corrective Action Closure Rate Calculator is not from a single specific standard but is a performance indicator used in quality management systems, such as those based on ISO 9001 and its derivatives like the pharma-focused CAPA process, to measure the efficiency and effectiveness of an organization's Corrective and Preventive Action (CAPA) system.

How it works: It measures the percentage of CAPAs that are successfully closed within a specific timeframe out of the total number opened. It is a Key Performance Indicator (KPI) used to monitor how effectively an organization identifies, corrects, and prevents non-conformities.

Relevance to Standards:

  • ISO 9001: The corrective action process is a fundamental requirement for continuous improvement and performance monitoring. Although ISO 9001 does not define a calculator, it emphasizes tracking and improving CAPA performance.
  • ISO 45001 & ISO 14001: Require monitoring of corrective action effectiveness as part of safety and environmental management systems.
  • Pharmaceutical Quality Standards: CAPA effectiveness is a key compliance measure for GMP-based systems, reflecting closure rate as a regulatory KPI.

🏁 Conclusion

The Corrective Action Closure Rate calculator provides a clear, quantitative view of an organization’s CAPA process effectiveness. It helps track timely resolution of non-conformities, ensures accountability, and supports compliance with quality and safety management frameworks like ISO 9001, ISO 45001, and OSHA. Maintaining a high closure rate reflects strong internal process discipline and proactive risk management.
