📘 About This Calculator

This calculator determines the Effective Steam Pressure in a gas mixture based on Dalton's Law of Partial Pressures.

❓ What is Dalton's Law?

Dalton's Law states that the total pressure exerted by a mixture of gases is equal to the sum of the partial pressures of each individual gas in the mixture.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Indicated Pressure: The total pressure of the gas mixture measured in bar absolute.
  • Steam Proportion: The fraction of steam in the total gas mixture (between 0 and 1).

📊 Real-Time Example

Imagine a chemical plant using a boiler to generate steam. A sensor measures the total pressure inside the steam line at 4.5 bar(a). The mixture contains approximately 70% steam (proportion = 0.7).

The effective steam pressure would be calculated as: 0.7 × 4.5 = 3.15 bar(a).

🛡️ Input Validation Rules

  • Indicated Pressure: Must be between 0–100 bar(a), numeric, max 2 decimals.
  • Steam Proportion: Must be 0–1 (fraction), numeric, max 3 decimals.
  • Cross-checks:
    • If P=0 & S>0 → System depressurized warning.
    • If S=0 → Result = 0 (no steam).
    • If S=1 → Result = total pressure (pure steam).
  • Output Validity: Effective steam pressure must be 0 ≤ Pₛ ≤ P_total.
  • Error Handling: Blank, non-numeric, or unrealistic values are rejected for compliance safety.
