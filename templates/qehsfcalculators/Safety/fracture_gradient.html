📘 About This Calculator

The Fracture Gradient Calculator helps engineers and safety professionals determine the fracture gradient of a formation. It plays a crucial role in well integrity, casing design, and blowout prevention by calculating the pressure required to fracture the formation.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • LOT (Leak-off Test Pressure): The pressure applied to determine the formation's fracture limit (psi).
  • D (Depth): The depth at which the leak-off test is conducted (ft).

📊 Interpretation

Fracture Gradient values determine formation stability:

  • Below 0.5 psi/ft → Low fracture gradient. Risk of formation instability.
  • 0.5 – 1.0 psi/ft → Optimal fracture gradient. Formation is stable.
  • Above 1.0 psi/ft → High fracture gradient. Risk of lost circulation and formation fracturing.

🔍 Applications in QEHS

The Fracture Gradient is a crucial tool for:

  • Well Integrity & Safety: Ensures casing and cementing are designed to withstand formation pressures
  • Blowout Prevention: Helps in determining safe mud weights to prevent well control incidents
  • Environmental Protection: Prevents underground fluid contamination by controlling formation fractures
  • Regulatory Compliance: Ensures adherence to industry safety and environmental standards (API, OSHA, etc.)
  • Risk Assessment: Used in well planning to assess potential drilling hazards

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Leak-off Test Pressure (LOT): Must be a positive number between 100-30,000 psi.
  • Depth (D): Must be a positive number between 100-30,000 ft.
  • No negative or zero values: Both parameters must be realistic positive values.
  • No text values: Only numerical inputs are accepted.

Note: These validations are based on API standards and industry best practices for drilling operations.
