It ensures that anchors are not overloaded during lifting operations, enhancing safety and preventing structural failure.

üìê Governing Principle

The standards for Safety Load Anchor Calculation Tools and the anchors themselves vary depending on the country or region where they are used. The specific standard also depends on the application, such as fall protection, work positioning, or structural design.

Common International Standards

  • European Standard (EN 795:2012): This standard specifically covers "Personal fall protection equipment‚ÄîAnchor devices". It classifies anchors into different types (A to E) based on their application, for instance:
    • Type A: Fixed anchors for walls, columns, and roofs.
    • Type C: Flexible horizontal lifelines.
  • CEN/TS 16415:2013: This is a technical specification for multi-user anchor devices, which supplements EN 795 when more than one person will be attached to an anchor.
  • Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) standards: In the United States, OSHA mandates that anchor points used for fall arrest must be capable of supporting at least 5,000 pounds (22.2 kN) per worker, or be designed and used as part of a complete fall protection system under the supervision of a qualified person with a safety factor of at least two.
  • American National Standards Institute (ANSI) Z359 standards: The Z359 series covers various aspects of fall protection. For example, ANSI Z359.18 covers requirements for anchorage connectors.
  • Australian/New Zealand Standard (AS/NZS 5532:2013): This standard provides manufacturing requirements for single-point anchor devices used for work at height in these regions.

Manufacturer-Specific Tools

Many companies develop their own calculation software to comply with relevant standards. A prominent example is Hilti's PROFIS Engineering Suite, a calculation tool used for anchor design that considers various international codes like the Indian Standard (IS) 1946 Part 2, Eurocode 2, and American Concrete Institute (ACI) 318.

Choosing the Right Standard

Because anchor load calculation depends on local regulations, the structural material (e.g., concrete, steel, wood), and the specific type of anchor, you must select the appropriate standard based on your project's location and application. Consulting with a qualified person or professional engineer is essential for ensuring that all calculations and equipment selections comply with the required safety standards.
