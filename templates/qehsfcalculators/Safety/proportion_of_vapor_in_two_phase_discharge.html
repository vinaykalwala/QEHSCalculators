üìò About This Calculator

The Proportion of Vapor Calculator estimates the fraction of a liquid that transforms into vapor in a two-phase discharge scenario. It is designed for engineers, process operators, and safety professionals to evaluate phase changes in steam systems, pressure-relieving devices, and other industrial processes.

By analyzing energy changes before and after expansion, this calculator helps users understand fluid behavior, optimize system efficiency, ensure safety, and prevent equipment damage.

Key applications include:

  • Design and sizing of safety and relief valves.
  • Evaluation of boiler, turbine, or refrigeration system performance.
  • Predicting and mitigating risks of flashing, cavitation, or two-phase flow in pipelines.
  • Improving energy efficiency by understanding phase change dynamics.

üìù Input Validation Rules

  • hf1: Positive number (>0) and greater than hf2.
  • hf2: Positive number (< hf1) to ensure energy decreases during expansion.
  • hfg2: Positive number (>0) to avoid division by zero and ensure meaningful calculation.
  • Resulting n: Must be between 0 and 1; values outside indicate inconsistent inputs.
  • Extreme Value Checks: hf1 > 5000 kJ/kg or hfg2 > 3000 kJ/kg should trigger verification of units and fluid type.
  • Cross-field consistency: hf1 > hf2; all values >0; ensures compliance with thermodynamic principles and QEHS standards.

üìä Output Validations

  • n = 0: Fully liquid; no vapor formed. Safe operation.
  • 0 < n < 1: Two-phase mixture; partial vaporization. Normal in controlled expansions.
  • n = 1: Fully vaporized; all liquid converted to vapor. Verify equipment limits.
  • n < 0 or n > 1: Physically unrealistic; check input values and units.

üìè Governing Principle

The Proportion of Vapor in Two-Phase Discharge Calculator is based on the API Standard 520, Part I and API Standard 521 / ISO 23251. These standards provide methods for sizing pressure-relief valves for two-phase (liquid and vapor) flows, which is a common application for this type of calculation.

The calculation is generally performed to determine the vapor relief load on a pressure safety valve (PSV) in situations where both liquid and vapor are present. This is particularly important for vessels that may contain a mixture of phases under fire or other overpressure scenarios.

  • API 520 / 521 Annex C: Sizing for Two-Phase Liquid/Vapor Relief, including flashing or non-flashing flow and sub-cooled liquid at the inlet.
  • Omega Method: Used for sizing two-phase flashing or non-flashing flow when the vapor mass fraction is known or needs to be calculated.
  • Homogeneous Equilibrium Model (HEM) & Homogeneous Non-Equilibrium Model (HNE): Additional modeling approaches for predicting two-phase flow through safety valves.
  • DIERS (Design Institute for Emergency Relief Systems) Method: Recognized method for calculating two-phase flow, referenced in technical literature.
  • Simulation Software: Dynamic simulation using tools like Aspen HYSYS can provide more accurate results, accounting for real gas effects if API assumptions are insufficient.
  • Other Standards: ASME and ISO 4126 provide additional guidelines for designing safety devices and pressure relief valves.
