üìê Governing Principle

A Volumetric Flow Rate of a Liquid Through an Orifice Calculator typically follows the standards set by ISO 5167, particularly ISO 5167-1 for general principles and ISO 5167-2 for orifice plates. These standards provide methodology and coefficients for accurate flow measurement using pressure differential devices. Other references include AGA 3 for natural gas and ASME MFC-14M for precision orifice meters.

Key Standards and Documents:

  • ISO 5167: Defines primary flow measurement devices (orifice plates, Venturi tubes, nozzles) for circular pipes.
  • ISO 5167-1: Describes general principles and installation requirements for pressure differential systems.
  • ISO 5167-2: Specifies dimensions and discharge coefficients for orifice plates.
  • AGA 3: Covers flow measurement for gases using differential pressure across orifice plates.
  • ASME MFC-14M: Provides precision measurement criteria for small orifice flow meters.

What These Standards Provide:

  • Geometry and Installation: Shape and placement requirements for accurate measurement.
  • Flow Equation: Bernoulli-based equations to determine volumetric flow.
  • Coefficients: Discharge (Cd) and flow (C) coefficients accounting for real-world losses.
  • Uncertainty: Defined error bounds and repeatability for orifice-based flow measurement.
