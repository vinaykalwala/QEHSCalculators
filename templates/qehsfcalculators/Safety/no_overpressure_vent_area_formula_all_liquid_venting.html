📘 About This Calculator

The No-Overpressure Vent Area Calculator determines the required vent area for liquid storage tanks to prevent overpressure conditions during fire exposure or process upsets. It ensures compliance with international safety standards such as API 2000 and ISO 28300 for atmospheric and low-pressure storage systems.

This tool helps engineers size vent valves accurately, ensuring the tank’s internal pressure does not exceed its Maximum Allowable Working Pressure (MAWP) while maintaining safe and efficient operation.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Heat Input (QT): Constant external heat input to the tank (kW).
  • Liquid Specific Volume (vfa): Volume occupied by the liquid per unit mass (m³/kg).
  • Mass Flux (G): Mass flow rate per unit vent area (kg/m²·s).
  • Vapor Specific Volume (vf): Volume of vapor per unit mass (m³/kg).
  • Latent Heat of Vaporization (hfa): Energy required to convert liquid into vapor (kJ/kg).

📝 Input Validation Rules

  • Heat Input (QT): Must be > 0 kW. Typical range: 1–100,000 kW. Warn if > 100,000 kW.
  • Liquid Specific Volume (vfa): Must be > 0 m³/kg. Typical: 0.0008–0.002. Warn if > 0.1.
  • Mass Flux (G): Must be > 0 kg/m²·s. Typical: 100–10,000. Warn if > 50,000.
  • Vapor Specific Volume (vf): Must be > 0 m³/kg. Typical: 0.5–10. Warn if > 100.
  • Latent Heat (hfa): Must be > 0 kJ/kg. Typical: 200–2,500. Warn if > 5,000.
  • Division by Zero Check: Ensure G × vf × hfa ≠ 0 to prevent calculation errors.

📊 Output Validations

  • Vent Area < 0.001 m² → ❌ Too small, risk of undersized venting (non-compliant per API/NFPA).
  • 0.001 ≤ Vent Area < 0.01 m² → ✅ Acceptable for low-risk or lab-scale applications.
  • 0.01 ≤ Vent Area < 0.1 m² → ⚙️ Optimal range for standard industrial tanks.
  • 0.1 ≤ Vent Area < 1.0 m² → ⚠️ Large vent area, verify heat input and tank parameters.
  • Vent Area ≥ 1.0 m² → 🚨 Excessive vent size, likely unrealistic — recheck all inputs.

📏 Governing Principle

The No-Overpressure Vent Area Formula for all-liquid venting is based on API Standard 2000: “Venting Atmospheric and Low-Pressure Storage Tanks.” This standard provides guidance for sizing vent devices to prevent pressure or vacuum damage in storage tanks under both normal and emergency conditions.

It is harmonized with ISO 28300, which governs similar venting applications in the petroleum, petrochemical, and natural gas industries.

Key Venting Scenarios:

  • Normal Venting: Covers filling (outbreathing) and emptying (inbreathing) during regular operations.
  • Emergency Venting (External Fire): Determines vent size for vapor generated due to heat exposure from fire.

Related Standards:

  • API 521: Pressure-relieving and depressurizing systems (high-pressure equipment).
  • NFPA 30: Flammable and Combustible Liquids Code for aboveground tanks.
  • NFPA 68: Deflagration venting for explosion protection — different from standard pressure venting.

✅ Conclusion

This calculator ensures safe vent design by applying the API 2000 and ISO 28300 no-overpressure formula. By verifying each parameter and validating output ranges, users can design vent systems that prevent catastrophic tank overpressure, ensuring operational safety and regulatory compliance.
