📘 About This Calculator

The Drilling Torque & Drag Calculator helps determine the torque experienced by a drill string during drilling operations. Torque is a critical factor in drill string design, as it affects the efficiency and safety of drilling operations.

❓ What is Torque in Drilling?

Torque is the rotational force experienced by the drill string, measured in foot-pounds-force (ft-lbf). It's a critical parameter that affects drill string design and operational safety.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Force Applied (F): The axial force applied to the drill string, measured in pounds-force (lbf).
  • Radius (r): The distance from the center of rotation to the point where the force is applied, measured in feet.

📊 Interpretation

  • Below 5000 ft-lbf → Safe Torque Level
  • 5000 – 10000 ft-lbf → Moderate Torque - Monitor conditions
  • Above 10000 ft-lbf → High Torque - Risk of equipment failure

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Force Applied (F): -1,000,000 to +1,000,000 lbf. Negative values allowed (reverse torque).
  • Radius (r): Must be >0, realistic range 0.1 – 10 ft (max 50 ft for safety validation).
  • No empty fields: Both force and radius must be provided.
  • Output thresholds: Safe ≤ 5000 ft·lbf, Moderate 5001–10000, High 10001–20000, Critical >20000.
  • Decimals: Force up to 2 decimals, radius up to 3 decimals.
  • Edge cases handled: Zero torque, unrealistic input rejection, division by zero prevention.
