📘 What are Total Loss Hours?

**Total Loss Hours** is a metric that quantifies the direct impact of workplace safety incidents on productivity. It calculates the cumulative production time lost due to injuries, equipment damage, or other safety-related disruptions. Unlike frequency rates, which measure *how often* incidents occur, this metric measures *how severe* their impact is on operations.

⚙️ Formula Used

The calculation is a straightforward multiplication of the number of incidents by the average time lost for each one.

$$ \text{Total Loss Hours} = \text{Number of Incidents} \times \text{Avg. Lost Hours per Incident} $$

💡 Why Track This Metric?

  • Financial Impact: It directly translates safety failures into a tangible productivity loss, highlighting the financial cost of incidents.
  • Resource Allocation: High loss hours can justify investments in better safety equipment, training, or procedures.
  • Operational Efficiency: It provides a clear view of how safety performance affects production schedules and efficiency goals. A low number indicates a resilient and safe operation.
