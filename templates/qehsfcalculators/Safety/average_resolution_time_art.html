📘 About This Calculator

The Average Resolution Time (ART) Calculator helps evaluate team efficiency by measuring the average time taken to resolve issues. It is widely used by support teams, IT departments, and service organizations to benchmark and improve performance.

❓ What is Average Resolution Time?

Average Resolution Time is a performance metric that shows how quickly a team resolves issues or completes tasks from initiation to completion.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Total Resolution Time: The cumulative time spent resolving all issues (in hours).
  • Number of Issues Resolved: The total count of issues successfully resolved.

📊 Interpretation

Lower Average Resolution Times = more efficient teams. Generally:

  • Below 2 hours → Excellent performance
  • 2 – 8 hours → Acceptable but can improve
  • Above 8 hours → Needs process review

🚀 Applications

  • Customer Support: Measuring how quickly support tickets are resolved.
  • IT Departments: Tracking incident resolution efficiency.
  • Project Management: Monitoring task completion rates.
  • Service Industries: Evaluating service delivery performance.

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Total Resolution Time (hrs): 0.01 – 8760 hrs, up to 2 decimals, must be numeric.
  • Number of Issues: 1 – 10,000, whole number only.
  • Cross-Check: Total time must be realistic for the number of issues (≥ 0.01 hr/issue).
  • Edge Cases: Prevent division by zero, reject negative or blank inputs, reject illogical combinations.

Note: These validations align with QEHS, OSHA, and ISO 45001 best practices for KPI reporting.
