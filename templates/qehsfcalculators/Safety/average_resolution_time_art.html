üß≠ Governing Principle

An Average Resolution Time (ART) Calculator does not strictly adhere to a single, universally defined standard but is a key performance indicator (KPI) for customer service that measures the time to resolve customer issues. Standards for ART are established through internal goals, Service Level Agreements (SLAs), or industry-specific benchmarks. These benchmarks vary significantly by factors like the industry, the communication channel (e.g., phone, email, chat), and the complexity of the customer query.

  • Internal Goals and SLAs: Companies set their own target ARTs based on their operational capacity and customer expectations. These become the internal standard for measuring success.
  • Industry Benchmarks: While there‚Äôs no single overarching standard, various sources publish industry-specific benchmarks for ART. For example, benchmarks may range from under 5 minutes for some sectors to 24 hours or more for others.
  • Communication Channel Benchmarks: ART differs significantly across channels. For instance, a good ART for a live chat might be around 5‚Äì10 minutes, while self-service resolutions are instant, and email resolutions may take up to 24 hours.
  • Customer Expectations: What is considered a ‚Äúgood‚Äù resolution time is also influenced by what customers expect. Complex issues might require longer, but customers still expect a swift resolution for most queries.

How to use this information:

  • Define your context: Understand the specific industry and the channels your team uses.
  • Set internal targets: Use your internal context to set realistic ART goals and SLAs.
  • Compare against industry averages: Use benchmarks to see how your ART compares and where to focus improvements.
  • Categorize issues: Segment ART by issue complexity (e.g., simple vs. complex) to gain deeper insights into performance.

üèÅ Conclusion

The Average Resolution Time (ART) is a vital KPI for measuring the responsiveness and efficiency of a support or service team.
