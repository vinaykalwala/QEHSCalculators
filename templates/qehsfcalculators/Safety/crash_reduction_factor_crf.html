📘 About This Calculator

The Crash Reduction Factor (CRF) is a key metric in road safety management that quantifies the percentage reduction in crashes after implementing a safety intervention. It helps evaluate the effectiveness of measures like speed limits, road redesign, or traffic signals.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Crashes Before Intervention: The total number of crashes recorded before implementing any safety measure.
  • Crashes After Intervention: The total number of crashes recorded after the safety measure has been applied.

📊 Interpretation

The Crash Reduction Factor is expressed as a percentage, where:

  • Below 20% → Slight reduction; consider revising the intervention
  • 20% - 50% → Moderate reduction; intervention is effective
  • Above 50% → Excellent reduction; highly effective intervention

🔍 Real-Life Applications

The CRF value is essential in various safety applications:

  • Road Safety Management: Evaluating the effectiveness of traffic calming measures
  • Infrastructure Planning: Assessing the impact of road redesign projects
  • Policy Development: Informing evidence-based road safety policies
  • Resource Allocation: Prioritizing cost-effective safety improvements

✅ Input Validation Rules (CRF Calculator)

  • Crashes Before: Must be an integer ≥ 1 and ≤ 100,000.
  • Crashes After: Must be an integer ≥ 0 and ≤ 100,000.
  • Crashes After ≤ Crashes Before: Post-intervention crashes cannot exceed pre-intervention crashes.
  • No Zero Baseline: Crashes Before cannot be 0 (avoids invalid division).
  • No Negative Values: Crash counts must always be non-negative.
  • Identical Values: If Before = After, CRF = 0% (no reduction).

Note: These rules follow Road Safety Audit standards (FHWA, AASHTO, ISO 39001).
