üìò About This Calculator

The Crane Capacity Index (CCI) is a measure of crane performance based on key operational parameters. It helps evaluate a crane's capability by combining radius, lifting height, and capacity into a single value.

‚öôÔ∏è Parameters Used

  • Radius: The horizontal distance from the crane's center to the load. A larger radius means the crane has to work harder to lift the load efficiently.
  • Lifting Height: The vertical height the load is lifted. This affects the crane's stability and performance as greater heights may require adjustments in lifting technique.
  • Lifting Capacity: The maximum weight the crane can lift under specific conditions. This depends on the crane model, counterweights, and boom configuration.

üìä Interpretation

CCI Performance Ranges:

  • CCI < 50 ‚Üí Limited capability - Suitable for light-duty operations only
  • CCI 50-150 ‚Üí Standard capability - Appropriate for general construction tasks
  • CCI 150-300 ‚Üí Good capability - Suitable for heavy lifting operations
  • CCI > 300 ‚Üí Excellent capability - Ideal for specialized heavy lifting projects

üîç Real-Life Applications

The Crane Capacity Index is essential in:

  • Construction Planning: Determining which crane is appropriate for specific lifting tasks.
  • Safety Assessments: Ensuring cranes operate within their safe working limits.
  • Equipment Selection: Choosing the right crane for projects based on required reach and capacity.
  • Operational Efficiency: Optimizing crane utilization on job sites.
