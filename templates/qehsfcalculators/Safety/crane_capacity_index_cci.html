📘 About This Calculator

The Crane Capacity Index (CCI) Calculator provides a standardized metric to evaluate and compare the overall capability of different cranes. By combining the key operational parameters of radius, lifting height, and capacity into a single, unified number, it offers a straightforward way to assess a crane's potential performance for various lifting tasks.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Radius (m): The horizontal distance from the crane's center of rotation to the load. A larger radius generally reduces the crane's effective lifting capacity.
  • Lifting Height (m): The vertical distance from the ground to the load. Greater heights can impact stability and require careful planning.
  • Lifting Capacity (Te): The maximum weight (in tonnes) the crane can safely lift at a given radius and height, as specified by the manufacturer's load chart.

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Radius: Must be a positive number, typically not exceeding 100 meters for standard cranes.
  • Lifting Height: Must be a positive number, generally within a practical limit of 200 meters.
  • Lifting Capacity: Must be a positive number, with an upper limit of 1000 tonnes to ensure realistic inputs.
  • Cross-Validation: The calculator flags combinations of very high capacity with extreme radius or height, as these scenarios are often unrealistic for standard equipment and may indicate an input error.

Output Validations

The performance assessment is based on the calculated CCI value, providing a quick reference for the crane's suitability for a task:

  • Limited capability: For CCI < 50, suitable for light-duty operations only.
  • Standard capability: For CCI 50-150, appropriate for general construction tasks.
  • Good capability: For CCI 150-300, suitable for heavy lifting operations.
  • Excellent capability: For CCI > 300, ideal for specialized heavy lifting projects.

Governing Principle

The Crane Capacity Index (CCI) is not based on a single official standard but is a concept developed and used by organizations like ESTA (European Association of Abnormal-Load Transport Services) to evaluate a crane's capability. It's calculated by averaging the product of a crane's radius, lifting height, and capacity, then dividing by 100 to give a unified value for comparison.

It’s a tool to provide a standard way to evaluate the general capability of different crane models by combining three key parameters into a single number. This unified value helps in comparing cranes and understanding their potential.

Conclusion

The Crane Capacity Index is a valuable tool for initial planning, equipment selection, and high-level comparisons. It provides a quick assessment of a crane's overall potential. However, it is not a substitute for detailed lift planning, which must always be based on the manufacturer's specific load charts, ground conditions, and adherence to safety standards such as OSHA and ASME B30.5.
