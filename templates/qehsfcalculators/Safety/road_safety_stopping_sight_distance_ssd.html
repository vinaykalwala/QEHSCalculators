📘 About This Calculator

The Stopping Sight Distance (SSD) Calculator determines the minimum distance required for a vehicle to stop safely, considering driver perception–reaction time and braking capability. It is essential for road design, traffic safety audits, and accident prevention.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • Speed (v): Vehicle speed in meters per second (m/s).
  • Perception–Reaction Time (t): Driver response time in seconds.
  • Coefficient of Friction (f): Resistance between tires and road surface (dimensionless).

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Speed: 0 < v ≤ 55.6 m/s (200 km/h), decimals ≤1.
  • Reaction Time: 0.5 ≤ t ≤ 5.0 seconds, decimals ≤1.
  • Friction Coefficient: 0.1 ≤ f ≤ 1.0, decimals ≤2.
  • No blanks, negatives, or unrealistic combinations allowed.
  • Division by zero (f = 0) is prevented.

📊 Output Validations

  • SSD < 50 m → ✅ Good stopping distance - suitable for urban roads with lower speed limits.
  • 50 ≤ SSD < 100 m → 🔵 Moderate stopping distance - typical for suburban roads and highways.
  • 100 ≤ SSD < 200 m → ⚠️ High stopping distance - requires careful road design and signage.
  • SSD ≥ 200 m → ❌ Very high stopping distance - may require special safety measures like warning signs or reduced speed limits.

Note: SSD results above 2000 m are flagged as unrealistic for compliance review.

📏 Standards

Calculations and thresholds align with: • AASHTO (American Association of State Highway and Transportation Officials) • IRC (Indian Roads Congress) • International Road Assessment Programme (iRAP) • ISO 45001 safety management requirements for traffic systems.

📝 Conclusion

The Stopping Sight Distance (SSD) Calculator is a reliable tool to evaluate whether a driver has sufficient distance to stop safely under given road and vehicle conditions. By combining perception–reaction distance and braking distance, it reflects real-world stopping behavior based on AASHTO, IRC, and iRAP standards.
