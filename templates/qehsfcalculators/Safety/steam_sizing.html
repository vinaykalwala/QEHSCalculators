üìò About Steam Relief Sizing

Properly sizing a steam safety relief valve is critical for preventing catastrophic overpressure events in boilers and pressure vessels. This calculation determines the minimum required orifice area of the valve to safely discharge a specific mass flow rate of steam at a given pressure. Undersizing can lead to vessel rupture, while significant oversizing can cause valve instability (chatter). This tool uses an ASME-approved formula for steam service.

‚öôÔ∏è Formula Used

The required orifice area (A) is calculated as:

$$ A = \frac{W}{51.5 \times P \times K_d \times K_n} $$

  • $A$ = Required Orifice Area (m¬≤)
  • $W$ = Mass Flow Rate (kg/hr)
  • $P$ = Relief Pressure (Set Pressure + Overpressure) (kPa)
  • $K_d$ = Discharge Coefficient (typically 0.975 for steam)
  • $K_n$ = Napier Correction Factor for superheated steam (1.0 for saturated steam)
