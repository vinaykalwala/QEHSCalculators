📘 About This Calculator

The Critical Pressure Calculator helps determine the critical pressure (Pd) which is crucial for analyzing gas and vapor flow behavior in pipelines, safety valves, and industrial systems.

❓ What is Critical Pressure?

Critical Pressure is the threshold pressure below which gas flow becomes critical (choked) and follows a different flow regime.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Upstream Pressure (Puf): The pressure before the restriction or orifice where gas flow begins.
  • Specific Heat Ratio (k): The ratio of specific heats (Cp/Cv), which characterizes gas behavior under expansion.

📊 Interpretation

  • Below 50,000 Pa → Low critical pressure. The system is stable under normal conditions.
  • 50,000 – 200,000 Pa → Moderate critical pressure. Monitor system parameters closely.
  • Above 200,000 Pa → High critical pressure! System may require pressure relief measures.

🔧 Applications

  • Industrial Safety: Sizing safety relief valves in high-pressure systems.
  • Petrochemical Engineering: Ensuring controlled gas expansion in pipelines.
  • Aerospace Engineering: Designing nozzles for jet engines and rockets.
  • HVAC Systems: Managing pressure differentials in ventilation systems.
  • Gas Storage & Transport: Regulating compressed gas tanks and distribution networks.

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Upstream Pressure (Puf): Must be between 1,000 Pa and 100,000,000 Pa.
  • Specific Heat Ratio (k): Must be between 1.01 and 2.0. Typical values: Air (1.4), Steam (1.3).
  • No Negative Values: Inputs must be positive and realistic.
  • Cross-Check: Calculated Critical Pressure (Pd) must always be less than Upstream Pressure (Puf).
  • Units: Pressure in Pascals (Pa), k is dimensionless.
