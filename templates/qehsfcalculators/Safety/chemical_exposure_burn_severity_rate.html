üìó Governing Principle

A Chemical Exposure Burn Severity Rate (CEBSR) Calculator isn't based on a single universal standard but generally uses established principles for calculating workplace injury severity rates and incorporates principles from standards like the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) IS 3786, as well as general safety guidelines.

  • Severity Rate Calculation: The core principle of a CEBSR is the calculation of a severity rate, which is a standardized formula used to measure the impact of workplace injuries.
  • Bureau of Indian Standards (IS 3786): The BIS standard provides a methodology for computing frequency and severity rates for industrial injuries. The CEBSR methodology aligns with these principles, especially for the Indian context.
  • General Safety Principles: CEBSR calculators draw from best practices for identifying, classifying, and quantifying chemical risks in workplaces.
  • OSHA Standards: In the U.S., similar safety principles are guided by OSHA regulations. While CEBSR itself isn't a regulatory metric, it serves as a tool for tracking and improving safety performance.
