📘 About This Calculator

The Orifice Plate Beta Ratio Calculator determines the ratio of orifice diameter to pipe diameter, which is crucial for understanding flow characteristics and pressure drop in fluid systems.

❓ What is Beta Ratio?

Beta Ratio (β) is defined as the ratio of the orifice diameter (d) to the pipe diameter (D). It helps determine the flow characteristics of a fluid through an orifice plate.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Orifice Diameter (d): The inner diameter of the orifice plate
  • Pipe Diameter (D): The inner diameter of the pipe

📊 Interpretation

Typical Beta Ratio Values:

  • 0.2 - 0.5 → Lower pressure drop, higher flow capacity
  • 0.5 - 0.7 → Balanced performance, common range
  • Above 0.7 → Higher pressure drop, potential for cavitation

🌍 Real-Life Applications

  • Fire Safety - Measuring water flow in fire suppression systems
  • Industrial Flow Measurement - Ensuring proper fluid distribution in pipelines
  • HVAC Systems - Regulating air and fluid flow in ventilation systems

📋 Important Condition

The orifice diameter (d) must always be smaller than the pipe diameter (D). If d ≥ D, the calculation is invalid as no fluid would flow through because there would be no open space left.

✅ Validation Rules

  • Orifice Diameter (d): Must be 0.001 – 10 m, positive, numeric, and strictly less than pipe diameter.
  • Pipe Diameter (D): Must be 0.001 – 10 m, positive, numeric.
  • Cross-check: Orifice diameter (d) must always be smaller than pipe diameter (D).
  • Units: Both values must be consistent (e.g., meters or inches).
  • Edge Cases: d ≥ D or D = 0 → invalid; negative/blank inputs not allowed.
