Summary

About Volumetric Flow Rate

The volumetric flow rate through a valve is essential for designing and managing fluid systems. It determines the volume of a fluid passing through the valve per unit of time, based on the valve's specific flow coefficient (Kv) and the pressure drop across it.

Parameters Explained

  • Flow Coefficient (Kv): A standard value indicating the volume of water (in m³/h) that will pass through a valve with a 1-bar pressure drop. It is a key measure of a valve's capacity.
  • Pressure Drop (ΔP): The difference in fluid pressure between the inlet and outlet of the valve, measured in bars.

✅ Validation Rules

  • Flow Coefficient (Kv): Must be a positive number. A value of 0 would mean the valve is closed.
  • Pressure Drop (ΔP): Must be a non-negative number. A negative pressure drop is not physically possible.

Applications

This calculation is widely used in industrial pipelines (oil, gas, chemical), HVAC systems, fire suppression systems (sprinklers), and water treatment plants to ensure proper fluid control, safety, and efficiency.
