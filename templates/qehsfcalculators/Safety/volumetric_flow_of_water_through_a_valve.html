Governing Principle

The calculation of volumetric flow rate using a flow coefficient (Kv) is defined by international standards to ensure consistency and comparability across different valve manufacturers and applications. The primary standards governing this calculation are:

  • IEC 60534: This is the key international standard for industrial-process control valves. It specifies the testing procedures and formulas for determining valve flow coefficients (Kv).
  • VDI/VDE 2173: A German standard that provides similar definitions and methodologies for flow coefficients, which are widely recognized in Europe.

These standards establish that Kv is the flow rate of water in cubic meters per hour (m¬≥/h) at a temperature of 5-30¬∞C that passes through a valve with a pressure drop of 1 bar. The calculation performed by this tool directly adheres to this standardized definition.

Conclusion

Calculating the volumetric flow rate is a fundamental task in fluid system design. By using the standardized Kv value, engineers can accurately select the right valve for a specific application, ensuring optimal performance, energy efficiency, and system safety.
