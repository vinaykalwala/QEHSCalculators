📘 About This Calculator

This calculator determines the minimum flow area (A₀) for dry gases and air in safety valves according to AD-Merkblatt standards. It ensures that gases are vented safely without causing dangerous pressure buildup.

❓ What is AD-Merkblatt?

AD-Merkblatt (Arbeitsgemeinschaft Druckbehälter) is a German standard that provides guidelines for the design, construction, and testing of pressure vessels to ensure safety and compliance with European regulations.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Mass Flow Rate (ṁ): The mass of gas flowing per unit time (kg/h).
  • Outflow Function (Ψ): Dimensionless parameter representing flow efficiency.
  • Outflow Coefficient (αw): Efficiency factor for the valve.
  • Absolute Relieving Pressure (PR): Pressure at which the valve operates (bar a).
  • Inlet Temperature (T): Temperature of the gas entering the valve (K).
  • Molar Mass (M): Mass of one mole of the gas (kg/kmol).
  • Compressibility Factor (Z): Accounts for real gas behavior deviations from ideal gas law.

📊 Interpretation

Flow Area Values indicate the size requirement for safe pressure relief:

  • Below 100 mm² → Small flow area required
  • 100 – 500 mm² → Moderate flow area required
  • Above 500 mm² → Large flow area required

🔬 Real-Life Applications

  • Used in industrial safety valves to prevent overpressure in boilers and pipelines.
  • Critical for fire protection systems to ensure safe steam release in emergencies.
  • Applied in environmental compliance to help industries regulate gas emissions.
  • Essential for chemical processing plants to maintain safe operating conditions.

🔢 Constant Values

  • 0.1791: A constant derived from thermodynamic principles, used to normalize units and account for standard gas flow conditions.
