📘 About This Calculator

The Critical Pressure Ratio determines whether the gas flow through a nozzle is choked (reaches maximum velocity). This helps in analyzing gas flow in pipelines, nozzles, and relief systems.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Pb (Critical Backpressure): The pressure downstream of the nozzle, determining whether flow is choked.
  • P1 (Actual Relieving Pressure): The initial pressure at which gas or vapor is being released.
  • k (Isentropic Coefficient): A property of gases affecting their expansion and compression.

📊 Interpretation

Flow Status:

  • If Actual Ratio (Pb/P1) < Calculated Ratio → Flow is choked
  • If Actual Ratio (Pb/P1) ≥ Calculated Ratio → Flow is not choked

🔍 Real-Life Applications

The critical pressure ratio is essential in:

  • Rocket Nozzles: Determines exhaust velocity and performance.
  • Gas Pipeline Flow: Ensures proper pressure control in gas distribution.
  • Safety Valves: Used in pressure relief calculations to prevent explosions.
  • Aircraft Engines: Helps in optimizing jet propulsion.

✅ Input Validation Rules (Engineering Compliance)

  • Pb (Critical Backpressure): > 0 bar, ≤ 500 bar, and must be less than P1.
  • P1 (Actual Relieving Pressure): > 0 bar, ≤ 1000 bar, and must be greater than Pb.
  • k (Isentropic Coefficient): 1.0 < k ≤ 2.0 (real gases range, e.g., air ~1.4).
  • No negative values: Pressures and k must always be positive.
  • Cross-check: Backpressure cannot exceed upstream pressure; otherwise, no valid flow exists.
  • Ratios: Both Actual Ratio and Critical Ratio must be between 0 and 1.

Compliance Note: These validations align with API RP 520, ASME Section VIII, NFPA 68/69 for gas flow and pressure relief design.
