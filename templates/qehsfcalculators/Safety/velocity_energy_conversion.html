üìò About This Calculator

This tool calculates an object's final velocity by applying the principle of energy conservation, where an object's Potential Energy at a certain height is converted entirely into Kinetic Energy just before it impacts the ground.

‚öôÔ∏è Formula Used

The calculation is based on equating Potential Energy (PE) and Kinetic Energy (KE):

$PE = KE$

$mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$

By simplifying, we can solve for velocity (v):

$v = \sqrt{2gh}$

‚ùì Parameters Explained

  • Mass (m): The amount of matter in the object, measured in kilograms (kg).
  • Height (h): The vertical distance from which the object is dropped, in meters (m).
  • Gravity (g): A constant value of $9.81 \, m/s^2$ is used for acceleration due to gravity.
  • Velocity (v): The final speed of the object just before impact, in meters per second (m/s).

‚úÖ Input Validation Rules

  • Mass (m): Must be a numeric value greater than 0.
  • Height (h): Must be a numeric value greater than 0.

Note: While mass is required to calculate energy (in Joules), it cancels out when solving for final velocity, which depends only on height and gravity.
