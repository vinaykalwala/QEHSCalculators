The OSHA DART Rate Calculator measures workplace safety by calculating the rate of incidents resulting in days away from work, restricted duty, or job transfer per 200,000 hours worked.

‚ùì What is OSHA DART Rate?

The DART (Days Away, Restricted, or Transferred) rate is a safety metric used by OSHA to track workplace injuries and illnesses that result in employees being away from work, on restricted duty, or transferred to another job.

  • Number of DART Incidents: Total recordable cases that resulted in days away from work, restricted activity, or job transfer
  • Total Employee Hours Worked: Total hours worked by all employees during the period

üìä Interpretation

Lower DART Rates = safer workplace conditions. Generally:

  • ‚â§ 1.0 ‚Üí Good safety performance
  • 1.1 - 3.0 ‚Üí Acceptable but needs improvement
  • > 3.0 ‚Üí Requires immediate safety interventions

üåç Importance of DART Rate

  • Regulatory Compliance - Required by OSHA for many employers
  • Benchmarking - Compare safety performance across industries
  • Safety Program Evaluation - Measure effectiveness of safety initiatives
  • Risk Management - Identify areas needing safety improvements
  • Insurance Premiums - Can affect workers' compensation costs

üìã OSHA Standards

OSHA uses the DART rate to identify workplaces with higher-than-average injury rates. Employers with DART rates above the national average for their industry may be targeted for inspections.
