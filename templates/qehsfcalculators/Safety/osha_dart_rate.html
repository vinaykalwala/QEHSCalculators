📘 About This Calculator

The OSHA DART Rate Calculator measures workplace safety by calculating the rate of incidents resulting in days away from work, restricted duty, or job transfer per 200,000 hours worked.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Number of DART Incidents: Total recordable cases that resulted in days away from work, restricted duty, or job transfer
  • Total Employee Hours Worked: Total hours worked by all employees during the period

📝 Input Validation Rules

  • DART Incidents: Integer only, 0 – 10,000; no decimals; cannot be negative.
  • Employee Hours: Integer only, 1 – 10,000,000,000; no decimals; cannot be zero.
  • Cross-field check: High incidents with very low hours will be flagged as unrealistic.

📊 Output Validations

  • ≤ 1.0 → Excellent safety performance
  • 1.1 – 3.0 → Acceptable performance; review safety measures
  • > 3.0 → Needs immediate improvement; investigate and act
  • Output is always expressed per 200,000 employee hours worked

📏 Governing Principle

The OSHA DART (Days Away, Restricted, or Transferred) rate uses a standard formula to normalize incident rates for 100 full-time employees. The base of 200,000 hours represents 100 employees working 40 hours/week for 50 weeks/year.

  • Formula: (Number of DART Cases × 200,000) ÷ Total Employee Hours Worked
  • Purpose: Provides a comparable incident rate across industries and companies
  • Includes: Injuries or illnesses that result in days away from work, restricted duties, or job transfers
  • Usage: Track safety program effectiveness, identify trends, and implement preventive measures

✅ Conclusion

  • Lower DART rates indicate better workplace safety performance.
  • Helps in regulatory compliance, benchmarking, and risk management.
  • Organizations can use it to improve safety programs and reduce incidents.
