Key Organizations and Standards
  • AASHTO (American Association of State Highway and Transportation Officials): This organization's formulas are a standard for calculating stopping distances in the United States, with calculators often using this basis for their calculations.
  • IRC (Indian Road Congress): In India, the IRC sets standards for road design and safety, and their recommendations and formulas are used to calculate stopping distances.
  • PIARC (World Road Association): As an international organization, PIARC also provides guidelines and information on braking distance as part of its Road Safety Manual, which contributes to global road safety standards, as mentioned on their website.
Common Components of Braking Distance Calculations
  • Lag Distance: The distance traveled during the driver's perception-reaction time.
  • Braking Distance: The distance the vehicle travels from the moment the brakes are applied until it comes to a complete stop.
  • Perception-Reaction Time (t): The time it takes for a driver to perceive a hazard and react to it.
  • Speed (v): The initial speed of the vehicle before braking begins.
  • Coefficient of Friction (f): A factor that represents the friction between the tires and the road surface.
  • Grade (G): The slope of the road, which affects braking distance (e.g., longer distance on downgrades).
