‚öñ Governing Principle

There isn't a single, globally recognized standard for kick tolerance calculators. Instead, it is a concept addressed by various industry standards, company guidelines, and proprietary software. Kick tolerance is generally defined as the maximum volume of influx that can be safely circulated out of a well without fracturing the formation at the casing shoe.

Key Standards and Organizations

  • NORSOK Standard D-010: A Norwegian standard that defines kick tolerance and is commonly referenced for well barrier integrity.
  • UK Oil and Gas Guidelines: Industry guidelines that incorporate kick tolerance calculations into well design and drilling operations.
  • NOGEPA Industry Standard No. 50: Netherlands-based standard for kick tolerance in oil and gas exploration and production.
  • Company-Specific Standards: Many oil and gas companies develop internal technical standards for kick tolerance, often implemented using proprietary software or spreadsheets.

In essence, a kick tolerance calculator estimates the maximum safe influx volume for a well based on these standards, engineering judgment, and company-specific procedures.
