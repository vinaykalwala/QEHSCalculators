📘 About This Calculator

The Severity Rate Calculator measures the seriousness of workplace injuries by comparing lost workdays to total hours worked. It standardizes results using OSHA/ANSI methodology for benchmarking and compliance.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • Lost Workdays: Total number of days employees could not work due to occupational injuries/illnesses.
  • Total Hours Worked: All hours worked by all employees during the reporting period.

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Lost Workdays: Integer, 0–10,000 (no decimals, cannot be negative).
  • Total Hours Worked: Integer, 1–100,000,000 (must be greater than 0).
  • Logical Consistency: Lost workdays × 8 hours ≤ Total Hours Worked.
  • Units: Whole counts only (days and hours, no fractions).

📊 Output Validations

  • Severity Rate < 100: ✅ Low Severity Rate – indicates fewer lost workdays relative to hours worked.
  • 100 ≤ Severity Rate < 300: 🔵 Moderate Severity Rate – suggests an average level of lost workdays.
  • Severity Rate ≥ 300: ⚠️ High Severity Rate – indicates a higher number of lost workdays, which may suggest safety or operational issues.

The rate is expressed as lost days per 200,000 hours worked for standardized OSHA comparison.

🧭 Governing Principle

A Severity Rate Calculator for industrial injuries typically adheres to standards like the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) IS 3786. This standard provides the methods for computing frequency and severity rates, classifying industrial accidents, and defining what constitutes a severity rate. The standard helps ensure that the calculation of days lost due to injuries is uniform and comparable across different industrial settings.

What is IS 3786?

  • Purpose: The Indian Standard IS 3786 (currently with a 2022 revision, though the 1983 version is widely referenced) outlines basic methods for recording, classifying, and computing industrial injury rates.
  • Key Components: It defines the formulas for calculating Frequency Rate, Severity Rate, and Incidence Rate.
  • Severity Rate (SR) Calculation: The standard provides the formula for Severity Rate, which measures the number of lost days per 1,000,000 man-hours worked:

SR = (Man-days lost due to reportable lost time injury × 1,000,000) / (Man-hours worked)

Why is a standard important?

  • Uniformity: Standards like IS 3786 promote a standardized approach to safety data collection and analysis.
  • Comparability: They allow for consistent comparison of safety performance between different companies and industries.
  • Insight: A standard helps to accurately assess safety performance and identify areas for improvement by providing consistent metrics.

📝 Conclusion

The Severity Rate Calculator provides a standardized way to measure the seriousness of occupational injuries by relating lost workdays to total hours worked. This KPI helps organizations identify the impact of workplace incidents on productivity and safety performance.

While the calculator follows OSHA, ANSI Z16.1, ISO 45001, and BIS IS 3786 guidelines, results should always be interpreted alongside other safety metrics (e.g., Total Recordable Incident Rate, Near-Miss Frequency) for a holistic view of workplace health and safety.

Final assessments should be validated by qualified safety professionals and compared with industry benchmarks to ensure compliance and drive continuous improvement in occupational health and safety programs.
