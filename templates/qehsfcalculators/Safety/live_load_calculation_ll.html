📘 About This Calculator

The Live Load Calculator determines the total live load for scaffold platforms based on duty classification and platform area, ensuring structural safety and compliance with industry standards.

❓ What is Live Load?

Live load represents the weight of workers, tools, equipment, and any other movable items placed on the scaffold. It is used to ensure the platform is designed to support the intended weight, maintaining safety and stability.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Load Type: Classification based on the type of work being performed
  • Platform Area: Total area of the scaffold platform in square meters

📊 Duty Classifications

  • Light Duty: 1.2 kN/m² (for workers only, no material storage)
  • Medium Duty: 2.4 kN/m² (for workers and some material storage)
  • Heavy Duty: 3.6 kN/m² (for workers and substantial material storage)

🌍 Applications

  • Construction Safety - Ensuring scaffold platforms can support intended loads
  • Structural Engineering - Calculating load requirements for temporary structures
  • Workplace Safety - Compliance with OSHA and other safety regulations
  • Project Planning - Determining equipment requirements for construction projects

📋 Industry Standards

OSHA 1926.451, ANSI/ASSE A10.8, and other international scaffolding safety standards that specify minimum live load requirements for different types of work.

✅ Validation Rules

  • Platform Area: Must be between 0.1 m² and 1000 m².
  • Decimals: Up to 2 decimal places allowed.
  • Duty Class: Light = 1.2 kN/m², Medium = 2.4 kN/m², Heavy = 3.6 kN/m².
  • Compliance Check: Loads exceeding duty × 1000 m² require engineering approval.
  • Formula: Live Load (kN) = Load Intensity × Platform Area.
  • Conversion: 1 kN ≈ 101.97 kg.
