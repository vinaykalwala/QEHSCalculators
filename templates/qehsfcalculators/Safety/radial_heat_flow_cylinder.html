📘 About This Calculator

The Radial Heat Flow Through Cylinder Calculator determines the steady-state heat transfer rate through cylindrical walls. Essential for pipes, pressure vessels, and containment systems where temperature varies radially.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • Thermal Conductivity (k): Heat conduction property (W/m·K).
  • Cylinder Length (L): Length of pipe/wall (m).
  • Inner Radius (rᵢ): Distance to inside wall (m).
  • Outer Radius (rₒ): Distance to outside wall (m).
  • Temperature Difference (ΔT): Between inner & outer surfaces (K).

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Thermal Conductivity (k): > 0, typical range 0.01–500 W/m·K.
  • Cylinder Length (L): > 0, practical 0.1–100 m.
  • Inner Radius (rᵢ): ≥ 0, recommended 0.01–5 m.
  • Outer Radius (rₒ): > rᵢ, with (rₒ - rᵢ) ≥ 0.001 m.
  • Temperature Difference (ΔT): ≥ 0, typical 0–1000 K.
  • Decimals: up to 3 places for radii, 2 places for k & L.

✅ Output Validations

  • q = 0 W: No heat transfer.
  • q < 10 W: Low heat transfer (Minimal effect).
  • 10 ≤ q ≤ 1000 W: Moderate (Typical systems).
  • 1000 < q ≤ 10000 W: High (Design check needed).
  • q > 10000 W: Very High (Verify inputs/materials).
  • q > 50000 W: Extreme → Engineering review required.

📏 Standards

Based on ASME B31.3 (Process Piping), ASTM C680 (Insulation), ISO 12241 (Thermal insulation), NFPA 80 (Fire barriers).

📝 Conclusion

This tool helps ensure thermal safety and compliance. Accurate validation prevents fire risks, material failures, and unsafe heat loads.
