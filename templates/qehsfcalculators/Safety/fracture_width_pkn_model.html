Fracture Width Assessment

📘 About This Calculator

This tool calculates the Maximum Fracture Width using the Perkins-Kern-Nordgren (PKN) model, which helps estimate the width of hydraulic fractures based on fluid and rock properties.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Pumping Rate (Q): The volume of fracturing fluid being pumped per unit time (barrels/min). A higher pumping rate generally results in larger fractures.
  • Fluid Viscosity (μ): The resistance of the fracturing fluid to flow (cp). A more viscous fluid can carry proppants better and influence fracture propagation.
  • Poisson's Ratio (ν): A material property describing how a rock deforms when compressed. It affects how fractures grow and widen under pressure (dimensionless, 0-0.5).
  • Fracture Half-Length (xf): The distance from the fracture's center to its tip (ft). It determines how far the fracture extends underground.
  • Shear Modulus (G): A measure of the rock's stiffness (psi). A higher shear modulus means the rock resists deformation, leading to narrower fractures.

📊 Interpretation

Fracture Width Guidelines:

  • Below 0.1 inches → Narrow fracture, may not effectively deliver proppant
  • 0.1-0.3 inches → Optimal range for most hydraulic fracturing operations
  • Above 0.3 inches → Wide fracture, may indicate excessive fluid or pressure

🔑 Key Applications

  • Hydraulic Fracturing Design: Determines the appropriate fracture width for optimal proppant placement.
  • Reservoir Engineering: Helps optimize fracture geometry for maximum hydrocarbon recovery.
  • Well Completion Planning: Assists in designing effective completion strategies for unconventional reservoirs.
  • Production Optimization: Ensures fractures are properly sized to maintain conductivity over the well's life.

✅ Validation Rules & Input Constraints

  • Pumping Rate (Q): Must be > 0 and ≤ 200 barrels/min (typical 1–100).
  • Fluid Viscosity (μ): Must be > 0 and ≤ 5000 cp (typical 1–1000).
  • Poisson's Ratio (ν): Must be 0.1–0.5 (typical 0.2–0.3 for rocks).
  • Fracture Half-Length (xf): Must be > 0 and ≤ 10000 ft (typical 100–2000).
  • Shear Modulus (G): Must be > 1e4 and ≤ 1e8 psi (typical 1e5–1e7).
  • Decimals: Up to 4 decimals allowed for accuracy.
  • Output Range: Normal = 0.01–1.0 inches; outside this → warning.
