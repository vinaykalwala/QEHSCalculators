📘 About This Calculator

The Allowable Leakage Calculator helps determine the maximum allowable leakage in a pipeline during pressure testing based on industry standards. It considers pipe length, diameter, test pressure, and optional valve size for comprehensive leakage assessment.

❓ What is Allowable Leakage?

Allowable Leakage is the maximum acceptable amount of fluid loss in a pipeline system during pressure testing, calculated based on system parameters and industry standards.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Length of Pipe Tested: Total length of the pipeline being tested (in feet).
  • Nominal Diameter of Pipe: The diameter of the pipe being tested (in inches).
  • Average Test Pressure: The average pressure applied during the leakage test (in PSI).
  • Nominal Valve Size: Size of closed metal-seated valves if applicable (optional).

📊 Interpretation

Lower Leakage Values indicate better system integrity. Industry standards typically consider:

  • Below 0.5 gph → Excellent system integrity
  • 0.5 – 2.0 gph → Acceptable with minor improvements needed
  • Above 2.0 gph → Requires system review and potential repairs
