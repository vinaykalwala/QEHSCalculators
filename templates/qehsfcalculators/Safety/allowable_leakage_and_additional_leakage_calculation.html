üß≠ Governing Principle

Allowable and additional leakage calculations are most commonly associated with the American Petroleum Institute (API) 598 standard, which details the testing and inspection procedures for various industrial valves, including their acceptable leakage rates. However, similar concepts and leakage classifications are also found in other standards, such as the HVAC-focused SMACNA standards and the broader ISO and MSS standards for industrial valves.

  • API 598: Scope ‚Äì Applies to gate, globe, check, ball, plug, and butterfly valves. Requirements ‚Äì Defines test procedures for shell, seat, and closure testing, with allowable leakage rates for both liquid and gas tests. Flexibility ‚Äì Manufacturers can be requested to perform additional testing for stricter leakage limits (may increase cost).
  • Other Relevant Standards: SMACNA ‚Äì HVAC Air Duct Leakage Test Manual uses a leakage class system. FCI 70-2 ‚Äì Defines valve seat leakage classes (Class I‚ÄìVI). ISO & ANSI ‚Äì Provide general leakage testing standards and align with API requirements. MSS SP-61 ‚Äì Specifies permissible leakage rates for various valve types, especially metal-seated valves.
  • Key Considerations: Valve Type ‚Äì Leakage limits differ for soft- vs. metal-seated valves. Application ‚Äì Acceptable leakage depends on operational purpose (e.g., high-pressure systems vs. HVAC). Purchaser Specifications ‚Äì Buyers may demand stricter leakage limits, which must be defined in contract requirements.
