📘 About This Calculator

The ASME (API RP 520) Minimum Flow Area Calculator determines the minimum required discharge area (A₀) for liquid relief valves. Proper valve sizing is critical to prevent overpressure, ensure safe fluid discharge, and maintain compliance with international safety standards.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • V̇t – Flow rate through the valve (US gal/min)
  • Kd – Coefficient of discharge (0.62–0.98 per API 520)
  • Kv – Viscosity factor (0.9–1.1)
  • Kw – Backpressure correction factor (0.9–1.0)
  • G – Specific gravity of the liquid (0.2–2.0 typical range)
  • PR – Relieving pressure at valve inlet (bar abs)
  • PB – Backpressure (psi abs)

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • V̇t: 1–1,000,000 gpm
  • Kd: 0.62–0.98 (API 520 limits)
  • Kv: 0.9–1.1
  • Kw: 0.9–1.0
  • G: 0.2–2.0
  • PR: >0 and ≤1000 bar a
  • PB: ≥0 psi and < PR (converted to psi)
  • Cross-check: PR must always be greater than PB

📊 Output Validations

The minimum flow area (A₀) determines valve size category. The same assessment feedback shown in the results will also apply here:

  • < 50 mm² → ✅ Small valve required (low-flow applications)
  • 50–200 mm² → 👍 Moderate valve size (general industrial)
  • 200–500 mm² → ⚠️ Medium valve size (process industry)
  • 500–1000 mm² → 📏 Large valve required (critical systems)
  • > 1000 mm² → 🚨 Very large valve needed — review inputs

Edge case: If (PR – PB) ≤ 0, calculation is invalid — backpressure too high relative to relieving pressure.

📏 Standards

This calculator follows ASME Boiler & Pressure Vessel Code and API RP 520 guidelines for liquid relief valve sizing. These standards are widely applied in oil & gas, chemical processing, and fire safety systems to ensure equipment protection and personnel safety.
