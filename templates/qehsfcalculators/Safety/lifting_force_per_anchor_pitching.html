📘 About This Calculator

The Lifting Force per Anchor (Pitching) Calculator is used to determine the lifting force each anchor must bear during the pitching process of precast elements. This calculation is crucial for ensuring safe handling and preventing overloading of anchors.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Half Weight (G/2): Half of the total weight of the element during pitching (in kN)
  • Lifting Coefficient (f): A factor that accounts for dynamic forces during lifting, typically 1.3
  • Spread Angle Factor (z): A factor based on the spread angle of the lifting cables, typically 1.0 for a 0° spread angle
  • Number of Anchors (n): The number of anchors supporting the load, fixed at 2 for this calculation

📊 Key Parameters Table

🌍 Real-Life Applications

  • Precast Factories: Ensuring safe lifting of precast elements during the pitching process
  • Construction Sites: Handling precast elements during installation

⚠️ Importance of Accurate Calculations

Accurate calculation of the lifting force per anchor is crucial for ensuring the safety and structural integrity of pitching operations. Incorrect calculations can lead to:

  • Anchor failure
  • Dropped loads
  • Structural damage
  • Serious injuries or fatalities

✅ Validation Rules (Safety Critical)

  • Half Weight (G/2): Must be between 0.1 – 10,000 kN. Zero or negative values are invalid.
  • Lifting Coefficient (f): Must be between 1.0 – 2.0 (typical: 1.1 – 1.5).
  • Spread Angle Factor (z): Must be between 1.0 – 3.0 (typical: 1.0 – 2.0 for safe geometry).
  • Anchors (n): Fixed at 2. Cannot be changed.
  • Output Feedback: ≤50 kN = Safe ✅, 51–100 kN = Caution ⚠️, >100 kN = High Risk ❌ (requires engineering verification).
