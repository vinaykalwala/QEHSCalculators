📘 About Subcritical Flow Sizing

This calculation determines the required orifice area of a pressure relief device for gas or vapor in **subcritical flow**. Subcritical flow occurs when the downstream pressure ($P_2$) is high enough to affect the flow rate through the valve (specifically, when $P_2 > P_{critical}$). This is common in systems with high backpressure. Sizing for these conditions is crucial to ensure the valve can still pass the required flow rate to prevent overpressure.

⚙️ Formula Used (API RP 520)

The required orifice area (A) for subcritical flow is given by:

$$ A = \frac{W \cdot \sqrt{T \cdot Z / M}}{735 \cdot F_2 \cdot K_d \cdot \sqrt{P_1 \cdot (P_1 - P_2)}} $$

Where the flow factor $F_2$ is:

$$ F_2 = \sqrt{\frac{\frac{k}{k-1} \left( \frac{P_2}{P_1} \right)^{\frac{2}{k}} \left( 1 - \left( \frac{P_2}{P_1} \right)^{\frac{k-1}{k}} \right)}{1 - \frac{P_2}{P_1}}} $$

📊 Key Parameters

  • $W$: Mass Flow Rate (kg/hr)
  • $P_1$: Upstream absolute pressure (kPa abs)
  • $P_2$: Back pressure (absolute) at the outlet (kPa abs)
  • $T$: Absolute temperature at inlet (K)
  • $M$: Molecular weight of the gas (kg/kmol)
  • $k$: Specific heat ratio ($C_p/C_v$)
  • $K_d$: Discharge coefficient (typically 0.65-0.85 for subcritical flow)
  • $Z$: Compressibility factor (1 for ideal gas)
