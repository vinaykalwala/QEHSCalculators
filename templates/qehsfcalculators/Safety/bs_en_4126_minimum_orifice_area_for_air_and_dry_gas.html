📘 About This Calculator

The BS EN 4126 Minimum Orifice Area Calculator helps determine the minimum required orifice area for air and dry gas according to the BS EN 4126 standard for safety relief valves.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Mass Flow Rate (ṁ): The amount of gas passing through per hour (kg/h).
  • Absolute Relieving Pressure (P₀): The absolute pressure at which the gas is released (bar a).
  • Isentropic Exponent Function (C): A factor that represents gas properties.
  • Derated Coefficient of Discharge (Kdr): A correction factor for real-world efficiency (0-1).
  • Correction Factor for Sub-Critical Flow (Kb): Accounts for flow conditions.
  • Specific Volume (ν): The volume occupied by 1 kg of gas at given conditions (m³/kg).
  • Constant Value (0.2883): Derived factor used in gas flow calculations for critical flow conditions.

📝 Input Validation Rules

  • Mass Flow Rate (ṁ): 1 – 1,000,000 kg/h, must be > 0.
  • Relieving Pressure (P₀): 1 – 400 bar a, must be > 0.
  • Isentropic Exponent (C): 2.0 – 3.5 typical for gases.
  • Kdr: 0.6 – 1.0 only.
  • Kb: 0.1 – 1.5 allowed, usually ~1.0.
  • Specific Volume (ν): 0.001 – 50 m³/kg, must be > 0.
  • Formula: A = ṁ / (0.2833 × C × Kdr × Kb × √(P₀ / ν))

📊 Output Validations

  • Area < 50 mm²: ⚠️ Small orifice area, ensure sufficient for gas discharge.
  • Area 50–2000 mm²: ✅ Optimal range, typical for safety valves.
  • Area > 2000 mm²: ❌ Large orifice area, verify system design.

📏 Governing Principle

Standard for Minimum Orifice Area Calculation. The calculation of the minimum orifice area for air and dry gas in safety valves is governed by EN ISO 4126-1. This standard specifies the general requirements for safety valves, including those used with air and dry gas.

How it Works

  • Gas Flow Principles: Applies isentropic and critical flow formulas to estimate minimum orifice area.
  • Real-World Correction Factors: Uses Kdr and Kb to adjust for deviations in actual valve performance.

Relevant Standards & Guidelines

  • EN ISO 4126-1: General requirements for safety valves.
  • BS EN 4126: Specifies safety relief valve design for air and dry gas.
  • Manufacturer Guidelines: Many valve manufacturers provide practical orifice sizing recommendations.

✅ Conclusion

This calculation ensures gases can be safely discharged under high pressure, preventing explosions or system damage by properly sizing safety relief valves.
