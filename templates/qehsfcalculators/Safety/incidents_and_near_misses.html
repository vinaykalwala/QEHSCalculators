üìê Governing Principle

An Incidents & Near Misses Calculator isn't tied to a single universal standard but follows methodologies established by key organizations such as the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA), the Indian Standards (IS 3786), and ISO 45001. These frameworks define how safety rates and ratios are derived to quantify workplace safety performance and near-miss frequency.

OSHA‚Äôs TRIR (Standard):
Used to calculate recordable workplace injuries and illnesses. OSHA defines a near miss as an unplanned event that had the potential for injury, illness, or damage but did not result in it.

Indian Standard (IS 3786):
Establishes methods for calculating injury frequency and severity rates. Although not directly addressing near misses, it underpins how incident data are structured and analyzed.

ISO 45001:
Provides an international framework for Occupational Health and Safety Management Systems. It defines a near miss as an incident with no injury or ill health but with potential to cause harm.

HSE Rate Formulas:
Introduces ratios comparing near miss events to recorded injuries or illnesses to identify safety trends and areas for intervention.

Summary:
Although no single global formula exists, the calculation and interpretation of incident-to-near-miss ratios are grounded in OSHA, IS 3786, ISO 45001, and HSE methodologies‚Äîensuring consistency, comparability, and continuous improvement in safety performance measurement.
