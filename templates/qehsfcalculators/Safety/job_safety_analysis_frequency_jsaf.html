📘 About This Calculator

The Job Safety Analysis Frequency (JSAF) Calculator measures how often Job Safety Analyses (JSAs) are performed per 1 million work hours. A higher JSAF indicates a proactive approach to safety.

❓ What is Job Safety Analysis Frequency (JSAF)?

JSAF is a metric that quantifies how frequently safety analyses are conducted relative to the total work hours. It helps organizations benchmark their safety planning efforts against industry standards.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Total Job Safety Analyses: The number of safety analyses conducted for various tasks or jobs
  • Total Employee Work Hours: The sum of all hours worked by employees during a reporting period

📊 Interpretation

Higher JSAF Values = more proactive safety culture. Generally:

  • Below 50 → Needs improvement
  • 50 - 100 → Good, but room for improvement
  • 100 - 150 → Excellent, meeting best practices
  • Above 150 → Outstanding, robust safety culture

🌍 Real-Life Applications

  • Construction Industry - Monitoring safety planning for high-risk tasks
  • Manufacturing - Assessing frequency of safety reviews for production processes
  • Oil & Gas - Evaluating safety analysis practices in hazardous environments
  • Corporate Safety Programs - Benchmarking safety culture across departments
  • Regulatory Compliance - Demonstrating commitment to proactive safety management

📋 Industry Standards

Industry best practices typically aim for 100+ JSAF per million hours. Organizations with strong safety cultures often exceed 150 JSAF, while values below 50 may indicate gaps in safety planning processes.

✅ Validation Rules Applied

  • Job Safety Analyses (JSAs): Must be a whole number (0 – 10,000).
  • Total Work Hours: Must be a positive number (1 – 10,000,000,000).
  • No Decimals: JSAs cannot have decimal values.
  • Cross-Check: Extremely low work hours with high JSAs will show a warning.
  • Division by Zero: Blocked (hours cannot be 0).
  • Extreme Outliers: JSAF > 1000 will trigger a caution message.
