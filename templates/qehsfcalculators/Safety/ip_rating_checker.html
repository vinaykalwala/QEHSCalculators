📘 About This Calculator

The IP Rating Checker helps you determine the Ingress Protection (IP) rating of electrical enclosures based on their protection levels against solids and liquids.

❓ What is an IP Rating?

The IP (Ingress Protection) rating system classifies the degree of protection provided by mechanical casings and electrical enclosures against intrusion, dust, accidental contact, and water.

⚙️ Rating System

  • First Digit (Solids): Ranges from 0-6, indicating protection against solid objects
  • Second Digit (Liquids): Ranges from 0-9K, indicating protection against liquids

📊 Common IP Ratings

  • IP54 → Dust protected and protected against water splashes
  • IP65 → Dust tight and protected against water jets
  • IP67 → Dust tight and protected against immersion (1m, 30min)
  • IP68 → Dust tight and protected against continuous immersion

🌍 Real-Life Applications

  • Consumer Electronics - Smartphones, watches, and headphones
  • Industrial Equipment - Control panels, motors, and sensors
  • Outdoor Lighting - Street lights, garden lights, and signage
  • Automotive - Car electronics, sensors, and lighting systems
  • Medical Devices - Equipment requiring sterilization or used in wet environments

📋 Industry Standards

IP ratings are defined by international standard IEC 60529, which is used by organizations worldwide including the European Committee for Electrotechnical Standardization (CENELEC) and the American National Standards Institute (ANSI).

✅ Validation Rules

  • Solids: Must be integer 0–6 (IEC 60529).
  • Liquids: Must be integer 0–8 or "9K".
  • Both fields must be selected for a valid rating.
  • Cross-check: "9K" normally requires Solids = 6 (dust-tight).
  • Warnings: IP values with 0 indicate no protection.
