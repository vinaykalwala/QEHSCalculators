📘 About This Calculator

The Critical Flow Scaling Parameter Calculator helps analyze two-phase fluid flow under critical conditions, where liquid and vapor coexist. It determines how pressure, temperature, and specific properties affect the transition between phases.

❓ What is the Critical Flow Scaling Parameter?

The Critical Flow Scaling Parameter (ω) is a dimensionless parameter that characterizes two-phase flow behavior under critical conditions. It's essential for understanding energy transfer and flow dynamics in thermodynamic systems.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Quality or Mass Fraction of Vapor (x): Represents the proportion of vapor in a liquid-vapor mixture (ranges from 0 to 1).
  • Difference in Specific Volume (v_fg): The difference in specific volumes between the vapor and liquid phases.
  • Specific Volume (v): The volume occupied by a unit mass of the substance.
  • Specific Heat at Constant Pressure (C_p): The heat required to raise the temperature of a unit mass by one degree while keeping pressure constant.
  • Temperature (T in Kelvin): The absolute temperature of the fluid.
  • Pressure (P in Pascals): The force exerted by the fluid per unit area.
  • Latent Heat of Vaporization (h_fg): The amount of heat required to convert a unit mass of liquid into vapor at constant pressure.

📊 Real-Life Applications

  • Boiler and Steam Systems: Determines steam flow efficiency in power plants and industrial boilers.
  • Nuclear Reactors: Helps in analyzing coolant flow to prevent overheating and ensure safety.
  • Cryogenics: Used in the design of liquid nitrogen and oxygen storage and handling systems.
  • Aerospace & Jet Propulsion: Important for fuel flow analysis in jet engines and rocket propulsion.
  • Refrigeration & HVAC Systems: Helps in designing efficient cooling and air conditioning systems.

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • x (Vapor Quality): 0 ≤ x ≤ 1 (dimensionless)
  • v_fg (Δ Specific Volume): >0, recommended range 0.0001 – 100 m³/kg
  • v (Specific Volume): >0, must be > x·v_fg, range 0.0001 – 100 m³/kg
  • C_p (Specific Heat): 0.1 – 10,000 J/kg·K
  • T (Temperature): 1 – 6000 K
  • P (Pressure): 1 – 1e9 Pa
  • h_fg (Latent Heat): >0, range 0.1 – 1e7 J/kg
  • Cross-check: Ensure units are consistent (e.g., J/kg vs. kJ/kg)
