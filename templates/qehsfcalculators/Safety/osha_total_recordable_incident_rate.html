📘 About This Calculator

The OSHA Total Recordable Incident Rate (TRIR) Calculator measures workplace safety performance by calculating the number of recordable injuries and illnesses per 100 full-time workers.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Recordable Injuries and Illnesses: Total number of OSHA-recordable workplace injuries and illnesses
  • Employee Hours Worked: Total number of hours worked by all employees during the period

📝 Input Validation Rules

  • Recordable Injuries: Must be an integer between 0 – 10,000; no decimals; cannot be negative.
  • Employee Hours: Must be an integer between 1 – 100,000,000; no decimals; cannot be 0.
  • Cross-field check: High number of recordables with very low hours will be flagged as unrealistic.
  • Output: Expressed as incidents per 200,000 hours worked.
  • Compliance: Based on OSHA recordkeeping requirements (29 CFR 1904).

📊 Output Validations

  • TRIR ≤ 1.0 → Good safety performance (green)
  • 1.0 < TRIR ≤ 3.0 → Acceptable performance, needs improvement (orange)
  • TRIR > 3.0 → Needs significant improvement (red)

📏 Governing Principle

The OSHA Total Recordable Incident Rate (TRIR) calculator uses a standardized formula defined by the Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA), but it isn't a separate standard itself. The standard calculation uses the (Number of injuries and illnesses × 200,000) ÷ Employee hours worked formula to measure the rate of recordable injuries and illnesses per 100 full-time employees annually.

  • Numerator: Total OSHA recordable injuries and illnesses for a specific period (usually a year).
  • Denominator: Total hours worked by all employees during that period.
  • 200,000 Factor: Represents the hours 100 full-time employees would work in a year (100 × 40 hrs/week × 50 weeks/year).
  • Purpose: Produces a comparable rate across companies and industries to benchmark safety performance relative to national or industry averages.
  • Usage: Benchmarking, identifying safety trends, and compliance monitoring using data from OSHA 300 and 300A forms.

✅ Conclusion

This calculator provides a standardized method to quantify workplace safety performance using TRIR. Organizations can use it to monitor safety trends, benchmark against industry standards, and ensure compliance with OSHA recordkeeping requirements.
