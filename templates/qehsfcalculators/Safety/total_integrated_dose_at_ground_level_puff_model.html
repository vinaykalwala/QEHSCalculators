📘 About This Calculator

The Total Integrated Dose at Ground Level (Puff Model) calculates cumulative exposure from an instantaneous release, considering atmospheric dispersion over time and space.

This tool relies on a Gaussian dispersion model, essential for emergency response planning and industrial safety assessments.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Qinstantaneous: Total pollutant mass released instantly (grams).
  • u: Average wind speed transporting the puff (m/s).
  • σy, σz: Horizontal & vertical dispersion coefficients (m), based on atmospheric stability and distance.
  • y: Crosswind distance from plume centerline (m).
  • H: Effective release height above ground (m).

📝 Input Validation Rules

  • Instantaneous Release (Q): 0.1 – 1,000,000 g.
  • Wind Speed (u): 0.1 – 30 m/s.
  • Horizontal Dispersion (σy): 1 – 5000 m.
  • Vertical Dispersion (σz): 1 – 3000 m.
  • Crosswind Distance (y): 0 – 50,000 m.
  • Release Height (H): 0 – 500 m.
  • Cross-Field Check: Denominator must not be zero to avoid division errors.

💡 Allowed Decimals

  • All inputs → up to 2–3 decimal places for practical accuracy.

📊 Output Validations

  • > 1 × 10-3: ☢️ Danger: Extremely high dose. Immediate evacuation required.
  • 1 × 10-6 – 1 × 10-3: ⚠️ Warning: Significant dose. Implement protective measures.
  • 1 × 10-9 – 1 × 10-6: 🟡 Caution: Moderate dose. Compare with PEL/OEL limits.
  • < 1 × 10-9: ✅ Low Dose: Minimal exposure. Continue monitoring.

📏 Governing Principle

The Puff Model is a Lagrangian Gaussian atmospheric dispersion method for instantaneous or short-duration releases. It calculates total integrated dose by tracking "puffs" over time and integrating concentrations at a specific location.

Regulatory guidance and models include:

  • United States: EPA (CALPUFF, SCIPUFF), NRC (RASCAL) for nuclear/chemical releases.
  • International: IAEA Safety Guide NS-G-3.2; European RIMPUFF model for emergency response.
  • India: AERB guidelines for nuclear facility dose computations, aligned with Gaussian puff methodology.

The model is scientifically validated and applied for regulatory compliance, accident consequence analysis, and emergency response planning.

✅ Conclusion

The Puff Model dose calculation is essential for health and safety professionals to anticipate and mitigate chemical release risks. Correct usage informs emergency planning and industrial safety measures, potentially saving lives.
