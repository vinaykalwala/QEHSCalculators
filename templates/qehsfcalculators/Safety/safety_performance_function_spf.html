üìò About Safety Performance Functions

A **Safety Performance Function (SPF)** is a statistical regression model used by transportation safety engineers to predict the average number of crashes per year at a specific location. SPFs are a core component of the Highway Safety Manual (HSM) and allow for a proactive approach to safety by identifying sites that may have a higher-than-expected crash risk, even before a long history of crashes accumulates.

‚öôÔ∏è Formula Used

The SPF is typically represented by a power function:

$$ N_{spf} = a \times (AADT)^b $$

  • $N_{spf}$ = Predicted average crash frequency for a base condition.
  • $AADT$ = Annual Average Daily Traffic.
  • $a, b$ = Regression coefficients (calibration factors) specific to a certain road type (e.g., rural two-lane, urban arterial).
