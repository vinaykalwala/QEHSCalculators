📘 About This Calculator

This **Safety Performance Function (SPF) Calculator** predicts the expected number of crashes per year on a roadway segment based on traffic volume (AADT) and site-specific calibration factors. It is designed to support proactive road safety management by identifying locations with higher-than-expected crash risks.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • AADT: Annual Average Daily Traffic – the average number of vehicles per day.
  • Calibration Factor (a): Adjusts the model for local roadway and traffic conditions.
  • Exponent (b): Determines how crash frequency scales with traffic volume.

📝 Input Validations

  • AADT (Annual Average Daily Traffic): Must be a positive whole number. Represents the average traffic volume per day. Invalid if left empty, zero, negative, non-numeric, or a decimal.
  • Calibration Factor (a): Must be a positive number. Used in the SPF formula to scale predicted crashes. Cannot be zero, negative, or non-numeric.
  • Exponent (b): Must be a positive number between 0 and 3. Determines how crash frequency grows with traffic volume. Cannot be zero, negative, non-numeric, or unreasonably high.
  • Logical consistency: All inputs must be numbers. Extreme values may produce unrealistic results and should be double-checked.

💡 These validations prevent calculation errors and ensure the predicted crash output is meaningful for road safety analysis.

📝 Output Validations

  • Predicted crashes calculation is based on: Predicted Crashes = a * AADT^b.
  • Output is displayed with two decimals and AADT is formatted for readability.
  • Feedback based on predicted crash range:
    • Predicted Crashes < 1 → ✅ Very low risk segment; current safety measures sufficient.
    • Predicted Crashes 1–5 → ⚠️ Moderate risk; review potential safety improvements.
    • Predicted Crashes 6–20 → ⚠️ High risk; consider engineering or enforcement countermeasures.
    • Predicted Crashes ≥ 21 → 🚨 Very high risk; immediate safety interventions required.
  • Invalid calculation (NaN, Infinite) → ❌ Calculation resulted in an invalid number. Please check your inputs.

💡 These outputs provide clear interpretation for road safety decision-making and are aligned with SPF best practices.

📏 Standards & Compliance

The calculator aligns with the **Highway Safety Manual (HSM)** methodologies for SPF modeling. Calibration factors and exponents should be chosen based on local crash data or HSM default values to ensure accuracy and compliance with transportation safety standards.

Conclusion

Using SPFs allows engineers and policymakers to estimate crash frequency and prioritize safety interventions. This proactive approach helps optimize safety resources, prevent accidents, and improve roadway safety performance.
