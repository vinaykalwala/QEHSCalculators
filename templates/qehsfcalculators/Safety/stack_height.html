📘 About This Calculator

The Stack Height Calculator determines the required exhaust stack height for diesel generator sets to ensure safe emission dispersion. This is critical for environmental compliance, occupational safety, and fire safety inspections.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • Building Height (h): Measured from ground to highest point (m).
  • Generator Capacity (KVA): Rated capacity of installed gensets.

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Building Height: 0.1 – 1000 m (decimals allowed, 2 places).
  • Generator Capacity: 0.1 – 100,000 KVA (decimals allowed, 1 place).
  • Both inputs must be positive numbers (no negatives/zeros).
  • Cross-check: Higher generator capacity → higher required stack height.

📊 Output Validations

  • H ≤ h: ❌ Non-compliant. Stack cannot be lower than building.
  • H = h + 0–3 m: 🔵 Minimum acceptable for small gensets, good dispersion.
  • H = h + 3–6 m: ✅ Adequate for most applications with emission controls.
  • H ≥ h + 6 m: ⚠️ Needed for large gensets or poor dispersion areas.

Assessment feedback shown with result matches these output rules.

📋 Standards

This calculator aligns with:
• EPA (40 CFR Part 51) – Good Engineering Practice (GEP) stack height
• CPCB India – Diesel generator emission guidelines
• NFPA 37 – Exhaust system installation
• ISO 6826 – Internal combustion engine installations

📝 Conclusion

Correct stack height ensures safe dispersion of exhaust gases, preventing re-entry into building air intakes and exposure to workers. This calculator supports compliance audits, but final design must be validated by a qualified engineer with site-specific dispersion studies.
