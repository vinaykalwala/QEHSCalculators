📘 About This Calculator

The Job Safety Analysis Coverage Calculator measures the proportion of tasks for which a Job Safety Analysis has been conducted, providing insight into the comprehensiveness of your safety program.

❓ What is Job Safety Analysis Coverage?

Job Safety Analysis Coverage is a metric that quantifies the percentage of tasks that have undergone a formal safety analysis, indicating how thoroughly potential hazards have been identified and addressed.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Total Job Safety Analyses Conducted: Number of formal safety evaluations performed on tasks
  • Total Tasks or Jobs Performed: Total number of tasks executed during the reporting period

📊 Interpretation

Higher Coverage Values = more comprehensive safety analysis. Generally:

  • Below 25% → Needs improvement
  • 25% - 50% → Moderate coverage
  • 50% - 75% → Good coverage
  • Above 75% → Excellent coverage

🌍 Real-Life Applications

  • Workplace Safety Programs - Evaluating the comprehensiveness of safety initiatives
  • Regulatory Compliance - Demonstrating due diligence to safety authorities
  • Risk Management - Identifying gaps in safety analysis coverage
  • Safety Audits - Benchmarking safety program effectiveness
  • Continuous Improvement - Tracking progress in expanding safety analysis coverage

📋 Industry Standards

OSHA recommends Job Safety Analysis for all tasks with injury potential. Best practice organizations typically aim for 80-100% coverage of high-risk tasks and at least 50% coverage of moderate-risk tasks.

✅ Validation Rules Applied

  • Total Job Safety Analyses Conducted: Integer only, range 0–10,000.
  • Total Tasks Performed: Integer only, range 1–10,000.
  • Cross-Check: Job Safety Analyses cannot exceed Total Tasks.
  • Units: Both values are whole numbers (counts, no decimals).
  • Edge Cases: Division by zero prevented, negatives not allowed, unrealistic outliers restricted.
