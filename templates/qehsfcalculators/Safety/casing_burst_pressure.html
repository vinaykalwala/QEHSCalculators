📘 About This Calculator

The Casing Burst Pressure Calculator helps determine the pressure at which a casing is likely to rupture using Barlow's formula, which is widely used for calculating the burst pressure of thin-walled cylindrical structures.

❓ What is Casing Burst Pressure?

Casing burst pressure is the internal pressure at which a cylindrical casing will fail or rupture. It's a critical parameter in industries like oil and gas where casing integrity is essential for safety and operational efficiency.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Tensile Strength (St): The material's resistance to tension, measured in psi (pounds per square inch).
  • Outer Diameter (Do): The external diameter of the casing, measured in inches.
  • Wall Thickness (t): The thickness of the casing wall, measured in inches.

📊 Interpretation

  • Below 5,000 psi → Low burst pressure, suitable for low-pressure applications
  • 5,000 – 15,000 psi → Medium burst pressure, typical for many industrial applications
  • Above 15,000 psi → High burst pressure, requires special materials and design considerations

💡 Important Note

This calculation uses Barlow's formula which assumes ideal conditions. Real-world performance may vary due to material defects, temperature variations, pressure cycling, and other factors. Always incorporate appropriate safety factors in engineering designs.

📝 About PSI

PSI stands for "Pounds per Square Inch" and is a unit of pressure commonly used in the United States to measure the intensity of force applied over an area. One psi is the pressure resulting from a force of one pound-force applied to an area of one square inch.

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Tensile Strength (Sₜ): 10,000 – 200,000 psi
  • Outer Diameter (Dₒ): 1 – 48 inches
  • Wall Thickness (t): 0.1 – 2.0 inches, and t < Dₒ/2
  • Formula: Pb = (2 × Sₜ × t) / Dₒ
  • Output Ranges:
    • <500 psi → Invalid (check inputs)
    • <5,000 psi → Low burst pressure
    • 5,000 – 15,000 psi → Medium (typical industrial range)
    • 15,000 – 30,000 psi → High (specialized casing)
    • >30,000 psi → Likely input error

⚠️ These validations align with API 5CT and NFPA pressure vessel safety guidance. Always apply safety factors in engineering design.
