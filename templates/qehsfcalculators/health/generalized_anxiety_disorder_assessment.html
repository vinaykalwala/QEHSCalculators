📘 About This Calculator

This GAD-7 calculator is a self-assessment tool used to screen for and measure the severity of generalized anxiety. By answering seven questions about your experiences over the last two weeks, you can obtain a score that helps quantify your anxiety level. This score can be a useful starting point for a conversation with a healthcare provider.

⚙️ Parameters Used

The total score is calculated by summing the points for each of the seven questions. The points are assigned based on the frequency of symptoms (0=Not at all, 1=Several days, 2=More than half the days, 3=Nearly every day). The total score is then used to classify the severity of anxiety.

  • 0-4: Minimal anxiety
  • 5-9: Mild anxiety
  • 10-14: Moderate anxiety
  • 15-21: Severe anxiety

✅ Input Validation Rules

To ensure a valid score, the following rules are enforced:

  • Completeness: All seven questions must be answered to calculate a result.
  • Valid Selections: Each answer must be one of the provided options, corresponding to a score between 0 and 3.
  • Error Notification: If any question is left unanswered, an error message will be displayed.

✅ Output Validations

Your total score corresponds to a specific level of anxiety severity, and the calculator will display one of the following interpretations:

  • Score 0-4: Minimal Anxiety: Little to no anxiety. Continue monitoring your well-being.
  • Score 5-9: Mild Anxiety: Mild symptoms. Consider relaxation techniques or talking with someone you trust.
  • Score 10-14: Moderate Anxiety: Moderate symptoms. Consulting a healthcare provider is recommended.
  • Score 15-21: Severe Anxiety: Severe symptoms. Strongly advised to seek professional support.

⚖️ Governing Principle

The GAD-7 (Generalized Anxiety Disorder 7-item scale) is a screening tool based on the diagnostic criteria for Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD) as outlined in the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders (DSM). It is not a specific standard itself, but rather a measure developed from and validated against the standard criteria for diagnosing GAD.

Key Points

  • Based on DSM Criteria: The GAD-7 questionnaire was designed to reflect the core symptoms of GAD according to the DSM.
  • Developed by Spitzer and Colleagues: The scale was created by Robert L. Spitzer, Kurt Kroenke, and J.B. Williams, with later input from B. Löwe.
  • Standard in its Field: While not a "standard" in the same way the DSM is, the GAD-7 has become a widely used and well-validated instrument for assessing anxiety in both clinical and general populations.
  • Purpose: It serves as a brief, self-administered screening tool and a measure of anxiety severity.

🧾 Conclusion

This GAD-7 calculator is an informational tool and does not provide a medical diagnosis. A high score suggests that a follow-up with a mental health professional is advisable for a comprehensive evaluation. Please consult a qualified healthcare provider for any mental health concerns.
