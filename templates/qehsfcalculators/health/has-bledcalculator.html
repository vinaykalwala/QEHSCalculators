📘 About This Calculator

The HAS-BLED Score Calculator helps estimate the 1-year risk of major bleeding in patients with atrial fibrillation (AF) who are undergoing anticoagulation therapy. Rather than discouraging treatment, the HAS-BLED score guides clinicians to identify and correct modifiable bleeding risk factors such as uncontrolled hypertension or labile INR, thereby making anticoagulation safer and more effective.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • H – Hypertension (Uncontrolled, >160 mmHg systolic)
  • A – Abnormal renal and/or liver function (1 point each)
  • S – Stroke history
  • B – Bleeding history or predisposition
  • L – Labile INR
  • E – Elderly (Age ≥ 65 years)
  • D – Drugs/alcohol (1 point each for medications predisposing to bleeding or alcohol use ≥8 drinks/week)

Each positive parameter adds 1 point to the total HAS-BLED score.

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Each input must be selected as Yes or No.
  • All dropdowns must contain valid numeric values (0 or 1) for calculation.
  • No empty or invalid inputs are permitted before the calculation is performed.

The calculator validates that each clinical factor is properly selected before processing the total score.

🧾 Output Validations

The output displays the total HAS-BLED score, corresponding annual bleeding risk, and a detailed clinical interpretation:

  • Low Risk (0–2 points): The patient has a low (approx. 1.1% annual) risk of major bleeding. Anticoagulation can be considered with routine follow-up.
  • Moderate Risk (3 points): The patient has a moderate (approx. 1.9% annual) risk of major bleeding. Use caution and address any modifiable risk factors (e.g., blood pressure, INR control).
  • High Risk (≥4 points): The patient has a high (approx. 8.7% annual) risk of major bleeding. Requires careful consideration of risks vs. benefits and aggressive management of modifiable risk factors.

⚖ Governing Principle

The same clinical interpretation and assessment principles used in output validation apply here:

  • Low Risk (0–2 points): The patient has a low (approx. 1.1% annual) risk of major bleeding. Anticoagulation can be considered with routine follow-up.
  • Moderate Risk (3 points): The patient has a moderate (approx. 1.9% annual) risk of major bleeding. Use caution and address any modifiable risk factors (e.g., blood pressure, INR control).
  • High Risk (≥4 points): The patient has a high (approx. 8.7% annual) risk of major bleeding. Requires careful consideration of risks vs. benefits and aggressive management of modifiable risk factors.

The HAS-BLED score calculator’s standard is based on the 2010 Euro Heart Survey cohort, which involved 3,978 patients with atrial fibrillation. It was developed to assess the 1-year risk of major bleeding in AF patients receiving anticoagulation. Major bleeding was defined as intracranial bleeding, hospitalization, a >2 g/dL decrease in hemoglobin, or transfusion.

🧩 Conclusion

The HAS-BLED score is a simple, validated, and practical clinical tool that helps healthcare professionals identify and manage bleeding risks in patients receiving anticoagulation therapy. While a higher score signals the need for closer monitoring and risk modification, it should not automatically preclude anticoagulation. Instead, it emphasizes the importance of optimizing clinical parameters to ensure safe and effective therapy.
