üìå About This Calculator

The Rapid Entire Body Assessment (REBA) is an ergonomic tool used to evaluate whole-body postural risks in occupational settings. It helps assess the physical strain associated with different tasks, particularly in workplaces where manual labor, repetitive movements, or awkward postures are involved.

REBA considers factors such as body posture, force exertion, and movement frequency to determine a risk score, which can guide workplace modifications and injury prevention strategies. By identifying high-risk postures, employers and safety professionals can implement ergonomic interventions to enhance worker health, efficiency, and overall well-being.

üìê Governing Principle

The Rapid Entire Body Assessment (REBA) method is not tied to a single standard but was developed by Sue Hignett and Lynn McAtamney in the UK and is a widely used ergonomic tool for assessing work-related musculoskeletal disorder (WMSD) risk in various industries. While it has been applied in studies using ISO 11226, the REBA system itself is an established observational method detailed in a worksheet and tables, forming its own framework for evaluating postures.

Key Aspects of the REBA Method

  • Developed in the UK: The REBA method was created by Hignett and McAtamney in the UK at the Nottingham Hospital.
  • Ergonomic Tool: It is an ergonomic instrument used to evaluate the risk of musculoskeletal disorders (MSDs) associated with different working postures.
  • Focus on Posture: REBA divides the body into segments to assess static and dynamic postures, providing a quick and easy way to measure the risk of WMSDs.
  • No Single Standard: It is not based on a single external standard but rather the method itself, as outlined by Hignett and McAtamney in their worksheets and scoring tables.
  • Application: It is a valuable tool for quickly assessing a variety of work postures found in healthcare and other service industries where manual handling and dynamic work positions are common.
