About Stroke Volume (SV)

Stroke Volume (SV) is a key measure of cardiac performance, representing the volume of blood pumped from the left ventricle per heartbeat. It is a crucial indicator of cardiovascular health and efficiency. It is calculated by dividing Cardiac Output by Heart Rate.

Interpretation of Results

  • Below 60 mL/beat: A low stroke volume may suggest impaired heart function or conditions like dehydration or heart failure. Medical evaluation is recommended.
  • 60‚Äì120 mL/beat: This is the typical range for healthy adults at rest, reflecting efficient and stable cardiovascular performance.
  • Above 120 mL/beat: A high stroke volume is often seen in well-trained athletes, indicating a strong, efficient heart. It can also occur during physical exertion.

Clinical Significance

Stroke Volume is a powerful indicator of cardiac health. It helps in the early detection of heart issues, monitoring fitness progress in athletes, and guiding medical decisions in clinical and critical care settings.
