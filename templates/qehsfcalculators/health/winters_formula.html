About Winters' Formula

Winters' Formula is a clinical tool used to determine the expected respiratory compensation in patients with metabolic acidosis. By calculating the expected partial pressure of carbon dioxide (pCO₂), clinicians can assess whether the patient's respiratory system is responding appropriately to the metabolic disturbance.

Interpretation

Compare the patient's actual measured pCO₂ to the calculated range:

  • Actual pCO₂ within range: Appropriate respiratory compensation.
  • Actual pCO₂ higher than range: Suggests a concurrent respiratory acidosis.
  • Actual pCO₂ lower than range: Suggests a concurrent respiratory alkalosis.
