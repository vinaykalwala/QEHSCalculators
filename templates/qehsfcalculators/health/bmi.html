What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure of body fat based on a person's weight in relation to their height. It serves as a general indicator of whether a person is at a healthy weight and helps healthcare providers evaluate risks associated with obesity or being underweight.

Why is the BMI Calculator Used?

The BMI calculator is a simple and widely used tool to assess whether a person is underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese. It helps identify potential health risks and determine if further evaluation is necessary. However, BMI does not distinguish between muscle mass and fat, so it is not a perfect measure of health. It’s important to consider other factors like body composition and lifestyle.

What is Obesity?

Obesity is a medical condition with excessive body fat, classified by BMI. Class I (BMI 30-34.9) is mild, Class II (BMI 35-39.9) is moderate, and Class III (BMI ≥40) is severe. It increases the risk of heart disease, diabetes, and high blood pressure.

Risks Associated with Being Overweight

Elevated BMI is linked to numerous health problems, including Type II diabetes, coronary heart disease, stroke, sleep apnea, and an increased risk of certain cancers.

Risks Associated with Being Underweight

Being underweight can be associated with malnutrition, vitamin deficiencies, anemia, osteoporosis, a decreased immune function, and potential complications from surgery.

BMI Prime

BMI prime is the ratio of a person's measured BMI to the upper limit of what is considered "normal" (25 kg/m²). A value between 0.74 and 1 is classified as normal. It allows for a quick assessment of how much a person's BMI differs from this upper limit.
