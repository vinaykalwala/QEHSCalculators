About Algor Mortis Calculation

This calculator estimates the time since death using the principle of algor mortis, the postmortem cooling of the body. The rate of cooling is influenced by the difference between the body's temperature at death (approx. 37¬∞C) and the ambient temperature of the surroundings.

Estimating Time with Rigor Mortis

In addition to body temperature, forensic investigators use rigor mortis (muscle stiffening) as a secondary indicator. The following table provides a general timeline:

Body ConditionMuscle StiffnessEstimated Time Since Death
WarmFlexibleLess than 2 hours
WarmRigidBetween 2 and 8 hours
ColdRigidBetween 8 and 36 hours
ColdFlexibleMore than 36 hours
