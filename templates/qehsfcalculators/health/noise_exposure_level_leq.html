About Equivalent Sound Level (Leq)

The Equivalent Continuous Sound Level (Leq) is the time-averaged sound level, representing the total sound energy of a fluctuating noise over a period. It is the standard metric used in occupational health to assess noise exposure and prevent noise-induced hearing loss.

Why is Leq Important?

  • Health & Safety: It quantifies the risk of hearing damage from cumulative noise exposure.
  • Regulatory Compliance: Occupational safety standards, like those from OSHA, set permissible exposure limits based on an 8-hour time-weighted average, which is a form of Leq. The action level is typically 85 dB.
  • Risk Assessment: It allows for the comparison of different noise environments and helps prioritize noise control measures.

How to Interpret the Result

The calculated Leq value indicates the overall risk:

  • Below 85 dB: Generally considered safe for an 8-hour workday without hearing protection.
  • 85 dB and above: This is the "Action Level." Employers are required to implement a hearing conservation program. Hearing protection is strongly recommended.
  • 100 dB and above: Represents a very high risk. Exposure should be limited, and hearing protection is mandatory.
