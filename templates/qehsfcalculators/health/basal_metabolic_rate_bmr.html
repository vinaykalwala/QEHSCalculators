üìò About Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)

BMR represents the number of calories your body needs to perform basic life-sustaining functions, such as breathing, circulating blood, and maintaining cell production, while at rest. It is influenced by several factors, including your age, gender, height, and weight.

The BMR calculator uses the following parameters to estimate basal metabolic rate and total calorie needs:

  • Age: The person‚Äôs age in years. BMR generally decreases with age due to metabolic changes.
  • Gender: Biological sex (male or female). Men typically have higher BMR because of higher muscle mass.
  • Height: The person‚Äôs height in centimeters. Taller individuals usually have a higher BMR.
  • Weight: Body weight in kilograms. Higher body weight increases the energy required for basic functions.
  • Activity Level: Factor used to estimate total daily energy expenditure. Includes categories from sedentary to extra active.
  • Optional Waist/Hip Circumference: Used for additional body composition insights in some calculations.

These parameters feed into formulas like the Mifflin-St Jeor or Harris-Benedict equations to calculate BMR and total daily calorie needs.

BMR gives a baseline for calories needed at rest. To estimate your total daily calorie needs, multiply your BMR by an activity factor:

üìò Governing Principle

BMR calculators use established formulas to estimate calories burned at rest. They are not absolute measures but provide a reliable baseline:

  • Mifflin-St Jeor Equation: Developed in 1990; widely considered the most accurate for most adults.
  • Revised Harris-Benedict Equation: Original formula from 1919, updated in 1984 for better accuracy.
  • Katch-McArdle Formula: Requires body fat percentage; useful for lean or muscular individuals.

Key Considerations:

  • Estimates, not absolute measures; clinical testing (indirect calorimetry) is the gold standard.
  • Accuracy varies by formula; Mifflin-St Jeor is generally preferred.
  • Body composition significantly affects BMR; more muscle increases metabolic rate.
