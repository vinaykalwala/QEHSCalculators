About Waist-to-Height Ratio (WHtR)

The waist-to-height ratio (WHtR) is a simple and effective measure of centralized obesity. It is often considered a better predictor of cardiometabolic risk than BMI because it directly accounts for abdominal fat. A common guideline is to keep your waist circumference less than half your height.

Interpreting Your WHtR Result
