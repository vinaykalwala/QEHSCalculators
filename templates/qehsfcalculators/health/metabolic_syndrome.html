About Metabolic Syndrome

Metabolic syndrome is a cluster of five conditions that increase your risk of developing heart disease, stroke, and type 2 diabetes. This diagnosis is made when a person has **at least three** of the five risk factors listed in the calculator.

Understanding the 5 Criteria

CriterionWhy it Matters
Large Waist CircumferenceExcess abdominal fat (visceral fat) is metabolically active and increases inflammation and insulin resistance.
High TriglyceridesHigh levels of this type of fat in the blood are linked to atherosclerosis (hardening of the arteries).
Low HDL ("Good") CholesterolHDL cholesterol helps remove harmful cholesterol from arteries. Low levels increase cardiovascular risk.
High Blood PressureSustained high pressure damages blood vessels and forces the heart to work harder.
High Fasting GlucoseIndicates that the body is not using insulin effectively (insulin resistance), a precursor to diabetes.

Treatment Recommendations

If you have metabolic syndrome, lifestyle changes are the first line of treatment:

  • Healthy Diet: Focus on fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains. Limit processed foods, sugar, and unhealthy fats.
  • Regular Exercise: Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week.
  • Weight Management: Losing even a modest amount of weight (5-10% of body weight) can significantly improve all criteria.
