Origin and Purpose

The FFMI formula was developed from a study by Kouri, Elena M., et al. in 1995, to identify the natural upper limits of muscle mass in the body. It is considered a valuable tool for evaluating muscular development and is used to determine whether a physique is achievable naturally, according to BodySpec.

Application in Natural Bodybuilding

The FFMI is considered the "gold standard" in natural bodybuilding for assessing muscle mass and is used to assess if a physique is achievable naturally, as detailed by BodySpec. The research by Kouri et al. indicates that a man's FFMI naturally tops out around 25, and any FFMI exceeding this suggests the use of doping, making it a common tool for controlling the use.

The FFMI calculator is a useful tool for tracking progress and understanding your body composition in the context of muscular development. However, it should be used as a guide and not as a definitive measure of health or athletic potential. Factors like genetics and training style also play a significant role.
