üìå Key Definitions

  • Glycemia: The presence and concentration of glucose in the blood.

  • Normoglycemia: Normal blood sugar range (2.8‚Äì5.5 mmol/L or 50‚Äì100 mg/dL).

‚öñÔ∏è Governing Principle

A Blood Sugar Converter uses the international IFCC standard for HbA1c and mmol/L for glucose, while mg/dL is used mainly in the United States. Conversion between these units allows universal interpretation of blood sugar and HbA1c values.

  • IFCC Standard: International standard for HbA1c, expressed in mmol/mol.
  • mmol/L: International standard for measuring blood glucose outside the US.
  • mg/dL: Common unit used in the United States.
  • Purpose: To standardize values for universal interpretation, facilitate global patient understanding, and ensure consistent communication with healthcare providers.
