📘 What is the Body Roundness Index (BRI)?

The Body Roundness Index (BRI) is a measure of body fat distribution, focusing on how much your body shape resembles a circle. It is calculated using your height and waist circumference. Unlike BMI, BRI considers the distribution of fat, particularly visceral fat, which is linked to health risks such as cardiovascular disease and diabetes.

💡 Why is BRI Important?

BRI provides a more accurate assessment of health risks related to body fat distribution. It helps identify individuals at risk for conditions like metabolic syndrome, hypertension, and insulin resistance. A higher BRI indicates a higher concentration of visceral fat, which is more dangerous than subcutaneous fat.

📝 How to Use the BRI Calculator

Follow these steps to calculate your BRI:

  1. Measure Your Height: Stand straight and measure your height in centimeters (cm).
  2. Measure Your Waist Circumference: Use a measuring tape to measure your waist at the midpoint between the bottom of your ribs and the top of your hips.
  3. Enter Your Values: Input your height and waist circumference into the calculator.
  4. Calculate: Click "Calculate BRI" to see your results and interpretation.

📊 Interpreting Your BRI Results

Based on studies, BRI values can be interpreted as follows:

  • BRI < 3.41: Moderate increase in health risks (25% higher risk of mortality).
  • BRI 3.41–4.44: No significant increase in health risks.
  • BRI 4.45–5.45: Low risk for health problems related to visceral obesity.
  • BRI 5.46–6.91: Moderate increase in health risks.
  • BRI > 6.91: High risk of health problems related to visceral obesity (50% higher risk of mortality).

⚖️ BRI vs. BMI

Unlike BMI, which only considers height and weight, BRI focuses on waist circumference and fat distribution. This makes BRI a better predictor of health risks related to visceral fat, such as heart disease and diabetes.

✅ Input Validations

  • Height (cm): Enter your actual height. Allowed range: 100–250 cm. Avoid unrealistic values.
  • Waist Circumference (cm): Measure around your natural waist. Allowed range: 50–150 cm. Waist cannot exceed twice your height.
  • Decimals: You can enter decimal values (e.g., 88.5 cm) for precision.
  • Logical Consistency: Waist must be smaller than twice your height.
  • Edge Cases: Empty, zero, negative, or non-numeric values are not accepted.

This ensures accurate BRI calculation and meaningful health risk assessment.
