Understanding the Karvonen Formula

The Karvonen formula is a widely used method for calculating your Target Heart Rate (THR) during physical activity. It is more precise than basic methods because it considers both your Resting Heart Rate (RHR) and Maximum Heart Rate (MHR). This personalized approach helps optimize your workouts by ensuring you train at the right intensity to meet your fitness goals.

Key Terms Explained

1. Resting Heart Rate (RHR): Your heartbeats per minute (bpm) at complete rest. A lower RHR generally indicates better cardiovascular fitness. Best measured in the morning.

2. Maximum Heart Rate (MHR): The highest bpm your heart can achieve during maximum effort. If unknown, it's estimated as 220 - Age.

3. Heart Rate Reserve (HRR): The difference between your MHR and RHR (MHR - RHR). It represents your heart‚Äôs capacity for work during exercise.

4. Target Heart Rate (THR): The ideal range to maintain during exercise. The formula is: THR = ((MHR - RHR) √ó % Intensity) + RHR.

How to Use This Calculator

  1. Enter your Resting Heart Rate (RHR), best measured after waking up.
  2. Choose your desired workout intensity (e.g., 70% for vigorous exercise).
  3. Select whether you know your Maximum Heart Rate (MHR). If not, the calculator will ask for your age to estimate it.

Recommended Intensity Zones

  • Light Exercise (50-60%): Ideal for warm-ups, cool-downs, and active recovery.
  • Fat-Burning Zone (60-80%): Optimal for weight loss and improving general cardiovascular fitness.
  • High-Intensity Zone (80-90%): Best for boosting performance and endurance.
