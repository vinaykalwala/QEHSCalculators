About the STONE Score

The STONE nephrolithometry scoring system is a tool used to predict the likelihood of a patient being stone-free after a single Percutaneous Nephrolithotomy (PCNL) procedure. The score is calculated from five specific parameters found on a preoperative CT scan.

The 5 Scoring Parameters

  • (S) Stone Size: The surface area of the stone. Larger stones receive more points.
  • (T) Tract Length: The distance from the skin to the stone. Longer tracts (>100 mm) are more challenging and score higher.
  • (O) Obstruction: The degree of kidney swelling (hydronephrosis). Moderate to severe obstruction scores higher.
  • (N) Number of Calyces: The number of kidney branches containing stones. More involved calyces or a full staghorn stone increase the score.
  • (E) Essence/Density: The hardness of the stone in Hounsfield Units (HU). Denser stones (>950 HU) are harder to break and score higher.

Important Note

This calculator uses the STONE nephrolithometry scoring system, designed to estimate the success rate of Percutaneous Nephrolithotomy (PCNL), a surgical procedure for kidney stone removal.

Parameter Details:

  • Stone size (mm¬≤): Calculated by multiplying the longest length and width of the stone on a CT scan. Larger stones are harder to remove entirely.
  • Tract length: The distance from the skin to the stone. Longer tracts are technically more challenging during surgery.
  • Obstruction (hydronephrosis): Indicates if the kidney is swollen due to blocked urine. Severe obstruction complicates treatment.
  • Number of calyces with calculi: More affected calyces indicate a more complex stone pattern. A staghorn stone fills much of the kidney.
  • Stone density (Hounsfield Units): Measured on a CT scan; denser stones are harder to break during surgery.

The total score ranges from 5 to 13, and a lower score indicates a higher chance of being stone-free after PCNL. This tool is informational and does not replace evaluation by a healthcare professional.

Percutaneous Nephrolithotomy (PCNL)

PCNL is typically used for large stones (>1.5 cm) or when less-invasive methods fail. The procedure is usually performed under general anesthesia.

Steps Involved:

  • Insertion of the sheath: A small incision is made to reach the kidney's calyx.
  • Insertion of instruments: Includes a telescope for visualization, laser or tools to fragment the stone, and devices to remove pieces.
  • Drainage placement: A nephrostomy tube and/or ureteral stent may be placed for urine flow, removed during follow-ups.

Potential Complications:

  • Bleeding
  • Injury to kidney or nearby organs
  • Infection
  • Incomplete stone removal

Interpreting the Score and Probability

The total score ranges from 5 to 13. A lower score indicates a less complex case and is associated with a higher chance of a stone-free outcome after the first PCNL procedure. This calculator maps the score to its corresponding success probability.
