📘 About This Calculator

The Epworth Sleepiness Scale (ESS) is a simple screening tool designed to assess daytime sleepiness. This calculator uses the 8-question scale to generate a score based on your likelihood of dozing off in various common situations. It helps provide a quantifiable measure of your general level of sleepiness, which can be a useful starting point for discussing sleep health with a provider.

⚙ Parameters Used

The calculator is based on your responses to the 8 situations in the ESS questionnaire. Each response is assigned a point value:

  • 0: Would never doze
  • 1: Slight chance of dozing
  • 2: Moderate chance of dozing
  • 3: High chance of dozing

The total score is the sum of these points, ranging from 0 to 24. The interpretation of the total score is as follows:

  • 0–6: Normal Sleepiness
  • 7–10: Higher Normal Sleepiness
  • 11–14: Mild Excessive Daytime Sleepiness
  • 15–18: Moderate Excessive Daytime Sleepiness
  • 19–24: Severe Excessive Daytime Sleepiness

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • ✔️ Each of the 8 questions must be answered before a score can be calculated.
  • ✔️ Responses must correspond to the provided options, with numeric values strictly between 0 and 3.
  • ✔️ The calculator will not accept blank fields, negative numbers, or values greater than 3.
  • ✔️ If any question is unanswered or contains an invalid entry, an error message will be displayed to guide the user.
  • ✔️ The final score will always be a whole number between 0 and 24.

Output Validations

Based on your total score, the calculator provides a specific assessment. The feedback displayed with the result corresponds to the following categories:

  • Score 0-6: "Normal Daytime Sleepiness: Your score suggests you are likely getting enough quality sleep."
  • Score 7-10: "Higher Normal Daytime Sleepiness: Your score is in the upper range of normal. It might be beneficial to review your sleep habits."
  • Score 11-14: "Mild Excessive Daytime Sleepiness: You may be experiencing mild sleepiness. Consider speaking with a healthcare provider."
  • Score 15-18: "Moderate Excessive Daytime Sleepiness: It is recommended to consult a healthcare provider or sleep specialist for evaluation."
  • Score 19-24: "Severe Excessive Daytime Sleepiness: It is strongly recommended to seek a professional evaluation from a sleep specialist."

Governing Principle

An Epworth Sleepiness Scale (ESS) Calculator is based on the 1997 version of the ESS questionnaire, which is the standardized version for most adults. The ESS itself is a widely recognized and validated tool developed by Dr. Murray Johns, with the 1997 update enhancing its reliability by ensuring most respondents complete all questions.

Here's a breakdown of the standard:

  • Development: The original ESS was created in 1990 and revised in 1997.
  • Standardization: The 1997 revision made it the standard version for adult use.
  • Purpose: It is a standardized tool to measure a person's usual level of daytime sleepiness.
  • Validation: The ESS is a reliable and widely used method for assessing daytime sleepiness.

Therefore, an ESS calculator will use the 1997 version of the questionnaire to ensure it aligns with the established standard.

Conclusion

The Epworth Sleepiness Scale (ESS) is not a substitute for a professional evaluation or diagnosis. This tool is adapted from validated resources like the ESS and other sleep health frameworks. Your experience may differ, and only a professional can offer personalized advice. If you have concerns about your sleep patterns or excessive daytime sleepiness, please consult with a healthcare provider. Always prioritize reaching out to a medical or sleep specialist for any concerns you may have.
