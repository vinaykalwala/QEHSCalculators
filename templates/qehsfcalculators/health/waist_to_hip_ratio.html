About Waist-to-Hip Ratio (WHR)

The Waist-to-Hip Ratio (WHR) is a simple measurement used to assess body fat distribution. A higher WHR is often linked to having more visceral fat (fat stored around the abdominal organs), which is a significant risk factor for cardiovascular disease and type 2 diabetes.

WHR Health Risk Categories

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), the health risk is categorized as follows:

How to Measure Correctly

  • Waist: Measure at the narrowest point between your last rib and the top of your hip bones (iliac crest), typically just above the navel.
  • Hip: Measure around the widest part of your buttocks or hips.
  • Ensure the tape measure is level and snug, but not compressing the skin.
