About Threshold Limit Value (TLV)

The Threshold Limit Value (TLV) is a guideline for the level of a hazardous substance a worker can be exposed to day after day without adverse health effects. This calculator determines the Time-Weighted Average (TWA) over a standard 8-hour workday, which is a common way to express TLV.

Difference Between TLV and WEL

FeatureThreshold Limit Value (TLV)Workplace Exposure Limit (WEL)
DefinitionA recommended exposure limit set by ACGIH.A legally enforceable exposure limit set by HSE (UK).
Regulatory StatusGuideline (not legally binding).Legally enforceable in workplaces.
JurisdictionUsed internationally, primarily in the US and Canada.Used in the UK and parts of Europe.

How to Interpret the Result

The calculated value represents the equivalent concentration of the chemical if the exposure had been spread evenly over an 8-hour shift. This TWA value should be compared to the official TLV-TWA for the specific chemical in question (published by organizations like ACGIH) to determine if the exposure is within safe limits.
