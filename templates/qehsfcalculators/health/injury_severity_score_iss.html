About the Injury Severity Score (ISS)

The Injury Severity Score (ISS) is a medical score used to assess trauma severity. It helps determine how critical a patient's condition is by evaluating injuries across six body regions. A higher score indicates more severe trauma.

How the ISS is Calculated

The score is calculated by taking the top three highest Abbreviated Injury Scale (AIS) scores from the six body regions, squaring each, and summing the result. The maximum possible score is 75. If any single injury is rated a 6 ("Unsurvivable"), the total ISS is automatically assigned as 75.

Interpretation of the ISS Score

  • 1-8: Minor Injury
  • 9-15: Moderate Injury
  • 16-24: Severe Injury
  • ≥ 25: Profound (Very Severe) Trauma

An ISS score greater than 15 is generally considered major trauma.
