About this Calculator

Hemoglobin A1c (HbA1c) is a form of hemoglobin with sugar (glucose) attached. It reflects the average blood sugar levels over the past 2-3 months. A higher A1c indicates elevated blood sugar levels, increasing the risk of diabetes-related complications.

When Should You Get Your A1c Test?

You should consider testing your A1c levels if:

  • You're over 45 years old or under 45 and overweight.
  • You have a family history of diabetes.
  • You engage in low physical activity (less than 3 times a week).
  • You have high blood pressure or cholesterol levels.
  • You have a history of gestational diabetes or delivered a baby weighing over 9 pounds.

Risks of Chronic High Blood Glucose

Consistently high A1c levels may lead to serious health complications, including:

  • Increased risk of heart disease and stroke.
  • Chronic kidney disease or failure.
  • Nerve damage (neuropathy) and loss of sensation.
  • Retinopathy leading to vision loss.
  • Frequent infections and poor wound healing.

How to Lower Your A1c

Effective strategies to manage and reduce your A1c levels include:

  • Maintain a balanced diet with low sugar and high fiber.
  • Exercise regularly to manage blood glucose.
  • Monitor blood sugar levels consistently.
  • Follow medical advice and prescribed medication.
  • Stay hydrated and manage stress effectively.
