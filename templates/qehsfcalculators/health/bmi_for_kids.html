📘 BMI for Kids: Why It Matters

BMI (Body Mass Index) is used to assess if a child’s weight is within a healthy range based on their age and sex. Since children grow at different rates, BMI is compared using percentiles. This helps screen for potential weight and health-related issues.

📊 BMI Categories for Children

BMI Category Percentile Range
UnderweightBelow 5th percentile
Healthy5th to 85th percentile
Overweight85th to 95th percentile
ObeseAbove 95th percentile

📈 Growth Charts

To determine if a child's BMI is healthy, doctors compare it to standardized BMI charts for children of the same age and gender. A child is generally considered to have a healthy BMI if they fall between the 5th and 85th percentile.

❗ Important Disclaimer

This calculator is a screening tool, not a diagnostic one. The percentile calculation is a simplified estimation. Growth spurts, muscle mass, and body composition can affect BMI. Always consult a pediatrician for a comprehensive health assessment and guidance.
