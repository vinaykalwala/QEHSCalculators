About Sleep Cycles

Sleep is not a uniform state. Throughout the night, your body cycles through different stages, including light sleep, deep sleep, and REM (Rapid Eye Movement) sleep. A complete cycle typically lasts about 90 minutes. Waking up at the end of a cycle, rather than in the middle of a deep sleep stage, helps you feel more refreshed and less groggy.

Recommended Sleep Durations by Age

  • Adults (18-64 years): 7-9 hours (approx. 5-6 cycles)
  • Older Adults (65+ years): 7-8 hours (approx. 5 cycles)
  • Teenagers (14-17 years): 8-10 hours (approx. 5-6 cycles)
