This calculator estimates a patient's 30-day risk of a major cardiac event‚Äîspecifically Myocardial Infarction or Cardiac Arrest (MICA)‚Äîfollowing a non-cardiac surgical procedure. It uses five key clinical variables to help healthcare providers stratify risk and guide perioperative management.

‚öôÔ∏è Parameters Used

The risk score is calculated using five key patient variables, each with a specific weight in the formula:

  • Age: Risk increases with age.
  • Functional Status: A patient's level of independence before surgery (independent, partially dependent, or totally dependent).
  • ASA Class: The American Society of Anesthesiologists' classification of a patient's overall health.
  • Serum Creatinine: A measure of kidney function, with levels >1.5 mg/dL indicating higher risk.
  • Surgery Type: The inherent cardiac risk associated with the specific surgical procedure.

‚úÖ Input Validation Rules

To ensure a reliable result, all fields are required and must contain clinically plausible values:

  • Age must be an integer between 18 and 120 years.
  • Creatinine levels must be within a realistic range of 0.1 to 20.0 mg/dL.
  • Selections for ASA Class, Functional Status, and Surgery Type are limited to the provided dropdown options.
  • An error message will be displayed if any field is empty or contains an invalid entry.

‚úÖ Output Validations

The interpretation provided with the result corresponds to the following risk categories:

  • Low Risk (<0.05%): Routine monitoring and care are appropriate.
  • Moderate Risk (0.05% - 0.14%): Enhanced observation may be considered.
  • Elevated Risk (0.14% - 1.47%): Consider a cardiology consultation and closer monitoring.
  • High Risk (1.47% - 2.60%): Intensive perioperative monitoring is recommended.
  • Very High Risk (2.60% - 7.69%): Post-operative ICU or step-down unit care should be considered.
  • Critical Risk (>7.69%): Urgent intervention and optimization are required. Surgery may need to be reconsidered.

‚öñÔ∏è Governing Principle

The Gupta Perioperative Risk Calculator is not of one particular standard, but rather it is derived from the American College of Surgeons' National Surgical Quality Improvement Program (NSQIP) database. The calculator, also known as the NSQIP-MICA score, uses variables like functional status, creatinine levels, ASA classification, age, and surgery type to predict the probability of perioperative myocardial infarction (MI) or cardiac arrest (CA).

Key Aspects

  • Origin: The model was created using data from the NSQIP database, which is a large prospective database of surgical outcomes.
  • Purpose: It provides an individual-based estimate of the risk for perioperative MI or CA, as well as other adverse cardiac events.
  • Components: The calculator incorporates five key patient variables: functional status, creatinine levels, age, American Society of Anesthesiologists (ASA) classification, and type of surgery.
  • Application: The goal is to help identify patients at high risk for these cardiac events, allowing for more targeted interventions and potentially reducing unnecessary cardiology workups and surgery delays.

üßæ Conclusion

This tool provides a statistical probability and is intended to supplement, not replace, professional clinical judgment. It is most accurate for elective, non-cardiac surgeries and should be interpreted by a qualified healthcare professional within the full clinical context of the patient.
