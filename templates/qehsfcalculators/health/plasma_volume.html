About Plasma Volume

Plasma is the liquid component of blood, making up about 55% of total blood volume. Calculating its volume is crucial for assessing hydration, diagnosing conditions like anemia, and planning treatments in critical care.

Calculation Methods Explained

  • Simple Method: A quick estimate based on average values (70 mL/kg for males, 65 mL/kg for females). Useful when height is unknown.
  • Nadler's Equation: A more precise formula using height and weight, widely used in clinical settings.
  • Lemmens-Bernstein-Brodsky: An advanced method that adjusts for Body Mass Index (BMI), improving accuracy for individuals with varying body compositions.
