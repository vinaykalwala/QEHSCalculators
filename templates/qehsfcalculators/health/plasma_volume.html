Analysis

What is Plasma?

Plasma is the fluid part of blood that transports cells, nutrients, hormones, and waste products. It makes up about 55% of the total blood volume and is primarily composed of water, proteins, and electrolytes.

Why Calculate Plasma Volume?

  • Evaluate hydration levels and detect dehydration or fluid overload.
  • Assist in diagnosing conditions like anemia or polycythemia.
  • Support treatment planning in critical care, dialysis, or surgery.
  • Monitor changes during athletic training or medical treatments.

How Plasma Volume is Calculated

Plasma volume is estimated by calculating the Total Blood Volume (TBV) and then determining the fraction that is plasma. This requires body weight, hematocrit percentage, and in some cases, height.

Total Blood Volume (TBV) Estimation Methods

  • Simple Method: Uses body weight only, convenient when height is unknown.
  • Nadler‚Äôs Equation: Considers both height and weight, more accurate for medical use.
  • Lemmens-Bernstein-Brodsky: Adjusts values for BMI differences, useful for varied body types.

Inputs Required

  • Weight (kg): Required for all methods.
  • Height (cm): Needed for Nadler & Lemmens methods.
  • Hematocrit (%): Determines plasma fraction of TBV.

Understanding the Results

  • Low plasma volume: Possible dehydration or blood loss.
  • High plasma volume: Could indicate fluid overload, heart, or kidney disease.
  • Interpret results alongside clinical findings with a healthcare provider.

Limitations of the Calculator

This tool provides estimates and may not be reliable for:

  • Very low or high body weight individuals
  • Children or elderly patients
  • People with unusual hematocrit values or fluid balance disorders
