About

This calculator estimates the flow velocity of water-like fluids in pipes using the Hazen-Williams method. Inputs must use consistent SI units: hydraulic radius (rH) in meters (m), slope (S) as m/m, and C as a dimensionless roughness coefficient. The result is reported as velocity U in m/s, suitable for hydraulic design, safety reviews, and QEHS documentation.

Input validations (summary)

  • C: required; numeric; > 0. Typical: 60–150. Acceptable: 20–300. Warn if >300 or <20.
  • r_H (m): required; numeric; > 0. Typical full-pipe: 0.001–2.0 m. Acceptable: 1×10⁻⁴ – 50 m.
  • S (m/m): required; numeric; > 0. Typical range: 1×10⁻⁸ – 0.1. Acceptable: 1×10⁻¹² – 1. Warn if user likely provided % instead of m/m.
  • Precision: up to 6 decimals for C and r_H; S may use more precision. Results shown to 4 significant digits.
  • Cross-checks: large U (>3 m/s) and/or very small r_H commonly indicate unit errors (mm → m) or slope in % → check inputs.

QEHS & Fire-Safety considerations

  • Low velocity may cause sedimentation and reduce firefighting effectiveness.
  • High velocity increases erosion/corrosion risk—inspect pipe integrity and support systems.
  • Log all inputs, units, computed U and warnings for audits and incident investigations.

Applications

  • Design and assessment of water distribution and fire protection piping.
  • Hydraulic checks for irrigation, water utilities, and building systems.
  • Preliminary screening for pipe erosion risk and sedimentation potential.
