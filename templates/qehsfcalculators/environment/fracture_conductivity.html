Assessment & Suggestions

📘 About

It screens whether the created/propped fracture provides sufficient flow capacity relative to the reservoir matrix.

✅ Input Validations

  • kf (md): required, finite, 0.001–1×106. Typical proppant packs 10–100,000 md.
  • wf (ft): required, finite, 0.0001–1. Typical created width 0.001–0.1 ft.
  • ke (md): required, finite, 1×10−6–10,000. Typical 0.001–1,000 md.
  • xf (ft): required, finite, 0.1–10,000. Typical 50–2,000 ft.
  • Cross-checks: Enforce wf < xf (geometric realism); warn if wf/xf > 5%.
  • Output sanity: FCD must be finite and in (0, 1×106]; warn if > 1,000 (possible overdesign/units mix).

🔎 Interpretation

  • FCD < 1 — under-conductive fracture; likely width/pack-limited.
  • 1 ≤ FCD < 10 — adequate to good conductivity for many reservoirs.
  • 10 ≤ FCD < 50 — strong conductivity; diminishing returns.
  • ≥ 50 — very high; check economics, proppant damage, and units.

🛡️ QEHS / Process-Safety Perspective

  • Higher conductivity can drive higher rates/pressures; verify well integrity and surface equipment limits.
  • Document fluid/proppant types and chemical handling; manage flowback/produced fluids per permits.
  • Record assumptions (temperature, stress, closure) for audits; aging/crushing may reduce kf.

🛑 Limitations

  • Ignores non-Darcy effects, multiphase flow, stress-dependent width, and proppant crushing.
  • Assumes matching units (md with md, ft with ft) so dimensions cancel.
  • Screening tool; use reservoir simulation for design decisions.
