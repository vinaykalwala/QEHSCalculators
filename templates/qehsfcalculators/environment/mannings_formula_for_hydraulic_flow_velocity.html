📘 About This Calculator

This calculator estimates the flow velocity in open channels using Manning’s method. It is commonly applied in open channel design, drainage systems, and flood routing studies.

📊 Interpretation (guidance)

  • U < 0.2 m/s → Low velocity; risk of sedimentation.
  • 0.2 ≤ U ≤ 2.0 m/s → Typical operational range for many channels.
  • 2.0 < U ≤ 5.0 m/s → High velocity; potential erosion/scour — review lining and protection.
  • U > 5.0 m/s → Very high; strong erosion risk and potential structural damage — investigate inputs.

⚠️ Input Validations

  • ✔ Hydraulic radius (rH): 0.001–100 m (meters)
  • ✔ Slope (S): 0–1 (dimensionless). Typical engineering S < 0.1 — values >0.1 trigger warnings.
  • ✔ Manning n: 0.001–2.0 (dimensionless). Typical n: 0.01–0.1.
  • ✔ Decimal recommendations: rH ≤4 dp; S, n ≤6 dp. Excessive decimals raise a warning.
  • ✔ Velocity cap: results >50 m/s flagged as invalid (likely input error).

🌍 Applications

  • Stormwater and drainage design
  • River and canal hydraulics
  • Flood routing and risk assessment
  • Erosion and scour evaluation
  • Firewater conveyance planning
