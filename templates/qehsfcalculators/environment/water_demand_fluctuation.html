📘 About This Calculator

The Water Demand Fluctuation Calculator determines different levels of water demand which are essential for designing and managing water supply systems. Water demand fluctuates daily, seasonally, and even hourly, requiring proper planning to avoid shortages or inefficiencies.

📝 Parameters

  • Total Annual Water Consumption: Total water used in one year (liters)
  • Population: Number of people served

⚠️ Input Validations

  • ✔️ Total Annual Water Consumption must be a numeric, positive value
  • ✔️ Minimum Water Consumption: 1,000 liters (smallest meaningful system)
  • ⚠️ Water Consumption < 10,000 liters → warning (very small system)
  • ⚠️ Water Consumption > 1 billion liters → warning (very large system)
  • ✔️ Population must be a numeric, positive integer
  • ✔️ Minimum Population: 1 person
  • ⚠️ Population < 10 people → warning (very small community)
  • ⚠️ Population > 1 million people → warning (very large city)
  • ❌ Zero, negative, or non-numeric inputs are invalid
  • ⚠️ Average Daily Demand < 50 L/person/day → warning (below WHO minimum recommendation)
  • ⚠️ Average Daily Demand > 400 L/person/day → warning (extremely high consumption)

📊 Output Validations

    • ✅ "Your water consumption is efficient. This is below the typical range of 100-200 liters per person per day." — when Average Daily Demand < 100 L/person/day.
    • ⚠️ "Your water consumption is within the typical range of 100-200 liters per person per day." — when 100 ≤ Average Daily Demand ≤ 200 L/person/day.
    • 🔴 "Your water consumption is higher than typical. Consider implementing water conservation measures." — when Average Daily Demand > 200 L/person/day.
    • Avg Daily Demand < 50 → Below WHO minimum
    • Avg Daily Demand > 400 → Extremely high
    • Total Water < 1,000 L → Very low input
    • Total Water > 1B L → Unusually large input
    • Population < 10 → Very small community
    • Population > 1,000,000 → Very large city

📏 Standards & Compliance

  • WHO recommends 50–100 L/person/day as a minimum standard
  • Urban design often assumes 100–200 L/person/day consumption
  • Peak factors (1.8 daily, 2.7 hourly) align with CPHEEO Manual (India) & common utility practice
  • Used in ISO 24512 (drinking water service guidelines)

🔚 Conclusion

This calculator estimates average, maximum, and peak water demand to support urban water supply design, planning, and conservation measures. Results help identify whether usage is efficient, typical, or excessive based on global and national standards.
