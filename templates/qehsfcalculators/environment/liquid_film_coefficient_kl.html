📘 About This Calculator

KL (m/s) represents the mass transfer rate per unit concentration difference across a stagnant liquid film. It is computed by dividing the diffusion coefficient (m²/s) by the film thickness (m).

⚠️ Input Validations

  • Diffusion Coefficient (D): required, numeric, > 0. Hard limits: 1e-12 ≤ D ≤ 1e-2 m²/s. Typical: 1e-9 – 1e-4 m²/s.
  • Film Thickness (Y): required, numeric, > 0. Hard limits: 1e-6 ≤ Y ≤ 1.0 m. Typical: 1e-4 – 1e-2 m (0.1–10 mm).
  • Precision: avoid excessive decimals (warn if > 9 decimal places).
  • Units: inputs must be SI (m²/s and m). Out-of-range inputs produce warnings suggesting unit checks.
  • Derived KL: values outside the plausible band are rejected (KL ≤ 0 or KL > 1.0 m/s). Warnings for KL < 1e-8 or KL > 0.5.

🌍 Key Applications

  • Wastewater Treatment: Evaluating oxygen diffusion in aeration tanks.
  • Gas Absorbers: Designing absorber columns for scrubbing operations.
  • Aquatic Systems: Studying gas exchange in lakes, rivers, or aquariums.
  • Environmental Monitoring: Modeling pollutant diffusion in water bodies.
  • Industrial Mass Transfer: Optimizing gas–liquid reactors and bioreactors.
