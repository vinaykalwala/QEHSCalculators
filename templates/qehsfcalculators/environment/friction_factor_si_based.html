Assessment & Suggestions

📘 About

This calculator estimates the turbulent-flow friction factor using an empirical approximation for smooth pipes (no roughness term). Applicable only when Re ≥ 4,000. For design-grade work or rough pipes, use methods that include relative roughness (e.g., Colebrook–White/Haaland).

✅ Input Validations

  • Re is required, finite, and ≥ 4,000 (turbulent regime).
  • Allowed numeric formats: integers or decimals; scientific notation permitted by browsers.
  • Hard bounds for practicality: 4,000 ≤ Re ≤ 1×10⁹ (warn above 1×10⁷ as “extreme”).
  • Result sanity: computed f must be finite and within (0, 0.2); otherwise inputs are inconsistent.

🛡️ QEHS & Fire Safety Perspective

  • Use to quickly gauge frictional losses that impact pump head, hydrant flows, and sprinkler/standpipe performance.
  • High uncertainty if pipe roughness/ageing is significant—treat results as screening, not final design.
  • For critical firewater systems, confirm with roughness-inclusive correlations and field data.

🛑 Limitations

  • Applies to turbulent flow only; not valid for laminar/transitional regimes.
  • Assumes smooth pipe; no explicit roughness term.
  • Does not distinguish between Darcy and Fanning conventions; use consistently within your calculations.
