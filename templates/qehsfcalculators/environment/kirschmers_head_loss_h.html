📘 About

This calculator determines head loss across bar racks using Kirschmer’s equation, which accounts for velocity head, bar geometry, and rack inclination. Useful in water/wastewater intake design, firewater strainers, and treatment plant headworks.

✅ Input Validations

  • Velocity (v): Must be > 0 and ≤ 5 m/s (typical 0.3–2 m/s for screens).
  • Angle (θ): Must be between 30° and 90° (common design 45–75°).
  • Bar Width (W): Must be > 0 and ≤ 0.2 m (20 cm max practical).
  • Bar Spacing (b): Must be > 0 and ≤ 0.5 m. Also requires W < b.
  • Shape Factor (K): Chosen from dropdown (1.67 – 2.42 typical range).
  • Gravity (g): Assumed 9.81 m/s², constant.
  • Output (h): Displayed in meters of head loss. Values > 2 m flagged as excessive for intakes.

🔎 Head Loss Classification

  • < 0.1 m: Negligible loss, excellent performance.
  • 0.1 – 0.3 m: Normal range for well-designed screens.
  • 0.3 – 1.0 m: Elevated loss, monitor system design.
  • > 1.0 m: High loss, redesign or cleaning recommended.

🛑 Limitations

  • Applies to bar racks; not valid for mesh screens or perforated plates.
  • Assumes steady, uniform approach flow.
  • Does not account for clogging/debris accumulation.
  • Neglects secondary losses from channel geometry or turbulence.
