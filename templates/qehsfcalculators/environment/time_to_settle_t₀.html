📘 About This Calculator

The Time to Settle Calculator determines how long particles take to settle through a sedimentation basin, helping engineers design efficient water treatment systems and ensure regulatory compliance.

📐 Parameters Used

  • Z₀ (Depth of Basin): Vertical depth of sedimentation basin. Units: m or ft.
  • v₀ (Terminal Settling Velocity): Velocity at which particles settle. Units: m/s, cm/s, ft/s.
  • t₀ (Time to Settle): Calculated as depth divided by velocity. Units: seconds, minutes, or hours.
  • Constraints: Depth ≥ 0.1 m, Velocity ≥ 0.0001 m/s for valid results.

⚠️ Input Validation Rules

  • ✔️ Depth of Basin (Z₀) must be a positive numeric value.
  • ✔️ Minimum Depth: 0.1 m (practical sedimentation basin depth).
  • ⚠️ Maximum Depth: 10 m → triggers warning (unusually deep basin).
  • ✔️ Terminal Settling Velocity (v₀) must be a positive numeric value.
  • ✔️ Minimum Velocity: 0.0001 m/s (very fine particles).
  • ⚠️ Maximum Velocity: 0.1 m/s → triggers warning (very fast settling).
  • ❌ Zero, negative, or non-numeric inputs are invalid.
  • ⚠️ Extremely short settling time < 1 second → warning (verify inputs).
  • ⚠️ Extremely long settling time > 24 hours → warning (impractical for treatment).

📊 Output Validations

  • t₀ < 10 minutes ✅ Fast settling → Particles settle quickly, indicating an efficient sedimentation process. This is ideal for most treatment applications.
  • 10 ≤ t₀ ≤ 60 minutes ⚠️ Moderate settling → Particles settle at a moderate rate. This may be acceptable but could potentially be optimized for better efficiency.
  • t₀ > 60 minutes 🔴 Slow settling → Particles take too long to settle, which may indicate inefficient design or inappropriate particle characteristics. Consider redesigning the basin or using coagulants to improve settling.
