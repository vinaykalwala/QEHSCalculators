A pitot tube velocity calculator is based on established engineering standards, primarily ISO 3966 (Measurement of fluid flow in closed conduits ‚Äî Velocity area method using Pitot static tubes) and national standards such as the Indian Standard IS 14973. These standards provide methods for measuring fluid flow and velocity using pitot tubes, detailing the technology, calculations, and conditions for applying the velocity-area method for accurate results in closed conduits.

Key Standards and Their Focus:

  • ISO 3966: International standard specifying methods for Pitot tube measurements, calculating local velocities from differential pressures, applicable to fluids of constant density, low Mach numbers (<0.25), and steady flow.

  • EPA Method 2: U.S. Environmental Protection Agency procedure for stack gas velocity and volumetric flow rate using standard pitot tubes, referencing calibration standards such as NIST.
