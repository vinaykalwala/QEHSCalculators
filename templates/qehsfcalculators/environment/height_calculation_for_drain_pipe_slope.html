Assessment & Suggestions

📘 About

This calculator computes the required drain pipe exit/invert height H₁ for a slope-based drainage system. The calculation considers a fixed base height allowance plus an increment proportional to the distance (D).

✅ Input Validations

  • Distance (D) required, numeric, >= 0.1 m (hard minimum)
  • Distance > 0 and decimals allowed (up to 2 decimals recommended)
  • Distances > 200 m flagged as unusual (warning); > 2000 m rejected (error)
  • Computed H₁ > 5 m flagged as high (warning); > 50 m rejected (error)

🛠 QEHS / Practical Notes

  • Use calibrated surveys for D. Incorrect D or unit mismatch (feet vs meters) can cause major design errors.
  • Ensure H₁ meets maintenance and access requirements — excessive invert heights may be impractical.
  • Verify that 1% slope meets local standards for oil drainage; adjust in design if local codes require steeper slopes.

🌍 Key Applications

  • Transformer Oil Pits: Ensures safe drainage of oil spillages into soak pits.
  • Fire Safety Compliance: Prevents standing oil that could become a fire hazard.
  • Environmental Protection: Reduces risk of soil and groundwater contamination.
  • Infrastructure Reliability: Maintains proper slope to avoid blockages or backflow.
  • Industrial Sites: Used in power plants, substations, and chemical storage areas for safe drainage.
