✅ Input Validations

  • Relative Density is required — cannot be left blank
  • Must be a positive finite number
  • Minimum value: 0.90 (highly improbable for aqueous solutions)
  • Maximum value: 1.20 (saturated brine solutions)
  • Values outside typical ranges (0.99-1.05) trigger warnings
  • Must be measured at exactly 15.5°C for accurate results

📊 Water Quality Classification

  • TDS < 500 ppm → ✅ Excellent (Ideal for drinking)
  • 500-1000 ppm → ⚠️ Good (Acceptable for drinking)
  • 1000-2000 ppm → ⚠️ Fair (May affect taste)
  • 2000-3000 ppm → ❌ Poor (Not recommended for drinking)
  • TDS > 3000 ppm → ❌ Unacceptable (Unsuitable for drinking)

🌍 Key Applications

  • Drinking Water Quality: Assessing safety and taste of potable water
  • Environmental Monitoring: Evaluating health of rivers, lakes, and groundwater
  • Industrial Processes: Monitoring water quality in boilers, cooling systems, and manufacturing
  • Agriculture: Determining irrigation water suitability
  • Aquaculture: Maintaining optimal conditions for aquatic life

🛑 Limitations & Important Notes

  • Measurement must be taken at exactly 15.5°C (60°F) for accuracy
  • Different water compositions may require specific conversion factors
  • Does not distinguish between different types of dissolved solids
  • For precise measurements, laboratory analysis is recommended
  • Extreme values may indicate measurement errors or unusual water composition
