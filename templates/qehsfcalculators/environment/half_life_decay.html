About

Uses the half-life exponential decay model. Provide an initial quantity and the half-life in the same time units as the elapsed time. The calculator reports remaining quantity, fraction remaining, and number of half-lives elapsed. Record units and timestamp for audits.

Input Validations

  • N₀: required; numeric; > 0; typ. range 1e-12 – 1e30 (reject clearly unrealistic magnitudes as likely unit errors).
  • τ (half-life): required; numeric; > 0; typical range accepted: 1e-9 – 1e9 (units flexible; must match t).
  • t (elapsed time): required; numeric; ≥ 0; same units as τ; very large t/τ will produce effectively zero remaining.
  • Precision: up to 6 decimals accepted; outputs show fixed or exponential notation depending on magnitude.
  • Cross-checks: If t = 0 → N = N₀. Warn if t/τ is extremely large (>1e6) or extremely small (<1e-6).

Applications

  • Radioactive material storage planning and clearance scheduling.
  • Estimating environmental pollutant persistence for remediation planning.
  • Pharmacokinetics: drug clearance and dosing interval planning.
  • Industrial safety audits and record keeping for hazardous materials.

QEHS considerations

  • Always log N₀, τ, t, units, computed N, %, timestamp and any warnings for compliance and investigations.
  • Watch for unit mismatches (e.g., τ in seconds while t in hours) — this is the most frequent source of error.
  • When computed N is extremely small (< detection limit), record as “Below detection / effectively zero” and log detection limit.
