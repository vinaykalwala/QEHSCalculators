📘 About This Calculator

This tool estimates the total fresh air supply needed to maintain indoor air quality based on the number of occupants and the per-person ventilation rate. Results are displayed in liters per second (L/s) and cubic metres per hour (m³/h) for practical HVAC design and QEHS review.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • Number of occupants (N)
  • Per-person ventilation rate (R) [L/s per person]
  • Total ventilation rate (calculated) [L/s & m³/h]

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Occupants (N): numeric, ≥1; if decimal → rounded to nearest person; very high values (>100,000) trigger warning.
  • Per-person ventilation rate (R): numeric, ≥0.1 L/s; typical ranges: 5–50 L/s per person depending on environment; extreme values trigger warnings.
  • Both inputs must be finite numbers; excessively large values may indicate unit or data-entry errors.

🧾 Output Validations & Feedback

  • Very large total ventilation (>100,000 L/s or >360,000 m³/h) triggers warnings for system feasibility.
  • Assessment / Feedback is displayed along with the results:
    • R(Per-person ventilation rate ) ≥ 30 → High per-person ventilation — suitable for labs or critical spaces
    • R(Per-person ventilation rate ) 20–30 → Above-typical ventilation — industrial / heavy processes
    • R(Per-person ventilation rate ) 10–20 → Typical ventilation for offices / general areas
    • R(Per-person ventilation rate ) 6–10 → Low-moderate ventilation — may be acceptable with additional controls
    • R < 6 → Low ventilation — likely insufficient for offices; review IAQ requirements

📐 Governing Principle

A Required Ventilation Rate Calculator for Indoor Air Quality is based on the ASHRAE Standard 62.1 (Ventilation for Acceptable Indoor Air Quality), which sets the minimum ventilation rates and methods for achieving acceptable IAQ in non-residential buildings, or ASHRAE Standard 62.2, which applies to residential buildings. These standards specify procedures for calculating ventilation airflow to minimize health effects from contaminants.

  • Key Standards:
    ANSI/ASHRAE Standard 62.1: applies to non-residential buildings and provides procedures for designers and engineers to determine the required ventilation rates to maintain acceptable indoor air quality.
    ANSI/ASHRAE Standard 62.2: applies to residential buildings, specifying the required whole-building ventilation airflow rates to meet IAQ requirements.
  • Purpose of These Standards:
    Define Minimum Rates: establish minimum ventilation rates for various spaces and activities to ensure adequate fresh air.
    Health and Comfort: control contaminants and minimize adverse health effects for occupants.
    Design Procedures: provide calculation methods, including the Ventilation Rate Procedure and Indoor Air Quality Procedure, for calculating outdoor air requirements.

📌 Conclusion

Recommended references include ASHRAE Standard 62.1, ISO 16890 for indoor air quality assessment, and internal plant SOPs for HVAC design, airflow distribution, and safety audits. Following these standards ensures the ventilation system delivers sufficient fresh air to maintain IAQ, occupant health, and energy efficiency.
