üìñ Governing Principle

A "Rankine's Equation" specifically refers to an empirical formula for calculating trickling filter efficiency in wastewater treatment, not to a specific standard like ISO 16889 for filter efficiency testing. While there isn't an official "Rankine's Standard" for this type of calculation, the equation is a common tool in environmental engineering.

Understanding the Context

Rankine's Equation: This is an empirical formula, meaning it's based on experimental observations and data, to estimate the efficiency of trickling filters in removing BOD (Biochemical Oxygen Demand) in wastewater treatment.

No Official Standard: It does not establish a standardized test method or define a specific rating system for filter performance, unlike standards like ISO 16889 or ISO 16890 which are used for general filter testing and classification.

Application: The equation is used in the design and analysis of trickling filters, which are biological treatment systems, particularly for high-rate filters.

In summary: If you encounter "Rankine's Equation - Filter Efficiency Calculator," it refers to the application of the Rankine equation in a calculator tool, not a filter standard. You'd need to look at different standards for standardized testing and rating of filters, depending on the type of filter and application.
