📘 About This Calculator

This calculator estimates kinematic viscosity from dynamic viscosity and density in SI units. It is used across QEHS, water/wastewater, fuels, and firewater systems to assess fluidity, pumping feasibility, and to benchmark against product specifications and audit criteria.

📊 Interpretation (cSt)

  • < 1 cSt → ✅ Very low (solvents/light fuels)
  • 1–10 cSt → ✅ Typical (water at ~1 cSt; many fuels 2–8 cSt)
  • 10–100 cSt → ⚠️ Medium (light–medium oils; rising headloss)
  • > 100 cSt → 🔴 High (pumping/heating considerations; check OEM limits)

Always compare with OEM design limits, MSDS/spec sheets, and site procedures.

⚠️ Input Validations (Summary)

  • μ (Pa·s) > 0 (hard reject if ≤ 0). Advisory if μ < 0.0002 or > 10.
  • ρ (kg/m³) > 0 (hard reject if ≤ 0). Advisory if ρ < 500 or > 1500.
  • Units must be SI (Pa·s and kg/m³). Suspected unit mix triggers warnings.
  • Result plausibility: reject if ν < 0.01 cSt or ν > 100,000 cSt for liquid service.
  • Reporting precision: ≤ 3 decimals for inputs; output shown to 4 decimals.

🌍 Key Applications

  • Firewater & Hose Reels: Confirm water-like viscosity (≈1 cSt) for pump/NPSH checks.
  • Fuels & Hydraulics: Verify grade vs. temperature; protect pumps/valves.
  • Lubrication: Ensure viscosity class (ISO VG) suitability under operating temps.
