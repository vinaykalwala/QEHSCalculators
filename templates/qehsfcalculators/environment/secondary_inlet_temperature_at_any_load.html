📘 About This Calculator

Determines the secondary fluid inlet temperature (Tₓ) for heat exchangers at any load. Critical for QEHS compliance, thermal efficiency, and fire safety in industrial systems.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • T₁: Secondary inlet temperature at full load [°C]
  • T₂: Secondary outlet temperature at full load [°C]
  • x: Load factor, fraction of full load (0–1)
  • Tₓ: Calculated secondary inlet temperature at any load [°C]

✅ Input Validations

  • All inputs must be numeric and provided.
  • T₁ must be ≥ 0°C and ≤ 600°C (typical industrial boiler limit).
  • T₂ must be ≥ 0°C and ≤ T₁ (inlet ≥ outlet for safe heat transfer).
  • Load factor x must be ≥ 0 and ≤ 1.
  • Non-integer temperatures allowed; decimals rounded to 2 decimal places in output.
  • Extreme temperatures or load factors trigger warnings for QEHS audit checks.
  • Logical consistency: T₂ cannot exceed T₁, x within 0–1.

⚙ Output Validations / Assessment

  • Tₓ is displayed in °C with 2 decimals.
  • Assessment messages displayed with result:
    • ✅ Tₓ within safe operating range → system safe and efficient.
    • ⚠️ Tₓ < 0°C → inputs may be unsafe; check system configuration.
    • ⚠️ Tₓ > T₁ → physically impossible; verify inputs.
    • ⚠️ High T₁ (>500°C) or T₂ (>T₁) → potential fire/thermal risk; verify QEHS compliance.

📏 Standards / Safety Reference

Based on ISO 50001 (energy efficiency), ISO 8501 (industrial heat exchange safety), and QEHS thermal safety protocols.

📝 Conclusion

  • Accurate T₁, T₂, and x inputs ensure safe and efficient Tₓ values.
  • Following input validations and warnings prevents unsafe heat exchanger operation.
  • This tool supports QEHS audits, thermal efficiency checks, and fire safety compliance.
