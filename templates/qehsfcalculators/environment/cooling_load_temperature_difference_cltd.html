📘 About

This calculator estimates the heat gain through a building surface using the CLTD approach. Provide U-factor in BTU/hr·ft²·°F, surface area in ft², and CLTD in °F (use values appropriate for your climate, orientation and time-of-day).

📏 Units & typical ranges

  • U-Factor: BTU/hr·ft²·°F — typical: 0.02 – 0.2 (very well insulated → high-loss assemblies outside this range trigger warnings).
  • Surface Area: ft² — typical: 1 – 50,000 (roof and large façade areas possible; very small or huge entries will warn).
  • CLTD: °F — typical: 20 – 120 (depends on material, orientation, time-of-day and climate).
  • Result: BTU/hr and tons of cooling (1 ton = 12,000 BTU/hr).

🛡️ Input Validations

  • All fields (U-Factor, Area, CLTD) are required.
  • Values must be numeric and finite.
  • U-Factor must be greater than 0.
  • Surface Area must be greater than 0 ft².
  • CLTD must be greater than 0 °F.
  • Unusually high or low values trigger warnings (but still calculate).
  • Errors block calculation until corrected.

🌍 Applications

  • Building Design: Helps architects and engineers size walls, roofs, and windows for thermal efficiency.
  • HVAC System Sizing: Used to estimate the cooling load needed for selecting air conditioners and chillers.
  • Energy Efficiency Studies: Assists in evaluating insulation performance and reducing energy consumption.
  • Green Building Projects: Supports sustainable design by analyzing heat gains and optimizing materials.
