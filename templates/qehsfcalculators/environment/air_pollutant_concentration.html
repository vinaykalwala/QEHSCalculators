📘 About This Calculator

The Air Pollutant Concentration Calculator converts ppb measurements into mass concentration (µg/m³), considering temperature, pressure, and molecular weight of the pollutant.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • ppb (parts per billion): Input concentration of the pollutant.
  • Pressure (atm): Ambient air pressure.
  • Molecular Weight (g/mol): Weight of the pollutant molecule.
  • Temperature (°C): Ambient temperature (converted to Kelvin internally).
  • Gas Constant (R): 0.0821 L·atm/(mol·K).

✅ Input Validation

  • Concentration (ppb): must be between 0 and 1,000,000
    • 0 → results in 0 µg/m³ (no pollutant detected).
  • Pressure (atm): must be between 0.1 and 10
    • Typical ambient range: 0.8 – 1.2 atm (outside = warning).
  • Molecular Weight (g/mol): must be between 2 and 300
  • Temperature (°C): must be between -273.15 and 200
    • Below -50 °C or above 60 °C → unusual for ambient air (warning).
  • All inputs must be valid numeric values (no text or symbols).

📊 Interpretation

  • Low concentration → Safe for ambient air quality.
  • Moderate concentration → Caution, may affect sensitive groups.
  • High concentration → Health risk, regulatory concern.

🌍 Key Applications

  • Environmental Monitoring: Checking compliance with air quality standards.
  • Industrial Emissions: Estimating pollutant release into the atmosphere.
  • Public Health: Assessing exposure risks from air pollutants.
