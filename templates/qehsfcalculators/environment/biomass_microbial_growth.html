📘 About

This calculator estimates microbial growth rate using the Monod equation with endogenous decay:

📏 Parameters & Units

  • Maximum Specific Growth Rate (μₘ): day⁻¹ (Typical range: 0.05–2.0)
  • Microorganism Concentration (X): mg/L
  • Substrate Concentration (S): mg/L
  • Half-Saturation Coefficient (Kₛ): mg/L (Typical range: 0.1–100)
  • Endogenous Decay Coefficient (kₑ): day⁻¹ (Typical range: 0.01–0.1)

✅ Input Validations

  • μₘ (max growth rate): must be > 0 (typical 0.05–2.0 day⁻¹)
  • X (microorganism conc.): must be > 0 (mg/L)
  • S (substrate conc.): must be ≥ 0 (mg/L)
  • Kₛ (half-saturation): must be ≥ 0 (typical 0.1–100 mg/L)
  • kₑ (decay coefficient): must be ≥ 0 (typical 0.01–0.1 day⁻¹)
  • ⚠️ Error if S and Kₛ are both 0 (division by zero)
  • ⚠️ Warning if inputs exceed realistic ranges → check units

🔎 Interpretation

  • Positive growth rate: Microbial population is growing
  • Zero growth rate: Steady state (growth = decay)
  • Negative growth rate: Population declining (decay exceeds growth)

🛑 Limitations

  • Assumes ideal conditions with single limiting substrate
  • Does not account for inhibition effects at high substrate concentrations
  • Assumes constant parameters throughout growth period
  • Does not consider spatial heterogeneity in real systems
