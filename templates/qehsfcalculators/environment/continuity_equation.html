Assessment & Notes

📘 About

This calculator uses the continuity principle to compute volumetric flow through a cross-section. Provide velocity and area using consistent SI units for accurate results.

✅ Input Validations

  • Required fields: Both Velocity (v) and Area (A) must be entered — empty values are not accepted.
  • Numeric check: Inputs must be finite numbers (no text, symbols, NaN, or infinity).
  • Area validation: A must be > 0. Zero or negative area is physically impossible and blocked.
  • Velocity range: -200 ≤ v ≤ +200 m/s. Negative values indicate reverse flow (allowed, but flagged). Beyond ±200 m/s is implausible for incompressible flow and triggers warnings.

🌍 Applications

  • Water Supply Systems: To calculate and manage water flow in pipelines.
  • HVAC Systems: For estimating airflow in ducts of heating, ventilation, and air conditioning.
  • Hydraulic Engineering: Used in dams, canals, and drainage system design.
  • Industrial Applications: For process control in petrochemical, chemical, and food industries.

🔎 Interpretation

  • Low flow: Q < 0.001 m³/s (~< 1 L/s) — may be slow for some applications.
  • Moderate flow: 0.001 ≤ Q < 0.1 m³/s — typical for many piping systems.
  • High flow: Q ≥ 0.1 m³/s — large industrial or process flows.
