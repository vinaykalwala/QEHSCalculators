📘 About This Calculator

The Municipal Water Quantity Estimator computes total daily water demand by multiplying the per-capita water demand (liters per person per day) by the population served. Use consistent SI units: PCD in liters/day/person, population in persons.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • Per-Capita Demand (PCD) — daily water requirement per person (liters/day/person).
  • Population — total number of people served.
  • Total Water Demand = PCD × Population (liters/day).
  • Conversions: m³/day = Total Liters ÷ 1000, L/s = Total Liters ÷ 86,400

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • PCD: required, numeric, >0, typical 65–300 L/day/person. Warning if <20 or >500. High precision (>3 decimals) → warning.
  • Population: required, numeric, ≥1, integer preferred. Hard-limit ≤1,000,000,000. Fractional → rounded with warning. >10 million → system-level warning.
  • Cross-check: extremely large totals >1×10⁹ L/day → error; totals >1×10⁶ L/day → warning.

📊 Output Validations

  • Total Water Demand displayed in liters/day, m³/day, and L/s.
  • Output must be finite and >0; >1×10⁹ L/day → error, >1×10⁶ → warning, <100 → warning.
  • Per-capita assessment:
    • <65 → Low consumption (possible rationing/water-saving)
    • 65–300 → Typical domestic/urban range
    • >300 → High consumption (possible industrial/process loads)
  • System scale:
    • <10,000 L/day → Small (neighbourhood/small community)
    • 10,000–1,000,000 → Medium (town / small city)
    • >1,000,000 → Large (city / regional system) - verify planning & capacity
  • All assessment/feedback messages are displayed along with the results in the UI.

📐 Governing Principle

A Municipal Water Quantity Estimator relies on national and international standards for per-capita water consumption, such as the National Building Code (NBC) of India and recommendations from CPHEEO, which specify a standard of 135–150 liters/person/day for estimating total water demand.

  • National Standards: NBC and CPHEEO guidelines provide baseline per-capita water requirements.
  • Calculation Method: Total water demand = Rate of consumption per capita per day × Design population.
  • Variations: PCD can be adjusted for living standards, economic conditions, industrial/commercial loads (e.g., 135 L/day for lower-income groups).
  • Other Standards: IAPMO or local municipal regulations may also be referenced, but Indian standards are primary.

🔎 Conclusion

This estimator integrates input validation, water demand computation, and standardized assessment. Feedback on per-capita usage and system scale is displayed along with results, assisting planners in ensuring adequate supply and complying with national/international water consumption standards.
