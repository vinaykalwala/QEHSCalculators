📘 About This Calculator

This calculator estimates the mean steam consumption rate, i.e., the steam mass flow required to achieve a specified heat transfer rate. It is designed for use with consistent SI units: Heat Transfer Rate (Q) in kW (equivalent to kJ/s) and Specific Enthalpy of Evaporation (hfg) in kJ/kg.

⚠️ Input validations

  • Q (kW): required, positive. Typical operational range 1 – 1000 kW. Hard allowed range 0.01 – 10000 kW.
  • hfg (kJ/kg): required, positive. Typical saturated-steam enthalpy ~2000–2500 kJ/kg. Hard allowed range 100 – 10000 kJ/kg.
  • Precision: inputs should be given with ≤4 decimal places for reporting; higher precision triggers a warning.
  • Units: entering Q in kW and hfg in kJ/kg yields kg/s directly. If you use other units convert first.
  • Cross-checks: very low hfg (e.g., <500 kJ/kg) is usually a sign of wrong units (J/kg instead of kJ/kg) — the calculator will warn.

🏭 Applications & QEHS relevance

  • Industrial boilers & steam networks — sizing and boiler capacity planning.
  • Heat exchangers & process heaters — ensuring correct steam supply for reaction or drying duties.
  • HVAC and district heating — balancing steam distribution and condensate return design.
  • QEHS / Fire Safety: over-sized steam use may indicate inefficiency or leak scenarios — high steam use affects pressure/valves, increases fire/steam-burn risks and requires safeguards (pressure relief, insulation, PPE).
