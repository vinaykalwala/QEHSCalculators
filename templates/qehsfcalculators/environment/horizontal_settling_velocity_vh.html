📘 About This Calculator

This calculator estimates fluid horizontal velocity from measured flow rate and cross-sectional area. It supports hydraulic checks in water/wastewater networks, treatment units, and open channels, helping prevent sedimentation at low velocities and erosion or excessive headloss at high velocities.

📊 Interpretation

  • vₕ < 0.30 m/s → ❌ Very low: sedimentation/deposit risk in many systems
  • 0.30 ≤ vₕ ≤ 2.50 m/s → ⚠️ Typical range: balance of transport & headloss
  • 2.50 < vₕ ≤ 3.50 m/s → ⚠️ High: increased headloss/noise, check supports
  • vₕ > 3.50 m/s → 🔴 Very high: potential erosion/cavitation (material-dependent)

⚠️Input Validations

  • ✔️ Q and A are required, numeric, and greater than 0.
  • ✔️ A must not be zero (division by zero).
  • ✔️ Recommended precision: ≤ 3 decimals for reporting.
  • 🛑 Reject if computed vₕ > 20 m/s (implausible for most liquid systems) or non-finite.

🌍 Key Applications

  • Water Supply / Firewater: Verify transport velocity and avoid excessive losses.
  • Wastewater Networks: Check self-cleansing velocities to limit solids build-up.
  • Treatment Units: Balance detention vs. short-circuiting in channels/basins.
  • Industrial Cooling/Process: Control erosion/corrosion risk at high velocities.
