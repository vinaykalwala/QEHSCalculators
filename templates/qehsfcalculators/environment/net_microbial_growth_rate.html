📘 About

This calculator computes the Net Microbial Growth Rate as growth minus endogenous decay:
Useful for environmental microbiology, wastewater treatment, fermentation, and bioprocess optimization.

✅ Input Validations

  • All inputs must be numeric and positive.
  • μm typically 0.01–2 1/hr; extremely high values trigger warnings.
  • X (biomass) realistic range 0.01–50 g/L.
  • S (substrate) realistic range 0.001–100 g/L.
  • Ks must be >0; typically 0.01–10 g/L.
  • kd ≥0; typical 0.001–0.5 1/hr.
  • Division by zero prevented: S + Ks > 0.
  • Warnings issued for biologically implausible combinations (e.g., extremely high growth vs decay).

🔎 Growth Assessment

  • Positive Net Growth → Microbial population increasing.
  • Zero Net Growth → Population stable, substrate limitation or equilibrium.
  • Negative Net Growth → Population declining, decay exceeds growth.

🏭 Applications

  • Wastewater treatment optimization.
  • Industrial fermentation monitoring.
  • Biotechnology process improvement.
  • Environmental microbiology studies.
