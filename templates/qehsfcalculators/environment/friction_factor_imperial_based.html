üìñ Governing Principle

An "Imperial-Based" Friction Factor Calculator follows the Darcy friction factor definition, where the imperial-based friction factor is four times larger than the SI-based friction factor, according to the TRIBOLOGY-ABC and Spirax Sarco websites. This definition is often used in the United States and other regions historically tied to imperial units, as detailed by TRIBOLOGY-ABC and Spirax Sarco.

Understanding the Standard

Darcy Friction Factor: This is the standard for imperial-based calculations, as opposed to the Fanning friction factor, which is more common in SI unit systems.

Relationship to Fanning Factor: The Darcy friction factor is four times greater than the Fanning friction factor for the same flow conditions.

Application: Both types of friction factors are determined using the Moody diagram or the Colebrook equation, but the scale of the Moody diagram differs for imperial units, as noted by TRIBOLOGY-ABC.

Why Imperial-Based?

Historical Usage: Practitioners in the United States and some other regions continue to use the imperial-based definition for pipe flow calculations, notes Spirax Sarco.

Consistency: For consistency within these regions, tools like "Friction Factor Calculators (Imperial-Based)" adhere to the Darcy definition and its associated scales.
