Assessment & Suggestions

📘 About

This calculator estimates the maximum theoretical efficiency of a heat engine using the Carnot efficiency formula. All temperatures must be in Kelvin.

📏 Units & typical ranges

  • Temperature at Turbine Inlet (Tᵢ): Kelvin (K). Typical range: 400-900 K for steam turbines, 800-1600 K for gas turbines.
  • Temperature at Turbine Exhaust (Tₑ): Kelvin (K). Typical range: 300-600 K, depending on cooling conditions.
  • Carnot Efficiency (η): Percentage (%). Theoretical maximum efficiency possible.
  • Note: Real-world efficiencies are always lower than Carnot efficiency due to various losses.

✅ Input Validation

  • Both Tᵢ (inlet temperature) and Tₑ (exhaust temperature) are required.
  • Inputs must be positive finite numbers in Kelvin (K).
  • Tᵢ must be strictly greater than Tₑ for a valid Carnot cycle.
  • Values outside 300 K – 5000 K (for Tᵢ) or 100 K – 2000 K (for Tₑ) trigger warnings.
  • Very small ΔT (< 50 K) triggers a low-efficiency warning.
  • Efficiency is always ≤ 100% — higher values are invalid.

🔎 Interpretation

  • High efficiency (> 60%): Large temperature difference - indicates potential for high performance.
  • Moderate efficiency (30-60%): Reasonable temperature difference - typical for many heat engines.
  • Low efficiency (< 30%): Small temperature difference - may indicate design limitations or poor heat utilization.
  • Very low efficiency (< 10%): Minimal temperature difference - likely impractical for energy conversion.

🛑 Limitations

  • Represents theoretical maximum efficiency, not achievable in practice.
  • Does not account for various real-world losses (friction, heat transfer, etc.).
  • Assumes ideal reversible processes, which don't exist in reality.
  • Actual system efficiency is typically 30-60% of Carnot efficiency.
