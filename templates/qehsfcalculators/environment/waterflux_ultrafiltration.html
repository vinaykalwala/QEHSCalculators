📘 About This Calculator

This calculator determines the water flux across a membrane, a crucial parameter in separation processes like water purification, filtration, and industrial membrane applications.

✅ Input Validations

  • Membrane Porosity (ε): Must be between 0 and 1 (typical range: 0.3-0.8).
  • Membrane Pore Size (r): Must be greater than 0 and typically in micrometers (1×10⁻⁶ m).
  • Net Transmembrane Pressure (ΔP): Must be greater than 0 and typically between 10,000-500,000 Pa.
  • Viscosity (μ): Must be greater than 0; typically 0.001 Pa·s for water at 20°C.
  • Membrane Thickness (δ): Must be greater than 0 and typically in micrometers (1×10⁻⁶ m).

📊 Interpretation

Water flux values can be interpreted as:

  • High flux (> 1×10⁻⁴ m/s): Indicates efficient filtration but may risk membrane damage or fouling.
  • Moderate flux (1×10⁻⁵ to 1×10⁻⁴ m/s): Optimal for most applications with balanced efficiency and membrane longevity.
  • Low flux (< 1×10⁻⁵ m/s): Suggests potential issues with membrane clogging, low pressure, or other operational problems.

🌍 Environmental Applications

  • Water Treatment: Used in desalination plants and filtration systems to optimize purification.
  • Pollution Control: Helps monitor membrane efficiency in wastewater treatment.
  • Industrial Applications: Applied in chemical processing and pharmaceutical industries for controlled separation.
