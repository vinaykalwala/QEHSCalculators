📘 About This Calculator

This calculator determines the Sound Pressure Level (SPL) at a defined distance from a safety valve outlet. It is designed for QEHS, fire safety, and occupational noise assessments.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • Sound Power Level (Lp): Acoustic energy emitted by the source (dB(A)).
  • Distance (R): Distance from the valve to the measurement point (meters).
  • Sound Pressure Level (SPL): Resulting noise perceived at distance R, in dB(A).

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Lp: 0.01 – 200 dB(A), numeric, max 2 decimals.
  • R: ≥ 0.01 m and ≤ 1000 m (realistic), numeric, max 3 decimals.
  • All inputs must be positive real numbers and not empty.
  • Near-field inputs (<0.1 m) and extreme values (>1000 m) trigger warnings.

📊 Output Validation Rules

  • SPL must be within -50 to 200 dB(A) and finite.
  • Assessment (same as result display):
    • SPL ≥ 140 → 🔴 Extremely hazardous noise level (Immediate action required)
    • 85 ≤ SPL < 140 → ⚠️ High noise level (Hearing protection required)
    • 70 ≤ SPL < 85 → 🟡 Moderate noise level (Monitoring recommended)
    • SPL < 70 → ✅ Low noise level (Safe)
  • Warnings (large/small distances, unusual Lp) are shown below the result.

🏭 Standards

Input and output validation rules follow international best practices and are aligned with: ISO 3744 (Acoustics - Sound Power Levels), ISO 9612 (Occupational Noise Exposure), and ISO 50001 (Energy Management Systems).

🔍 Conclusion

This calculator supports compliance audits and workplace noise monitoring by ensuring noise levels from safety valve outlets are quantified, assessed, and compared against international standards.
