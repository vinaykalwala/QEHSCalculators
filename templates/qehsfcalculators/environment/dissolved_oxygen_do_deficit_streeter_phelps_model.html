📘 About

This calculator determines the dissolved oxygen deficit in water bodies using the Streeter-Phelps model. It predicts oxygen depletion due to organic pollution, which is critical for assessing water quality and ecosystem health.

✅ Input Validations

  • Reaeration rate (k₂): Must be greater than 0 and ≤ 5.0 1/day.
  • Deoxygenation rate (k₁): Must be greater than 0 and ≤ 2.0 1/day.
  • Initial BOD (L₀): Must be greater than 0 and ≤ 500 mg/L.
  • Initial deficit (D₀): Must be non-negative and ≤ 20 mg/L.
  • Time (t): Must be non-negative and ≤ 30 days.
  • Rate comparison: k₂ must be greater than k₁ for valid calculation.

🔎 Water Quality Classification

  • Low deficit (< 2 mg/L): Good water quality, supports aquatic life.
  • Moderate deficit (2-4 mg/L): Moderate pollution, some stress on aquatic life.
  • High deficit (4-6 mg/L): Poor water quality, significant stress on aquatic life.
  • Critical deficit (> 6 mg/L): Severe pollution, potential fish kills.

🌍 Applications

  • Wastewater Management: Predicting oxygen depletion from effluent discharges.
  • Environmental Monitoring: Assessing impact of organic pollution on rivers and lakes.
  • Regulatory Compliance: Ensuring water bodies meet quality standards.
  • Ecological Risk Assessment: Evaluating potential harm to aquatic ecosystems.
  • Treatment Plant Design: Sizing aeration systems for wastewater treatment.

🛑 Limitations

  • Assumes complete mixing and constant temperature.
  • Valid for point source pollution in rivers with steady flow.
  • Does not account for photosynthetic oxygen production.
  • Assumes first-order kinetics for BOD degradation.
  • May not accurately predict oxygen sag in complex systems.
