Assessment & Suggestions

📘 About

This calculator estimates annual fuel cost savings from improved condensate return in boiler systems. All values should use consistent units.

📏 Units & typical ranges

  • Improvement in Condensate Return (X): Percentage (%). Typical range: 5-30%.
  • Cost of Fuel per GJ (A): ₹/GJ. Varies by fuel type and market conditions.
  • Energy per kg Make-up Water (B): kJ/kg. Typically 300-600 kJ/kg depending on temperature difference.
  • Boiler Evaporation Rate (C): kg/h. Varies by boiler size and capacity.
  • Operational Hours (D): h/year. Typically 6,000-8,000 h/year for continuous operations.
  • Boiler Efficiency (E): Percentage (%). Typically 75-90% for modern boilers.

✅ Input Validations

  • Improvement in condensate return (X) must be between 0–100%.
  • Cost of fuel per GJ (A) must be a positive number, typically 1–10,000 ₹/GJ.
  • Energy per kg make-up water (B) must be between 0–1000 kJ/kg.
  • Boiler evaporation rate (C) must be between 0–200,000 kg/h.
  • Operational hours per year (D) must be between 0–8,760 hours.
  • Boiler efficiency (E) must be between 0–100%.

🔎 Interpretation

  • High savings (> ₹1,000,000/year): Significant cost reduction potential - excellent return on investment.
  • Moderate savings (₹100,000-1,000,000/year): Substantial cost savings - good investment opportunity.
  • Low savings (< ₹100,000/year): Modest cost reduction - evaluate based on implementation costs.
  • Zero savings: No improvement in condensate return or other constraints.

🛑 Limitations

  • Assumes linear relationship between condensate return improvement and fuel savings.
  • Does not account for implementation costs of condensate return systems.
  • Does not consider variations in fuel prices over time.
  • Assumes constant boiler efficiency across operating conditions.
  • Does not account for maintenance costs of condensate return systems.
