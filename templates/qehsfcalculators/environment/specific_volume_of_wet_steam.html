📘 About This Calculator

This calculator estimates the specific volume of wet steam (m³/kg) using dryness fraction and dry steam volume data. It helps in steam quality checks, separator design, condensate management, and QEHS risk audits.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • vg: Specific volume of dry saturated steam, m³/kg (from steam tables).
  • x: Dryness fraction (dimensionless, 0 = saturated liquid, 1 = saturated vapor).

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • vg: Required, numeric, > 0 m³/kg.
  • Warning if vg < 0.01 m³/kg (very small, high-pressure or unit error).
  • Warning if vg > 100 m³/kg (very large, low-pressure or unit error).
  • Error if vg > 1,000,000 m³/kg (unrealistic, unit/data error).
  • x: Required, numeric, must be between 0 and 1 inclusive.
  • Warning if more than 4 decimal places are used in x.
  • Boundary check: x = 0 → saturated liquid (steam table reference advised), x = 1 → dry steam.

🔎 Output Validations

  • Output unit: m³/kg, rounded to 6 decimal places.
  • Result must be finite and > 0 — otherwise flagged as invalid.
  • Warning if result < 1e-6 m³/kg (extremely small) or > 1000 m³/kg (very large).
  • Assessment categories included in result and audit notes:
    • x ≥ 0.95 → ✅ Mostly dry steam — Low moisture risk, continue routine monitoring.
    • 0.80 ≤ x < 0.95 → ⚠️ Minor moisture present — Check separators occasionally.
    • 0.50 ≤ x < 0.80 → ⚠️ Wet steam (moderate) — Risk of erosion/carryover, inspect separators.
    • x < 0.50 → 🔴 Very wet steam — High risk of water hammer/corrosion, immediate action required.
  • Warnings (unit/precision/operational) are displayed together with the assessment for compliance logs.

📏 Standards

Reference standards include ASME Steam Tables, ISO 3046/1 for turbo-machinery performance, and internal plant SOPs for steam quality monitoring, separator design, and QEHS compliance reporting.

✅ Conclusion

The calculator provides a quick KPI for specific volume of wet steam with audit-ready assessments. Always validate results with official steam tables and plant instrumentation before making operational or safety decisions.
