Assessment & Notes

📘 About

This tool computes the effective contact time available for disinfection and the disinfectant exposure (C × T). Use it to quickly evaluate if a chlorine contact chamber (drinking water or effluent) meets disinfection targets.

✅ Input Validations

  • Theoretical Detention Time (TDT): Must be > 0.01 min. Typical design range: 1–1440 min (1 minute to 24 hours). Values outside this trigger warnings.
  • Baffling Factor (BF): Must be > 0 and ≤ 1. Typical operational range: 0.3–0.9. Values <0.15 or >0.95 are flagged as unrealistic.
  • Residual Chlorine (C): Must be > 0 mg/L. Typical range: 0.2–2.0 mg/L for drinking water. Values <0.1 mg/L (too low) or >10 mg/L (too high) raise alerts.
  • Numeric Validity: Inputs must be finite numbers; non-numeric, empty, or negative values are rejected.
  • Cross-check: C × T must be realistic (0.01–5000 mg·min/L). Extreme outliers are flagged for unit errors or data entry mistakes.

🌍 Key Applications

  • Drinking Water Treatment: Verify contact chamber performance to meet public-health disinfection targets.
  • Wastewater Disinfection: Check effluent disinfection exposure to protect receiving waters.
  • Emergency / Field Water Treatment: Rapid checks for operational decisions in temporary systems.
