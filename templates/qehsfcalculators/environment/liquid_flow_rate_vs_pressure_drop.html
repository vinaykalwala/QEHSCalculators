📘 About This Calculator

Uses the square-root relationship between flow and pressure drop valid for orifice/venturi/acceleration-based elements. Make sure P₁ and P₂ are measured in the same unit (bar here) and represent comparable pressure-loss measurements.

⚠️ Input Validations

The calculator enforces strict input rules to ensure accurate and physically meaningful results:

  • ✔️ Flowrate V₁ (m³/h): Must be numeric and > 0. Typical operational range: 0.001 – 50,000 m³/h.
  • ✔️ Pressure P₁ (bar): Must be numeric and > 0. Prevents division by zero errors and unrealistic readings. Recommended range: 0.01 – 50 bar.
  • ✔️ Pressure P₂ (bar): Must be numeric and ≥ 0. Can be greater or smaller than P₁, but extreme ratios are flagged as warnings.
  • ⚠️ Very small or very large values trigger warnings to confirm measurement accuracy or unit correctness.
  • ⚠️ Extreme pressure ratios (P₂/P₁ < 0.01 or > 100) trigger additional warnings for potential sensor errors.
  • ⚠️ Very large V₂/V₁ changes (>10× increase or <0.1× decrease) trigger plausibility warnings.

🌍 Key Applications

  • Fire Safety Systems: Quick screening of flow changes in sprinkler, hydrant, and standpipe systems.
  • Pipeline Monitoring: Checking flow consistency across pressure drops in industrial fluid transport.
  • Hydraulic Troubleshooting: Identifying abnormal flow behavior or measurement errors in process piping.
  • QEHS Audits: Supports verification of system performance against safety and operational benchmarks.
  • Industrial Design Checks: Preliminary design validation for pumps, orifices, and valves using measured or design pressures.
