📘 About This Calculator

Reynolds number is a dimensionless parameter used to predict pipe flow regime (laminar, transitional, or turbulent). It helps engineers design for pressure drop, mixing efficiency, erosion risk, and QEHS compliance in fluid systems.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • Fluid density (ρ) [kg/m³]
  • Fluid velocity (V) [m/s]
  • Pipe diameter (d) [m]
  • Dynamic viscosity (μ) [Pa·s]
  • Kinematic viscosity (ν = μ / ρ) [m²/s] (computed internally)

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • ρ: Must be numeric > 0; typical liquids 700–14000 kg/m³, gases ~0.5–200 kg/m³
  • V: Must be numeric > 0; typical 0.01–25 m/s, high velocities >50 m/s trigger warnings
  • d: Must be numeric > 0; typical 0.005–5 m
  • μ: Must be numeric > 0; water ~0.001 Pa·s, warning if <1e-6 or >100 Pa·s
  • All inputs must be finite numbers; decimal values allowed
  • Cross-field: ν = μ / ρ must be positive finite; warning if ν < 1e-9 (Re may blow up)

🧾 Output Validations & Feedback

  • If Re ≤ 0 or not finite → error: "Computed Reynolds number invalid"
  • Re < 2000 → Laminar: low mixing, predictable flow. Feedback displayed: "Low mixing; predictable flow. If your process requires mixing or higher heat transfer, consider increasing velocity or reducing viscosity."
  • 2000 ≤ Re ≤ 4000 → Transitional: unstable, avoid for critical systems. Feedback: "Unstable / unpredictable regime — avoid sustained operation in this band for critical systems."
  • Re > 4000 → Turbulent: good mixing, higher losses. Feedback: "Good mixing and heat transfer. Monitor for possible erosion and higher pressure losses at very high Re."
  • Warnings: Re < 100 → creeping flow; Re > 1e5 → high erosion risk; Re > 1e6 → severe erosion/vibration possible (shown along with result)

📏 Standards

Operational references include ASME fluid mechanics guidelines, ISO 5167 for flow measurement, and internal plant SOPs for pipe flow design, mixing, and QEHS risk assessment.
