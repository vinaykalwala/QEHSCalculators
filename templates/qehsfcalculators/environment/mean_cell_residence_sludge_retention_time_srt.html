📘 About This Calculator

This calculator computes Sludge Retention Time (SRT) or Mean Cell Residence Time (MCRT) for biological reactors. Inputs must use consistent units: V in m³, flows in m³/day, concentrations in mg/L.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • V — Reactor volume (m³)
  • X — MLSS in reactor (mg/L)
  • Qw — Waste sludge flow (m³/day)
  • Xr — MLSS in return sludge (mg/L)
  • Xe — MLSS in effluent (mg/L)
  • Q — Influent flow (m³/day)

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • V: 0.01–1,000,000 m³
  • X: 50–20,000 mg/L; Xr: 100–200,000 mg/L; Xe: 0–2,000 mg/L
  • Q: 0.0001–10,000,000 m³/day; Qw: 0 ≤ Qw < Q
  • Decimals: concentrations ≤3 dp; volumes/flows ≤3 dp (warnings if exceeded)
  • Denominator (mass leaving) must be > 0 → error if ≤0
  • All missing or non-numeric inputs → error
  • Out-of-range inputs → error

📊 Output Validations & Feedback

  • SRT (days) computed using reactor and flow inputs
  • Non-finite or invalid SRT → error
  • Classification and operational guidance displayed alongside results:
    • SRT < 2 → Too Short: "Biomass washout risk — increase retention or reduce wastage."
    • 2 ≤ SRT < 5 → Marginal: "Biomass just stable — monitor process closely."
    • 5 ≤ SRT ≤ 15 → Typical: "Stable operation; SRT in recommended range."
    • 15 < SRT ≤ 30 → Long: "Watch for sludge bulking and endogenous decay."
    • SRT > 30 → Very Long: "Investigate sludge handling strategy."
  • Warnings for extreme SRT values (e.g., SRT > 50 days or SRT > 365 days or SRT < 0.1 days) are shown
  • Feedback includes classification, guidance, and potential operational issues

📐 Governing Principle

A Mean Cell Residence Time (MCRT) or Sludge Retention Time (SRT) calculator is not tied to a single universal standard, but rather uses principles from widely accepted wastewater engineering and microbial kinetics. The underlying formulas and concepts are derived from biological process design, such as the Michaelis-Menten equation, which governs microbial growth and substrate utilization rates, rather than a specific regulatory document.

  • Microbial Kinetics: Based on microbial growth and decay principles (e.g., Michaelis-Menten model).
  • Biological Wastewater Treatment Design: SRT is critical for activated sludge process design and operation.
  • WEF / EPA Standards: Widely accepted guidelines from organizations like Water Environment Federation and EPA.
  • Engineering Handbooks: Standard environmental and wastewater engineering references provide established formulas and methodologies.
  • Application: SRT calculator applies scientific principles and engineering practices, not a specific standard document.

🔎 Conclusion

This SRT calculator integrates input validation, computation, classification, and operational guidance. Feedback and assessment are displayed alongside results, enabling informed decisions for process control and sludge management.
