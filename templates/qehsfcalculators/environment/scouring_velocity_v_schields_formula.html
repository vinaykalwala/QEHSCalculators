📘 About This Calculator

The Scouring Velocity (V) Calculator determines the critical flow velocity required to prevent grit deposition in wastewater treatment systems using Schield's Formula. This calculation is essential for designing grit chambers that effectively remove inorganic particles while allowing organic matter to continue through the treatment process.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • Specific Gravity (Ss): Density ratio of particle to water (dimensionless).
  • Particle Diameter (d): Size of grit particles in meters (m).
  • Constant (k): Empirical coefficient ranging from 3 to 4.5 (dimensionless).

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Specific Gravity (Ss): Must be > 1 (typical range: 2.0-2.7 for grit particles).
  • Particle Diameter (d): Must be > 0.0001 m and < 0.1 m (typical range: 0.0005-0.01 m).
  • Constant (k): Must be between 3.0 and 4.5 (default 3.5).
  • Realistic Values: Ss < 1.5 may indicate organic matter, not grit.
  • Extreme Values: Very large particles (>0.05 m) may not be applicable to standard grit chambers.

🔎 Output Validations

  • Velocity Range: Typical scouring velocities range from 0.15 m/s to 0.6 m/s.
  • Too Low Velocity (<0.15 m/s): May not prevent grit deposition.
  • Optimal Velocity (0.15-0.6 m/s): Suitable for most grit chamber designs.Will prevent grit deposition while not resuspending organic matter.
  • Too High Velocity (>0.6 m/s): May resuspend organic matter and reduce treatment efficiency.
  • Negative/Nan/Infinity: Indicates invalid input combination.
