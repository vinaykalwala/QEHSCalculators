📘 About This Calculator

This calculator evaluates the Rankine Cycle Efficiency (ηₖ), a thermodynamic indicator of how effectively heat energy is converted into mechanical work in steam power plants. It helps engineers, energy managers, and QEHS professionals monitor and optimize turbine performance.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • Hi: Turbine Inlet Enthalpy (kJ/kg)
  • He: Condensate Enthalpy (kJ/kg)
  • Hc: Turbine Exhaust Enthalpy (kJ/kg)

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Hi, He, Hc must be numeric and ≥ 1 kJ/kg
  • Hi must be greater than Hc and He
  • Hc must be greater than He
  • (Hi − He) must be positive (denominator in efficiency formula)
  • Values above 10,000 kJ/kg trigger warnings

⚠️ Output Validations

  • Efficiency (ηₖ) must satisfy 0 < η ≤ 100
  • If η ≥ 40% → ✅ High efficiency: "System performance is excellent. Maintain current operating conditions and schedule routine monitoring."
  • If 20% ≤ η < 40% → ⚠️ Moderate efficiency: "Efficiency is acceptable but can be improved. Review turbine maintenance records and consider energy optimization."
  • If 5% ≤ η < 20% → 🔴 Low efficiency: "System is underperforming. Check for turbine wear, condenser fouling, or steam leakage. Immediate corrective action recommended."
  • Else → ❌ Invalid efficiency: "Inputs appear inconsistent. Please recheck enthalpy values."

📏 Standards

Recommended references include ISO 50001 for energy management, ASME boiler and turbine guidelines, and internal plant SOPs for turbine operation and thermal efficiency verification.
