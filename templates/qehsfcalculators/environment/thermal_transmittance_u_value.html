üìê Governing Principle

A thermal transmittance (U-value) calculator is based on standards such as ISO 6946 for building components like walls and roofs, ISO 13370 for ground floors, ISO 10077 for windows, and also uses conventions like BR 443 from the BRE Group in the UK. These standards define the methods for calculating the thermal performance of building elements, considering factors like material thermal conductivity and resistance.

Key Standards and Documents

  • ISO 6946: Provides the calculation method for the thermal resistance and transmittance of building elements, particularly walls and roofs.
  • BR 443 (Conventions for U-value Calculations): Published by the BRE Group, this UK document specifies how U-value calculations should be carried out in line with building regulations.
  • ISO 10077: Specifically for calculating the thermal transmittance of windows.
  • ISO 13370: Used for calculating the U-value of ground floors.
  • ISO 10211: Applies to thermal bridge calculations where metal components penetrate insulation.

How U-Value Calculators Work

  • Material Properties: Requires the thermal conductivity (k-value) and thickness of each layer in the building component (e.g., insulation, brick, plaster).
  • Thermal Resistance: Calculate the thermal resistance (R-value) of each layer.
  • Summation: Add the resistances of all layers along with internal and external surface resistances to get the total thermal resistance.
  • Inverse Calculation: The U-value is the inverse of the total thermal resistance (U = 1/Rtotal), representing the rate of heat flow through the component.

Summary

The choice of standard depends on the specific building component being analyzed (wall, roof, window, or floor) and the application of the calculator.

üìå Conclusion

This calculator provides a quick and reliable way to compute U-Value from R-Value. A low U-Value indicates strong insulation and energy efficiency, while a high U-Value signals significant heat loss. Results should be compared with ISO, ASHRAE, or local building code standards to ensure compliance in construction and insulation design.
