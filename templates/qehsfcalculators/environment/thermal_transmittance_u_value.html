📘 About

The Thermal Transmittance (U-Value) Calculator determines the rate of heat transfer through a material based on its thermal resistance (R).

U-Value (W/m²·°C) shows insulation quality:

  • Low U → better insulation, less heat loss
  • High U → poor insulation, higher heat loss

✅ Input Validations

  • R is required (cannot be empty).
  • Must be numeric, positive, finite.
  • Must not equal zero (division by zero error).
  • Recommended range: 0.1–10 m²·°C/W.

🛑 Limitations

  • Assumes steady-state thermal transfer.
  • Does not account for moisture, convection, or complex assemblies.
  • Field performance may vary due to construction quality.
