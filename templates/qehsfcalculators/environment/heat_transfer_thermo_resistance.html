📘 About

The calculator estimates steady conductive heat flow across a barrier using thermal-resistance parameters. Provide the surface area (m²), the temperature difference across the barrier (K or °C), and the barrier's thermal resistance (m²·K/W). Results are total power (W), kW equivalent and heat flux (W/m²).

✅ Input Validations

  • A (m²): required; numeric; typical range 0.01 – 10,000 m². Reject if ≤0 or >1,000,000 m² (likely unit error).
  • ΔT (K or °C): required; numeric; typical range 0.1 – 200 K. Reject if ≤0 or >1,000 K.
  • R (m²·K/W): required; numeric; typical range 0.001 – 100 m²·K/W. Reject if ≤0 or >1×10⁶ (unit/mis-entry likely).
  • Precision: up to 6 decimals accepted; outputs rounded: W to 2 decimals, kW to 3 decimals, flux to 2 decimals.
  • Cross-checks: R too small may mean you entered U (W/m²·K) instead of R; very large ΔT or tiny R produces extreme power—check units.

🔎 QEHS & Fire-Safety considerations

  • Large heat release near combustibles increases ignition risk — review separation, ventilation and fire-rated barriers.
  • High heat flux on surfaces can damage insulation, sensors and protective equipment — check manufacturer limits.
  • For audits, keep input values, units, Q, flux and timestamp in records; note assumptions (steady-state, uniform R).

🏭 Typical applications

  • Assessing wall/window heat loss for building energy audits
  • Sizing cooling for electrical cabinets/electronics
  • Estimating heat removal in process enclosures and thermal insulation checks
