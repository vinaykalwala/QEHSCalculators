üìú Governing Principle

A thermal resistance calculator is not tied to a single standard, but the international standard ISO 6946 is commonly used to calculate the thermal resistance of building components and elements, using either the Parallel Path Method or the Isothermal Planes Method. Other standards, such as those based on the Heat Flow Meter method by ISO 9869 or ASTM E1046 and ASTM E1155, are used for experimental measurements of thermal resistance.

Calculation Methods for Thermal Resistance

  • ISO 6946: This standard provides analytical methods for calculating thermal resistance for building components.
    • Parallel Path Method: Assumes heat flow is perpendicular to the wall.
    • Isothermal Planes Method: Used for certain building configurations.
    • Combined Method: ISO 6946 utilizes a combination of these methods for comprehensive calculations.
  • ISO 9869: This standard is used for the in-situ measurement of thermal resistance of walls using the heat flow meter method.
  • ASTM Standards: Standards like ASTM E1046 and ASTM E1155 also support the heat flow meter method for experimental measurements.

üìù Conclusion

This calculator provides quick estimates of thermal resistance and heat transfer, supporting energy audits, design evaluations, and safety checks. For critical applications, cross-check results with relevant ISO or ASTM standards and consult detailed engineering references.
