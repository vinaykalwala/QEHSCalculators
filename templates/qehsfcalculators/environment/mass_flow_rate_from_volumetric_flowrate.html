📘 About This Calculator

This calculator estimates the mass flow rate (ṁ) based on the given volumetric flow rate (qv) and specific volume (vg). Ensure consistent SI units: qv in m³/s and vg in m³/kg. The result is given in kg/s and additionally converted to kg/h.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • Volumetric Flow (qv) → volume of fluid per unit time (m³/s).
  • Specific Volume (vg) → volume per unit mass of fluid (m³/kg).
  • Mass Flow (ṁ) → computed using ṁ = qv / vg (kg/s).
  • Hourly Mass Flow → ṁ × 3600 (kg/h).

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Volumetric Flow (qv): numeric, may be negative (reverse flow allowed). Range checked: |qv| ≤ 1×10⁶ m³/s.
  • Specific Volume (vg): numeric, must be > 0. Allowed range: 1e-6 – 1×10⁴ m³/kg.
  • Zero or negative values → error.
  • Unrealistic extreme values outside these ranges → error or warning.
  • Excessive decimals (>4) trigger a precision warning.

📊 Output Validations

  • qm < 0 → Reverse flow detected — check orientation or sensors.
  • 0 ≤ qm < 0.001 → Very low mass flow — check for leaks or unit mismatch.
  • 0.001 ≤ qm < 10 → Normal / small-system flow (HVAC, small piping).
  • 10 ≤ qm < 1000 → Large industrial flow — confirm pipe sizing & safety systems.
  • qm ≥ 1000 → Extremely large flow — urgent design review & unit check required.

📐 Governing Principle

Mass flow rate, when expressed in standardized units like sccm (standard cubic centimeters per minute) or slpm (standard liters per minute), is based on standardized volumetric flow rate standards, which reference a specific mass of gas to a defined volume under Standard Temperature and Pressure (STP).

These standards are defined to allow comparison and conversion of volumetric flow to mass flow by correcting for density changes due to variations in temperature and pressure.

Key Concepts:

  • Volumetric Flow Rate: fluid volume per unit time (e.g., L/min).
  • Mass Flow Rate: fluid mass per unit time (e.g., g/min).
  • Standard Temperature & Pressure (STP): defined conditions for gas measurements.
    • American Standard: 0 °C (273.15 K), 1 atm (1013.25 hPa).
    • European Standard: 0 °C, 1013 mbar. Variants with 20 °C or 25 °C exist.

How it works:

  • True volumetric flow varies with temperature & pressure.
  • Correcting to STP gives standardized volumetric flow.
  • Standardized volumetric flow is directly convertible to mass flow.

Examples of Standard Units:

  • sccm: Standard cubic centimeters per minute
  • slpm: Standard liters per minute
  • SCFM: Standard cubic feet per minute

Why it's used: ensures consistency and accuracy in reporting gas flow, regardless of real-time environmental conditions.
