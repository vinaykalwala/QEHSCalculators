📘 About

This calculator determines the vertical rise (ΔH) of stack emissions using Holland’s Equation. It is essential for air quality modeling, industrial emissions assessment, and fire safety planning.

✅ Input Validations

  • Stack Gas Exit Velocity (Vₛ): 0.1 – 50 m/s
  • Stack Diameter (Dₛ): 0.1 – 10 m
  • Wind Speed (U): 0.5 – 25 m/s
  • Atmospheric Pressure (Pₐ): 950 – 1050 mb
  • Stack Gas Temperature (Tₛ): 300 – 1500 K
  • Ambient Air Temperature (Tₐ): 273 – 320 K
  • Temperature difference: Tₛ must be greater than Tₐ

🔎 Plume Rise Classification

  • ΔH < 10 m → Low Plume Rise – plume may stay near ground, monitor local air quality.
  • ΔH 10–50 m → Moderate Plume Rise – typical operating conditions.
  • ΔH > 50 m → High Plume Rise – check safety distances and AQ compliance.

🏭 Applications

  • Air pollution modeling (Gaussian plume)
  • Industrial stack design and height planning
  • Regulatory compliance for emissions
  • Meteorological studies and risk assessments
