📘 About This Calculator

Estimate the steam temperature (Ts) in a heat exchanger based on secondary fluid temperatures and the temperature design constant. Useful for thermal system efficiency, boiler and heat exchanger monitoring, and fire safety management.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • TDC (dimensionless): Temperature Design Constant, >1.
  • T1 (°C): Secondary fluid inlet temperature.
  • T2 (°C): Secondary fluid outlet temperature.

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • TDC: Required, numeric, 1 < TDC ≤ 10 (prevents division by zero or negative denominator).
  • T1, T2: Required, numeric, 0 < T1, T2 ≤ 500°C (realistic thermal system limits).
  • Advisories: Warnings for decimal inputs (rounded to 2 decimal places).
  • Constraints: All inputs must be numeric and non-empty.

📊 Output Validation

  • Ts: Displayed in °C, rounded to 2 decimal places, must be finite.
  • Assessment:
    • Ts < 50°C: Too low — may cause condensation and corrosion.
    • 50°C ≤ Ts ≤ 300°C: Safe operating temperature.
    • Ts > 300°C: High temperature — risk of overheating, fire, or system damage.
  • Warnings triggered for: non-finite results or invalid input combinations.

What is IAPWS-IF97?

IAPWS-IF97 is the "Industrial Formulation 1997 for the Thermodynamic Properties of Water and Steam," as defined by the International Association for the Properties of Water and Steam.

It provides a comprehensive and accurate method for calculating steam properties such as temperature, pressure, enthalpy, and density.

Why use this standard?

Accuracy: IAPWS-IF97 is the universally accepted standard for calculating water and steam properties, ensuring accurate results for various applications.

Comprehensive Data: It covers a wide range of conditions, allowing for precise calculations of steam properties under different pressures and temperatures.

Standardization: Using a standard ensures consistency and comparability of calculations across different systems and industries.
