📘 About This Calculator

Estimates how long water stays in a settling basin based on basin dimensions and flow rate. Proper retention time improves sedimentation efficiency, water quality, and ensures compliance with environmental standards and safety requirements.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • L: Basin length (meters or feet)
  • W: Basin width (meters or feet)
  • Z₀: Basin depth (meters or feet)
  • Q: Flow rate (m³/s, m³/day, or gpm)
  • t: Retention time (seconds, minutes, hours, or days)

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • ✔ Basin length (L): required, numeric, 0 < L ≤ 500 m (or 1,640 ft)
  • ✔ Basin width (W): required, numeric, 0 < W ≤ 200 m (or 656 ft)
  • ✔ Basin depth (Z₀): required, numeric, 0 < Z₀ ≤ 50 m (or 164 ft)
  • ✔ Flow rate (Q): required, numeric, 0 < Q ≤ 1,000 m³/s
  • ✔ After unit conversion: Qₘ³/ₛ > 0; otherwise error “Invalid flow rate after unit conversion”
  • ✔ Logical checks: prevent division by zero and unrealistic basin/flow combinations

📊 Output Validations

  • ❌ If computed retention time (tₛ) ≤ 0 or not finite → error: “Computed retention time is invalid”
  • ⚠ Extreme values: t < 1 min or t > 7 days triggers warnings
  • Assessment (same as displayed in results):
    • t < 1 hr → ❌ Too Low: Risk of poor settling. Target ≥ 2 hours.
    • 1 ≤ t < 2 hr → ⚠️ Low-Moderate: Settling may be incomplete.
    • 2 ≤ t ≤ 4 hr → ✅ Typical: Within standard design range for treatment plants.
    • t > 4 hr → ✅ High Retention: Good settling, but verify design feasibility.
    • t > 168 hr → ⚠️ Very high (>7 days) – check for unrealistic input combination.

📏 Standards

Industry references: ISO 24525:2022 – Operation and maintenance of on-site domestic wastewater services, EPA Settling Basin Recommendations, Internal SOPs / design guidelines for retention time and sedimentation performance.
