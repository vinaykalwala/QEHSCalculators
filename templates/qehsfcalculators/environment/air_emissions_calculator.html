📘 About This Calculator

The Air Emissions (kg CO₂) Calculator estimates greenhouse gas emissions produced from fuel combustion. It supports environmental performance tracking under ISO 14064 and GHG Protocol reporting frameworks.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • Fuel Consumed (L): Volume of fuel used during the activity.
  • Emission Factor (kg CO₂ / L): Standard value indicating kilograms of CO₂ emitted per litre of fuel.

✔️ Input Validation Rules

  • Fuel Consumed > 0 and ≤ 1,000,000 L (practical range).
  • Emission Factor > 0 and ≤ 5 kg CO₂/L (typical range for fuels).
  • Decimals allowed up to 2 places.
  • Logical check: Both values must be positive.

📊 Output Validations / Feedback

  • Emissions < 1000 kg CO₂ → 🌿 Low emissions: Excellent fuel efficiency.
  • 1000 ≤ Emissions < 10000 kg CO₂ → ⚠️ Moderate emissions: Consider optimizing usage.
  • Emissions ≥ 10000 kg CO₂ → 🔥 High emissions: Review fuel consumption patterns urgently.
  • Warnings appear for unusual fuel consumption or emission factor inputs.
  • Calculated result is displayed in kg CO₂.
  • Assessment/feedback is always visible and does not depend on JS calculation.

📐 Governing Principle

The Air Emissions (kg CO₂) Calculator is grounded in internationally recognized methodologies, primarily following the IPCC (Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change) guidelines and the ISO 14064 standard for greenhouse gas quantification and reporting. These frameworks ensure consistency, accuracy, and transparency in emission estimation.

The governing principle is to multiply the quantity of fuel consumed by a scientifically determined emission factor specific to that fuel type. This factor represents the average CO₂ released per litre of fuel burned, accounting for the carbon content and combustion efficiency. Mathematically:

CO₂ Emissions (kg) = Fuel Consumed (L) × Emission Factor (kg CO₂ / L)

This principle aligns with environmental management systems (such as ISO 14001) and national greenhouse gas inventory procedures. It enables organizations to track and minimize their carbon footprint, ensuring compliance with sustainability goals and regulatory frameworks.

🏁 Conclusion

Tracking air emissions enables organizations to benchmark fuel efficiency, comply with sustainability goals, and contribute to reduced greenhouse gas footprints. This calculator provides a quick estimation of CO₂ emissions from fuel consumption, helping users make informed decisions about energy usage and environmental impact.
