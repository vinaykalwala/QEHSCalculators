📘 About This Calculator

The Wind Profile Calculator estimates wind speed at different elevations using the Power Law method. This is essential for environmental impact assessments, structural design, and pollution dispersion modeling in QEHS applications.

⚙️ Parameters Used

  • U₁₀ (Wind Speed at 10m): Reference wind speed measured at 10 meters (m/s)
  • Z (Elevation): Height above ground where wind speed is estimated (meters)
  • p (Terrain Coefficient): Exponent based on terrain roughness: Urban = 0.40, Suburban = 0.28, Rural = 0.16
  • UZ: Calculated wind speed at elevation Z (m/s)

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Wind Speed at 10m must be numeric and ≥ 0.1 m/s (instrument resolution)
  • Elevation must be numeric and ≥ 1 meter
  • Maximum Wind Speed: 50 m/s → triggers warning (hurricane-force winds)
  • Elevation > 300 meters → triggers warning (beyond typical measurement range)
  • Extreme wind speed > 30 m/s → warning (potentially dangerous conditions)
  • Extremely low wind speed < 0.5 m/s → warning (may be instrument sensitivity issue)
  • Zero, negative, or non-numeric inputs are invalid

🔎 Output Validations

  • The calculated wind speed is displayed along with an assessment message.
  • Assessment / Feedback Messages:
    • ✅ Wind Speed < 5 m/s → Low wind conditions (Generally safe for most operations)
    • ⚠️ 5 ≤ Wind Speed ≤ 15 m/s → Moderate wind conditions (Monitor operations, potential restrictions)
    • 🔴 Wind Speed > 15 m/s → High wind conditions (Operations likely restricted, safety concerns)
  • Feedback messages appear alongside the result to guide interpretation.

⚖️ Governing Principle

A "Wind Profile Calculator" isn't tied to a single standard; it calculates a wind profile using methods like the Power Law or may adhere to specific standards such as ASCE 7, IS 875-3, or Eurocode EN 1991-1-4 depending on the application. The standard or methodology depends on the intended purpose: structural engineering, environmental impact assessment, or renewable energy projects.

  • Common standards and methodologies:
    • ASCE 7 (American Society of Civil Engineers): Structural design and wind load calculations
    • IS 875-3 (India): Wind loads on buildings and structures
    • Eurocode EN 1991-1-4: European standard for wind actions
    • Power Law: Approximation for estimating wind speed variation with height
    • Hellman Exponential Law: Alternative method for calculating wind profiles
  • Factors influencing the standard or method used:
    • Geographic location: national building codes and regulations
    • Application: structural engineering, environmental assessment, or renewable energy projects

🔚 Conclusion

The Wind Profile Calculator estimates wind speed at any elevation using terrain-based methods. With proper inputs, validations, and assessment feedback, it helps engineers, environmental professionals, and operators make safe and informed decisions regarding structural design, environmental studies, and operational safety.
