📘 About

The calculator derives the required steam mass by dividing the total sensible energy required to heat the water (m·cₚ·ΔT in kJ) by the steam latent energy available per kilogram (hfg in kJ/kg), then dividing by the recovery time (h) to obtain kg/h.

✅ Input Validations

  • Mass (m): positive (kg). Typical: 10 – 1,000,000 kg. Hard limits enforced: 0.1 – 1,000,000 kg.
  • Specific heat (cₚ): positive (kJ/(kg·°C)). Typical water ~4.18. Allowed range: 0.5 – 10 kJ/(kg·°C).
  • Temperature rise (ΔT): positive (°C). Typical: 1 – 200 °C. Allowed range: 0.01 – 1000 °C.
  • Enthalpy of evaporation (hfg): positive (kJ/kg). Typical steam: 1500 – 2800 kJ/kg. Allowed range: 100 – 4000 kJ/kg.
  • Recovery time (t): positive (hours). Typical: 0.1 – 168 h. Allowed range: 0.001 – 8760 h.
  • All inputs must be numeric. Units must be consistent (kJ, kg, °C, hours).
  • High precision inputs (>4 decimal places) will trigger a warning to reduce to reasonable reporting precision.

🏭 Applications

  • Used for sizing steam supply in storage calorifiers and hot water systems.
  • Helps ensure boilers are adequately rated for process and utility heating loads.
  • Supports HVAC and district heating projects where accurate steam demand calculation is essential.
  • Applied in food processing, textile, and pharmaceutical industries for precise thermal energy planning.
  • Useful for energy audits to estimate steam consumption and improve efficiency.
