📘 About This Calculator

This calculator assesses **mixing intensity** in water/wastewater treatment by computing the velocity gradient (G) and Gt value. It helps engineers optimize coagulant/flocculation performance and ensure safe, compliant operations.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • P → Power input to the mixer (N·m/s)
  • μ → Fluid dynamic viscosity (Pa·s)
  • V → Tank volume (m³)
  • t → Mixing time (s)

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • All inputs must be numeric.
  • Power (P): 0.01 ≤ P ≤ 1,000,000 N·m/s
  • Viscosity (μ): 0.0001 ≤ μ ≤ 1 Pa·s
  • Tank Volume (V): 0.01 ≤ V ≤ 10,000 m³
  • Mixing Time (t): 1 ≤ t ≤ 3600 s
  • Warnings are displayed for extreme but valid values:
    • G > 1000 s⁻¹ → risk of floc shear
    • G < 10000 → under-mixing
    • G > 1000000 → excessive mixing

🔎 Output Validations

  • Displays computed G (velocity gradient, s⁻¹) and Gt (mixing intensity).
  • assessment/feedback:
    • G 10,000 – 100,000 → ✅ Optimal mixing intensity
    • G < 10,000 → ⚠️ Under-mixed; increase energy or mixing time
    • G > 100,000 → ⚠️ Over-mixed; reduce energy or mixing time
  • Warnings for extreme G or Gt values are displayed alongside results.

📏 Standards

Recommended references include ISO 7027 for turbidity assessment, EN 12255-4 for flocculation/mixing basin design, and internal plant SOPs for mixer power, G & Gt verification, and process optimization.
