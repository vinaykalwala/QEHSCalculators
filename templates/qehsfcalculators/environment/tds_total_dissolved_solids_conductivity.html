✅ Input Validations

  • Conductivity is required — cannot be left blank
  • Conductivity must be a positive finite number (µS/cm)
  • Minimum conductivity: 1 µS/cm (ultra-pure water threshold)
  • Maximum conductivity: 100,000 µS/cm (saturated brine solution)
  • Values outside typical ranges trigger warnings but not errors

📊 Water Quality Classification

  • TDS < 300 ppm → ✅ Excellent (Ideal for drinking)
  • 300-600 ppm → ⚠️ Good (Acceptable for drinking)
  • 600-900 ppm → ⚠️ Fair (May affect taste)
  • 900-1200 ppm → ❌ Poor (Not recommended for drinking)
  • TDS > 1200 ppm → ❌ Unacceptable (Unsuitable for drinking)

🌍 Key Applications

  • Drinking Water Quality: Assessing safety and taste of potable water
  • Environmental Monitoring: Evaluating health of rivers, lakes, and groundwater
  • Industrial Processes: Monitoring water quality in boilers, cooling towers, and manufacturing
  • Aquaculture: Maintaining optimal conditions for aquatic life
  • Agriculture: Assessing irrigation water quality

🛑 Limitations

  • The conversion factor of 0.7 is an approximation that varies with water composition
  • Different water types may require specific conversion factors (0.5-0.9)
  • Does not distinguish between different types of dissolved solids
  • For precise measurements, laboratory analysis is recommended
