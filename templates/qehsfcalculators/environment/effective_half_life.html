🛡️ QEHS Perspective

  • Plan decay-in-storage durations and waste release timing to meet clearance levels.
  • Estimate patient release or work-area re-entry intervals after administration/spills.
  • Right-size PPE, shielding, and monitoring based on how fast activity effectively declines.
  • Track deviations over time (e.g., hydration or organ function changes affecting τb).

📊 Interpretation (Guidance)

  • τe < min(τr, τb) → expected; combined processes accelerate clearance.
  • When τb ≪ τr: biological elimination dominates; activity falls quickly in vivo.
  • When τr ≪ τb: physical decay dominates; biological processes add little.

(Model assumes independent first-order processes.)

⚠️ Input Validations

  • ✔️ τr, τb (days): required, > 0, allowed 1e-9 to 1e9 days (typical clinical: minutes–days; environmental: days–years).
  • ✔️ Decimals allowed; step ≤ 0.0001 days (~8.64 s) recommended.
  • ✔️ Cross-field: τe < min(τr, τb) by definition (the calculator enforces this).
  • ❌ Zeros, negatives, non-numeric values rejected (prevents divide-by-zero and non-physical results).

🌍 Applications

  • Nuclear medicine: dosing, imaging schedules, patient release calculations.
  • Radiation safety: spill cleanup duration, area re-entry, decay-in-storage for waste.
  • Environmental fate: combined decay and environmental removal processes.
  • Research & pharmacokinetics: modeling tracer clearance and compartment studies.
