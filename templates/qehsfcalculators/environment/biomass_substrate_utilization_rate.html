Assessment & Suggestions

📘 About

This calculator estimates the substrate utilization rate using the Monod equation:

📏 Units & typical ranges

  • Maximum Specific Utilization Rate (k): day⁻¹. Typical range: 1–10 day⁻¹ for many substrates.
  • Microorganism Concentration (X): mg/L. Varies widely by system (10–5000 mg/L).
  • Substrate Concentration (S): mg/L. Concentration of the limiting nutrient.
  • Half-Saturation Coefficient (Kₛ): mg/L. Typical range: 0.1–100 mg/L depending on substrate.

✅ Input Validation

  • Maximum specific utilization rate (k): Must be a positive number. Warning if > 20 day⁻¹ (check units).
  • Microorganism concentration (X): Must be a positive number. Warning if > 10,000 mg/L (unrealistic).
  • Substrate concentration (S): Must be a non-negative number. Warning if > 10,000 mg/L (verify units).
  • Half-saturation coefficient (Kₛ): Must be a positive number. Warning if > 500 mg/L (outside typical range).
  • Division by zero check: Cannot calculate if both S = 0 and Kₛ = 0.
  • General: All fields are required and must be valid numeric values (scientific notation allowed).

🔎 Interpretation

  • High utilization rate: Efficient substrate consumption, optimal microbial activity.
  • Moderate utilization rate: Typical operation and substrate may be limiting.
  • Low utilization rate: Possible nutrient limitation, toxicity, or suboptimal conditions.
  • Zero utilization rate: No substrate available or microbial activity inhibited.

🛑 Limitations

  • Assumes single limiting substrate and ideal conditions.
  • Does not account for inhibition effects at high substrate concentrations.
  • Assumes constant parameters throughout the process.
  • Does not consider spatial heterogeneity in real systems.
  • May not accurately represent complex microbial communities.
