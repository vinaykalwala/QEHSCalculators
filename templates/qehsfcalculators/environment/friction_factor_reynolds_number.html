📘 About

Use this to choose the right friction-factor model, assess erosion/noise risk, and support QEHS/fire protection reviews for pumps, hydrants, and sprinkler/standpipe systems.

✅ Input Validations (SI)

  • ρ (kg/m³): required, finite, 0.1–20,000 (water ≈ 1,000; air ≈ 1.2).
  • U (m/s): required, finite, 0.0001–100 (typical water lines 0.5–5).
  • D (m): required, finite, 0.0001–5 (flag < 0.01 m as micro/coil scale).
  • μ (kg/(m·s)): required, finite, 1e-6–1,000 (water ≈ 0.001; air ≈ 1.8e-5).
  • Cross-check: inferred kinematic viscosity ν = μ/ρ should typically be 1e-7–1e-2 m²/s (warn if outside).
  • Output: Re must be finite and in (0, 1×10¹⁰]; warn if > 1×10⁷ (extreme turbulence).

🔎 Regime Classification

  • Laminar: Re < 2,000 — use Darcy f = 64/Re for head-loss estimates.
  • Transitional: 2,000 ≤ Re ≤ 4,000 — high uncertainty; avoid design decisions.
  • Turbulent: Re > 4,000 — use roughness-aware correlations (Colebrook/Haaland/Moody).

🛡️ QEHS & Fire Safety Perspective

  • Verify velocities within safe limits to mitigate water hammer, erosion, and noise.
  • For firewater, confirm turbulent regime and size pumps/diameters accordingly.
  • Document fluid, temperature, and pipe condition; aging/roughness affects downstream calcs.

🛑 Limitations

  • Assumes Newtonian fluid and circular pipe; does not compute friction factor or head loss.
  • Minor losses, fittings, and elevation changes are not included.
  • Use consistent SI units and verified property data (temperature dependent).
