📘 About This Calculator

Computes the freezing point depression and the resulting new freezing point of a solution. This helps optimize winter safety operations such as de-icing and antifreeze dosing, ensuring fire-water networks and hydrants remain operable in sub-zero conditions while avoiding excess chemical use that could harm the environment.

📊 Interpretation

  • ΔTf < 1 °C → ✅ Mild depression (light protection)
  • 1–5 °C → ⚠️ Moderate depression (typical winter operations)
  • 5–10 °C → ❗ Strong depression (heavy de-icing)
  • > 10 °C → 🔴 Extreme depression (verify feasibility and environmental impact)

⚠️Input Validations

  • ✔️ m: required, 0 ≤ m ≤ 25 mol/kg (warning if > 10)
  • ✔️ Kf: required, 0.01 ≤ Kf ≤ 30 K·kg/mol (warning if < 0.1 or > 10)
  • ✔️ Tpure: required, −200 ≤ Tpure ≤ +150 °C
  • ✔️ ΔTf guardrails: 0 ≤ ΔTf ≤ 120 °C (warning if > 10)
  • ✔️ Tnew ≥ −200 °C (warning if < −100 °C)
  • ❌ Negative or non-numeric values are invalid
  • ℹ️ Inputs allow up to 6 decimals; outputs rounded to ΔTf 3 decimals, Tnew 2 decimals

🌍 Key Applications

  • Road & facility de-icing (salt/alternative deicers)
  • Fire-water systems (hydrants, tanks, sprinkler mains)
  • Process safety (coolants/heat-transfer fluids)
  • Environmental compliance (avoid over-dosing to limit chloride loading)
