📘 About

Mass flow rate is fundamental for ventilation design, duct/pipeline sizing, fire and smoke transport analysis, and leak-rate assessment. Use density (ρ) for direct mass-based inputs (kg/m³) or specific volume (v = m³/kg) if your data source provides that instead. The calculator accepts either convention and validates units.

⚠️ Input Validations

  • Area (A): must be positive. Typical examples: 0.005 m² (10 cm diameter pipe) — 1.0 m² (large duct). Hard limits enforced: 0.00001 – 100 m².
  • Velocity (u): must be positive. Typical 0.1 – 30 m/s for HVAC and process flows. Hard limits enforced: 0.0001 – 200 m/s.
  • Density (ρ) (if selected): must be positive (kg/m³). Typical ranges: air ~1.2, water ~1000. Hard limits enforced: 0.1 – 20000 kg/m³.
  • Specific Volume (v) (if selected): must be positive (m³/kg). Typical liquid values ~0.001; gases larger. Hard limits enforced: 0.00001 – 100 m³/kg.
  • Decimal precision: avoid >4 decimals for reporting; warnings shown if inputs have excessive decimals.
  • Cross-checks: if 'specific volume' is selected and value is extremely small (e.g., < 0.0001), the calculator will warn about likely unit mismatch (you may have meant density instead).

🛠️ Applications

  • HVAC design: Proper sizing of ducts, fans, and air handling units.
  • Process engineering: Flow measurement and control in chemical, pharmaceutical, and food processing lines.
  • Fire and smoke modeling: Simulating spread rates in buildings and industrial facilities.
  • Leak detection & safety: Quantifying leak magnitude and emergency response planning.
  • Environmental monitoring: Air quality and pollutant transport assessment in enclosed spaces.
