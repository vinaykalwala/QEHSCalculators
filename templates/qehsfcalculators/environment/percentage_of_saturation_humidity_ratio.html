📘 About This Calculator

This calculator estimates the Percentage Saturation (%) of air based on the partial pressure of water vapor, the saturation pressure at the same temperature, and the total system pressure. It helps determine how close the air is to being fully saturated with water vapor, which is critical for HVAC design, industrial drying processes, and environmental monitoring.

⚠️ Input Validations

  • ✔️ pₐ (Partial Pressure): Must be > 0 Pa and less than total system pressure P.
  • ✔️ Pₐ (Saturation Pressure): Must be > 0 Pa and logically ≥ pₐ (cannot have saturation < actual).
  • ✔️ P (Total Pressure): Must be > max(pₐ, Pₐ) and typically ~101325 Pa (1 atm) for standard air, but allow 10 kPa–500 kPa.
  • ❌ Non-numeric or negative entries are invalid.
  • ⚠️ Very high values (e.g., Pₐ > 50000 Pa) may indicate extreme humidity conditions; verify physically.
  • ⚠️ pₐ ≥ P triggers division by zero or negative humidity ratio — blocked.
  • ⚠️ Saturation >100% triggers condensation warning.

🌍 Key Applications

  • HVAC system design and air quality control
  • Industrial drying processes and moisture-sensitive operations
  • Fire safety: high humidity may affect fire suppression systems
  • Environmental monitoring in laboratories and clean rooms
