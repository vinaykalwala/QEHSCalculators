📘 About This Calculator

This calculator uses the first-order BOD exertion model to estimate biochemical oxygen demand over time.

Assumptions: constant temperature/biological activity, single-stage first-order kinetics, and L expressed in mg/L.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • L: Ultimate BOD (mg/L)
  • k: Reaction/decay rate constant (day⁻¹)
  • t: Time (days)
  • yt: BOD exerted at time t (mg/L)
  • Lt: Remaining BOD at time t (mg/L)

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • All inputs (L, k, t) are required and must be finite numbers.
  • L > 0: Ultimate BOD cannot be zero or negative.
  • k > 0: Decay rate must be positive.
  • t ≥ 0: Time must be non-negative.
  • Contextual warnings: L >>10⁵ mg/L, k >10 day⁻¹ or <1e-6 day⁻¹, t >10⁴ days.

📊 Output Validations

✅ Assessment/feedback is displayed along with the calculated result exactly as it appears in the output:

  •  - % Exerted ≥ 80% ⚠️ High BOD exertion — risk of oxygen depletion.
  •  - % Exerted 30–80% ✅ Moderate BOD exertion — typical progression.
  •  - % Exerted < 30% ℹ️ Low BOD exertion — may indicate fresh influent or slow kinetics.
⚠️ Warnings appear for extreme L, k, or t values, such as L >>100,000 mg/L, k >10 day⁻¹, or t >10⁴ days.

📐 Governing Principle

A Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD) exertion calculator would adhere to one of the standard methodologies for BOD testing, such as ISO 5815-1 (Dilution and seeding method), Standard Methods 5210D, or Indian Standard IS 3025 (Part 44)-1993. The specific standard depends on the geographical region or the guidelines for the test.

📝 Conclusion

This calculator provides yt, Lt, % BOD exerted, and estimated t95 for any valid input. The assessment/feedback helps users identify high, moderate, or low exertion scenarios and detect potential input errors or unrealistic values.
