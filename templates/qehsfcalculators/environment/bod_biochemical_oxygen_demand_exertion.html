📘 About

This calculator uses the first-order BOD exertion model:

Assumptions: constant temperature/biological activity, single-stage first-order kinetics, and L expressed in mg/L.

📘 Applications of BOD Exertion

  • 🌊 Assessing pollution load in municipal and industrial wastewater.
  • 🏭 Designing and evaluating wastewater treatment plants.
  • 🌱 Estimating oxygen demand exerted on natural water bodies.
  • 📉 Predicting dissolved oxygen depletion in rivers, lakes, and streams.
  • ⚖️ Comparing efficiency of different treatment methods and processes.

⚠️ Validations (checked strongly)

  • All inputs are required and must be finite numbers.
  • L > 0. Ultimate BOD cannot be zero or negative.
  • k > 0. A zero decay rate produces zero exertion — we require positive k (realistic kinetics).
  • t ≥ 0 (days).
  • Large/small values trigger warnings: L >>10⁵ mg/L, k >10 day⁻¹ or k <1e-6 day⁻¹, or t >10⁴ days — these usually indicate unit or measurement errors.

🔎 Interpretation

  • % Exerted ≥ 80% → Most BOD has been exerted; risk of oxygen depletion.
  • 30%–80% → Moderate exertion; typical progression.
  • < 30% → Low exertion — either fresh influent or slow kinetics.
