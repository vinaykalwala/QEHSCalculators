📘 About This Calculator

This calculator estimates Plume Rise Height (ΔH) for non-buoyant plumes, where momentum dominates over buoyancy. It is essential for air pollution dispersion, industrial stack design, and environmental safety compliance.

⚠️ Validations

  • ✔️ Ds must be > 0.1 m and ≤ 10 m (typical stack diameters).
  • ✔️ Vs must be ≥ 10 m/s (to ensure formula validity) and ≤ 100 m/s.
  • ✔️ T must be ≥ 250 K and ≤ 1500 K (physical limits for stack gases).
  • ❌ Non-numeric, zero, or negative entries are invalid.
  • ⚠️ Extremely high ΔH (> 500 m) may indicate unrealistic input combination.
  • ⚠️ Very low ΔH (< 1 m) may indicate insufficient momentum; check inputs.

📊 Interpretation

  • ΔH > 100 m → ✅ High plume rise, excellent dispersion
  • 30 m ≤ ΔH ≤ 100 m → ⚠️ Moderate plume rise, acceptable under normal conditions
  • ΔH < 30 m → ❌ Low plume rise, potential ground-level pollutant accumulation

🌍 Key Applications

  • Industrial stack design
  • Air quality modeling and emission studies
  • Environmental compliance and safety audits
  • Urban emission control and ventilation analysis
