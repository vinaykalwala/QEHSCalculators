üìê Governing Principle

The Buoyancy Flux Parameter (F), used in plume rise calculations, is based on methods developed by G. A. Briggs in 1969 and 1975, which have been widely adopted in air quality modeling, including by the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). While not a direct "standard" itself, it is a fundamental concept incorporated into various regulatory dispersion models like the ISCST3 and ISCLT3 models, as documented in the EPA's Guideline on Air Quality Models.

üîö Conclusion

The buoyancy flux (F) is a screening parameter. Use it together with meteorology, stack height, and dispersion models for reliable environmental impact evaluations. The classification guidance provides context but is not a regulatory standard.
