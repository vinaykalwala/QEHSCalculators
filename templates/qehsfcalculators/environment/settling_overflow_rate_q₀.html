📘 About This Calculator

The Settling Overflow Rate (q₀) Calculator determines the surface loading rate for sedimentation basins in water and wastewater treatment systems. This critical design parameter represents the settling velocity of particles that are completely removed and is essential for optimizing sedimentation efficiency and ensuring reliable treatment performance.

⚙ Parameters Used

  • Q (Flow Rate): The volumetric flow rate of water entering the treatment system.
  • Aₛ (Surface Area): The top surface area of the sedimentation basin.

✅ Input Validation Rules

  • Flow Rate (Q): Must be > 0 (typical range: 0.01–100 m³/s or equivalent).
  • Surface Area (Aₛ): Must be > 0 (typical range: 10–10,000 m² or equivalent).
  • Division Prevention: Surface area cannot be zero to avoid calculation errors.
  • Realistic Values: Inputs should reflect practical sedimentation basin dimensions.
  • Unit Consistency: Calculator automatically handles unit conversions.

🔎 Output Validations

  • Overflow Rate (q₀) must be finite: If result is NaN or Infinity → ❌ Invalid result, check inputs.
  • Overflow Rate (q₀) < 20 m³/m²·day: ⚠️ Too Low → Risk of underutilized basin capacity.
  • Overflow Rate (q₀) 20–40 m³/m²·day: ✅ Optimal → Typical range for wastewater treatment plants.
  • Overflow Rate (q₀) 40–60 m³/m²·day: ⚠️ Moderate → Acceptable for high-efficiency systems.
  • Overflow Rate (q₀) > 60 m³/m²·day: ❌ Too High → Particles may not settle effectively.

These same classifications are also shown in the Assessment section of the calculator results so that users get immediate feedback about the operational meaning of their calculated q₀.

📐 Governing Principle

A "Settling Overflow Rate (q₀)" calculator is based on design principles from the field of Environmental Engineering, specifically related to water and wastewater treatment processes, and does not adhere to a single universal standard but uses generally accepted design criteria that can vary by the application or a specific regulatory body.

The values used in calculators are derived from established design practices, often referencing typical ranges for plain or coagulant-aided sedimentation tanks used by various organizations and engineering courses.

What is the "Standard"?
The "standard" is not a specific document or code, but rather a collection of accepted engineering practices and design guidelines for sedimentation tank operation.

Key Factors influencing the Design Criteria:
- Type of Water: Different water sources (e.g., plain water vs. wastewater) require different overflow rates.
- Presence of Coagulants: Using chemical aids like coagulants allows for higher overflow rates.
- Type of Sedimentation Tank: The design of different tanks (rectangular, circular, lamella) influences the appropriate overflow rate.
- Regulatory Requirements: Local or national bodies may specify overflow rate limits that engineers must follow.

Examples of Criteria:
- Plain Sedimentation Tanks: Typical overflow rates range from 12,000 to 18,000 L/d/m² (≈ 500–750 L/hr/m²).
- Coagulant-Aided Sedimentation Tanks: Higher overflow rates are acceptable, such as 24,000 to 30,000 L/d/m² (≈ 1000–1250 L/hr/m²).

How it works:
The calculator uses the formula: SOR (Surface Overflow Rate) = Q / Aₛ, where Q is the discharge and Aₛ is the basin surface area. The design overflow rate (q₀) is then compared with particle settling velocities to determine sedimentation efficiency.
